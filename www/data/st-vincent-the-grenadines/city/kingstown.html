Kingstown - Profile

Location

Kingstown is the capital as well as the main port for St Vincent and The Grenadines. It lies on the south western coast of St Vincent.

It is an attractive busy little town with cobbled streets however there is no marina facility in the town here.

The opening of the St. Vincent Marine Center at the Ottley Hall Shipyard makes this a viable place for getting work done on your boat.

Position 13° 09'03"N, 61° 13'31"W (ferry port)

Clearance

Yachts should anchor at the new Kingstown Cruise Ship and Ferry Berth. First clear in with Customs and then Immigration, which is in town, and finally the Port Authority. This can take some time. Because of the lengthy formalities and difficult mooring in Kingstown.

Last updated March 2016.

Docking

There is no comfortable docking here. A short stop at the ferry port is possible. It is not advisable to leave the boat unattended.

It may be better to clear in at Bequia and visit St Vincent on the ferry, which sails there daily.

A popular cruising boat anchorage is at Young Bay.

Fuel is available here and the provisioning is good.
