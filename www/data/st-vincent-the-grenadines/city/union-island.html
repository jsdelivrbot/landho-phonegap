Union Island - Profile

Location

Union Island is located midway between Grenada and St. Vincent and is equidistant from Barbados, Trinidad and Martinique. It is the largest island in the southern Grenadines and the clear waters of the protected bay at Clifton Harbour is a popular anchorage.

Security

Three sailors returning to their boat late at night were attacked in May 2016 after leaving the Anchorage Yacht Club. Dinghy and outboard theft is increasing here. Be extra vigilant with securing your valuable items at all times.

Position 12° 35'30"N, 61° 24'25"W (entrance to Clifton Bay)

Clearance

Customs and Immigration are based here and clearance can be carried out at Clifton Bay. Immigration is based at the airport and Customs has offices at both the ferry dock and the airport.

Berthing at the Anchorage YC or anchoring on the eastern side of the bay make the walk to the airport for clearing in a short one.

Last updated March 2016.

Docking

There are plenty of anchorages in the bay, as well as stern-to berths at the Anchorage yacht Club, the Bougainvilla dock and at several other small docks. Sail Grenadines may have a few moorings that should be reliable, but it is recommended not to use any others. There is now a great new dinghy dock just outside the elegant L'Aquarium restaurant in the Bougainvilla complex.

Note: The safety department at SVG Air strongly urges yachts to avoid the area within 2 miles of the airport's final approach area in Union Island. Parasailing activities south of the airport can cause difficulties to aircraft landing there.

Last updated May 2015.
