Yemen - Formalities

Clearance

Call on VHF channel 16 on approach. Check in and out with Customs, Immigration and the Harbour Master's office.

Departures at night are prohibited and port control should be contacted on Channel 16 when ready to leave.

Last updated October 2014.

Immigration

No visas are required for sailors on a short visit who do not leave the port area. In this case passports are held until departure and shore passes issued.

Visas are essential for all nationalities if intending to visit other parts of Yemen outside of the port area. Currently, these are difficult to obtain.

Yachts may be able to stop at Hodeidah in an emergency, and also Shi'ir without the crew having visas. But if stopping a Hodeidah, the nature of the emergency should be made clear during the call to Hodeidah port radio.

Last updated October 2014.

Customs

Firearms must be declared to customs.

Prohibited are illegal drugs, obscene books and magazines, and all products of Israeli origin.

Last updated October 2014.

Health

Chloroquine-resistant malaria exists in the low-lying coastal areas so appropriate precautions should be taken, especially between September and February.

Vaccination against typhoid is advised.

Documents

It may be possible to obtain a permit to cruise the south Yemen coast, obtainable from customs and the harbour master. A yacht should not stop in any coastal areas without written permission.

Fees

There are fees for clearance, however currently we only know those for Socotra.

Restrictions

Cameras are not permitted ashore and no photographs should be taken in the port area. Visiting other yachts in the harbour needs permission from Customs.

Local Customs

This is a muslim country and Islamic law must be respected. Sensitivity regarding standards of dress should be observed.  Women should dress conservatively and men must not wear shorts or go shirtless in public. During the month of Ramadan, one should not eat, drink or smoke in public.
