Arica - Profile

Location

This Chile's most northern city and is just 12 miles from the Peruvian border. It is a major commercial seaport.

Position 18° 27'S, 70° 19'35"W (harbour entrance).

Clearance

For full details on clearing into and out of Chile see Chile Formalities.

Contact the port captain on Channel 16 and follow berthing instructions. It's advisable to proceed directly to the Yacht Club and avoid the commercial port.

Check in here is reported to be friendly and the Armada most helpful. A sanitary inspection with the Maritime Health Department is required here, and costs charged seem to vary. Cruisers report being charged between USD 270 and USD 425 for boats clearing into Chile here. This appears to be the only port of entry going south that makes this charge.

Last updated April 2015.

Docking

Contact the Yacht Club on VHF Ch 16 for assistance; they will send the club launch as a guide to the anchorage. The manager, Sr. Abraham, is reported to very accommodating.

Visiting yachts are charged US$20 per day for anchoring with a stern line to one of the Club's mooring buoys. This includes use of the launch for getting ashore. The bottom is rocky and there is a big surge in the anchorage.

Water by jerry cans, no electricity, sporadic hot water shower, bathrooms, fuel at the shore station or by tanker arrangement with Abraham, shopping nearby.

Last updated April 2015.
