Valparaiso - Profile

Location

Port of Valparaiso lies on the coast of central Chile about 100 miles northwest of the country’s capital, Santiago. The port lies on the shores where a coastal mountain range forms a rocky peninsula and a sheltered bay. Built on steep hillsides, Port of Valparaiso is a labyrinth of cobblestone alleys and streets. It is a treasure of cultural and architectural history.

Position 33°2.7' S, 71°37' W.

Clearance

This is a Port of Entry. See Noonsite/Chile/Formalities for information.

Docking

This is Chile's busiest port. There are two mooring facilities in this harbour that welcome visiting sailors, though both anchorages are a little rolly.  If coming from the South the first is Puerto Deportivo Valpariaso (located at the Baron Pier or Muelle Baron in Spanish) and then Yacht Club de Chile (on some charts named Yacht Club Recreo).

Just north of Valpariaso habour are two small and protected bays, also with welcoming yacht clubs. Club de Yates de Higuerillas (about 5 miles north) and the Club de Yates de Quintero (about 15 miles north of Valparaiso).  Access to the splendors of Valpariaso is easy from either of these clubs via bus or train.

Sadly we have just had a report that both these yacht clubs have been destroyed by a storm, earthquake & tsunami.
