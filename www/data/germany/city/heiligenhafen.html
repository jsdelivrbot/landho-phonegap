Heiligenhafen - Profile

Location

This small holiday town is a major yachting centre (with the 2nd largest marina in Germany) as well as a fishing port. It lies about 45NM west of Kiel.

Position 54° 22' 34" N,10° 58' 55" E.

Docking

The approach is well marked, although maybe not very comfortable in strong onshore winds.

Keep clear of the commercial/fishing port on your port side and head for the huge marina.

In the main yacht harbour (see photo courtesy of Alan Wilson), which has box berths throughout, berth in any space on piers 2 to 12 that has a green card showing. Piers 1a, 1b & 1c are reserved for charter boats & sailing schools, although spaces are usually available except on Fridays & Saturdays, when the crews change. There are no specific visitors' berths and space may be hard to find.

The harbourmaster's office is opposite pier 6: payment for berthing is required in advance, not on departure. Good English may be spoken, depending on who is on duty.

Last updated May 2015.

Electricity & water on the berths; wifi is available (2 euros per hour). Toilets & showers opposite piers 1c, 3 and others: showers are by tolken from the machine which takes 1 euro coins (2 tolkens necessary for a decent shower). Washing machine & dryer opposite pier 3; keycard from the marina office. Camping Gaz available (but expensive) from the shop Oleu, between piers 9 & 10. Book exchange but few titles in English.
