Borkum - Profile

Location

Borkun is the western-most of the German Frisian islands and the town has the same name. Not the prettiest of places but a useful deep water approach for Germany.

Position 53° 33.55' N, 06° 45.00' E.

Customs

Docking

This port can be entered in all conditions. When entering from sea, it should be done from the west via Ems. Entering from the northeast is dangerous without local knowledge.

Watch out for commercial vessels and high-speed ferries.

The Yachthaven, "Port Henry", is first harbour to port. Exposed in strong southerlies. The entrance is shallow, so be aware of the depth.

There are also visitors' berths in Burkana Hafen, second harbour to port. Visiting yachts moor alongside large pontoons 1, 2 or 3 in harbour. Deep, with room for large yachts. Uncomfortable in southerlies.

There is a steam railway which takes you into the town.

Port Henry Yachthaven.
First harbour to port. 1.4m draft yachts can enter and leave at all states of tide but avoid fingerberths west in harbour as too shallow. Deeper draft yachts enter and leave HW+/- 2hours. Many yachts will touch bottom at LW in soft mud. Moor at pontoons 1, 2 or 3.
Diesel in small quantities, some chandlery, restaurant and bar.
