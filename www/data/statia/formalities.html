Statia - Formalities

Clearance

Proceed ashore and check in with Customs and Immigration which is based in 3 modified white containers at the head of the main town dock (see the large "Customs" sign). The office is normally open 08:00-16:00, although there is often someone around out of hours.

Clearance is reported to be easy and straightforward with no fees. For a short stay officials will clear you out at the same time.

Now Statia is part of the Netherlands, the eSeaClear computer system is not being used.

Currently Dutch officials are much in evidence and will sometimes board to check papers and equipment.

Last updated December 2014.

Immigration

Passports required for all visitors.

Visa are not required for citizens of most western and northern European countries, Australia, New Zealand, Canada and the USA, but there is a long list of other countries for which they are required. Check Statia website for a complete list.

Although Statia is now a municipality of the Netherlands, it is not part of the European Union nor part of the Schengen Agreement Area.

Last updated December 2014.

Customs

Statia is no longer a duty-free port.

Last updated December 2014.

Health

There are two decompression chambers on Statia

Fees

There are no clearance fees.

Statia Marine Park charges US$10 per day (US$30 per week), which includes the use of a buoy. Pay at the Marine Park office, and be sure to tell them about the poor state of the mooring buoys.

Last updated January 2013.

Restrictions

Diving is only allowed if you are accompanied by someone from the local dive centre.
