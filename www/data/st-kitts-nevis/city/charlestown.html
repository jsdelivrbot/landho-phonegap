Charlestown - Profile

Location

Charlestown lies on the western coast of Nevis at southern end of Pinneys Beach and is the capital of the island.

Position 17° 08'16"N, 62° 37'55"W.

Clearance

Yachts should pick up the yellow quarantine buoy off the ferry dock.

First go to customs, which is upstairs in the 2-story building immediately to the right of the ferry dock entrance(the Cotton Ginnery), open 0800-1200, 1300-1600 weekdays. Then check in with The Port Authority at the dock.

Immigration is in the police station, which is on the right, down Main Street.

A permit no longer seems be required to visit anchorages on Nevis. It is no longer necessary to return to Customs for clearance to sail over to St Kitts.

Last updated April 2015.

Docking

There are now 80+ moorings in Pinneys Bay and anchoring is no longer permitted there. The moorings are reported to be well managed and inspected. There are no dinghy docks in the area as they were destroyed by a tropical storm and landing is very difficult on the steep-to beach.

Pay for your mooring at the Customs Dock right above the town dock.

The Four Seasons hotel has now re-opened and it is possible to anchor close by.

Extension of the Tender Pier in Charlestown Port should be completed by the start of the 2014 winter season. This will be available for yachts and tenders.

Last updated September 2015.
