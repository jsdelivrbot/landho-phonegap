Myanmar (Burma) - Formalities

Clearance

The authorities must be advised of the yacht's ETA and the vessel must proceed without stopping to an official port of entry. Boats coming from the south should keep well away from the Burmese coast after leaving Thai territorial waters and only head inshore when ready to enter Kawthaung (Victoria Point) harbour.

Stopping at any islands is prohibited and should be avoided.

A cruising permit must be obtained in advance: a personal visit must be made to Yangon to pay the fee for a two year permit that can cover three yachts. Alternatively permits can be obtained from yacht agents in Phuket. There are several agents in Phuket that can arrange entry permits. See the list of Noonsite/Myanmar/ClearanceAgents below.

A copy of the yacht(s) registration must be given along with a photo each of the front, side and stern view of the yacht. The department handling yachts in Yangon is MTT and the email is mtt.mht@mptmail.net.mm

Before arrival, email a full crew and passenger list to MTT in Yangon and a photocopy faxed to the agent at the port of entry in Kawthaung.

Also on arrival, you must produce 1 copy of the crew and passenger list, 2 passport size photos and 2 copies of the passport (the page with the photo is sufficient) plus the entry fees (see below).

It is compulsory to take a Burmese guide on board while the vessel is in Burmese waters.

Last updated February 2015.

Immigration

Passports must be vaild for 6 months.

All nationals require visas. The maximum length of stay being 28 days. Visas can be obtained from Myanmar embassies in Bangok, Kuala Lumpur or Singapore.

Visas are valid from three months from the date of issue.

The new e-visa only permits entry to the country through Yangon, Nay Pyi Taw and Mandalay international airports and also requires the visitor to stay in registered accommodation. It is not possible to use one when arriving by sea or staying on a yacht.

It is possible to obtain a Visa on arrival, but only on production of a pre-approval letter, which takes 2 weeks to obtain. Visas on arrival will not allow any crew or passenger to fly to Yangon or Mergui from Kawthaung. These visas are strictly for sailing the islands in the Mergui Archipelago.

Holders of North Korean passports are refused admission.

Note: Visa regulations change frequently.

Last checked January 2015.

Customs

Inspections are thorough and firearms as well as valuables (including cameras and electronic equipment) should be declared.

Any currency must also be declared on arrival and may have to be accounted for on departure.

Visitors may bring in one laptop computer per person and must declare it upon arrival.

Export of antiques, Buddha images and gems without official dealer's receipt, is strictly prohibited.

Last checked February 2015.

Health

Cholera is a serious risk. Malaria, as well as tuberculosis, hepatitis, and other infectious diseases are endemic in most parts of the country.

Medical facilities are inadequate for even routine medical care. There are few trained medical personnel. Avoid intrusive medical treatments, if possible.

Most foreign drugs on sale have been smuggled into the country, and are often counterfeit or adulterated and thus unsafe to use.

Bottled water should always be used.

Documents

A permit must be obtained in advance. The fee for one boat is the same as for three boats and requires a trip to Yangon to organize the permit and make the payment. This covers the three vessels.

Fees

No matter what the "official" fees might be, the total cost, in reality, is likely to be a substantial amount.

The fees below are for visiting sailing yachts. For luxury motor or sailing yachts the fees are substantially higher. Please clear with your agent for these fees.

Cruising permit fee is $2500 excluding the agent's fee.

Yacht crew are "passengers", if they do not have a Seaman book. If they have a Seaman book, they do not have to pay visa fee or any other fees.

To get a better overview, there are per person fees and yacht fees:-
Per person fees:

Visa fee of $ 30/person.

Entry fee up to the Northern tip of Lampi island US$ 120 - valid for up to 5 days.

Entry fee for the Southern Mergui and North of Lampi island US$ 170 - valid for up to 5 days.

After day 5 there is an additional US$ 20 - to be added per person per day.

Yacht fees:

Clearing in fee THB 5000.

Fee for the obligatory Burmese guide ($40/day – which the guide does not receive but it’s called that).
Guide salary about THB 2-3000 per week plus a tip if he is helpful, which most of them are.

Port clearance THB 1000.

Mergui Mooring & lighthouse fee US$ 100
Mergui port clearance fee (for large yachts) US$ 180
(not applicable if not visiting that port)

Amounts are quoted in US$ and Thai Baht

Last updated March 2013.

Restrictions

Internet connections are strictly controlled, very expensive and all emails are read by military intelligence. The government prohibits access to most free international e-mail services.

Mobile phones services are not available outside Kawthaung and they have Thai mobile phone system coverage only around Kawthaung.
