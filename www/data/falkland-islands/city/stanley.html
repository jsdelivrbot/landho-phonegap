Stanley - Profile

Location

Stanley is the capital and principal settlement of the Falkland Islands. It lies on the eastern side of the main island.

Position 51°41′31.56″S,57°51′32.04″W.

Clearance

Both the Customs & Immigration Department and Harbour Authorities require at least 24 hours notice of any vessel's arrival, except in an emergency. This information can be passed by your agent or representative in Stanley.

Stanley is the only Port of Entry to and from the islands.

Any vessel entering or leaving the port MUST report via VHF channel 10 or 16 before crossing the reporting line. The Reporting Line is a line joining the positions 51° 31'S 57° 44'W (Volunteer Point) and 51° 41'S 57° 43'W (Cape Pembroke). This line forms the outer limit of Berkeley Sound and Port William.

Boats can go alongside the Public Jetty if checking in and the cruise ship tenders are not using it, or there are mooring buoys between the Public Jetty and the Government Jetty.

If it is too rough, anchor off and you will receive a visit by launch. The most sheltered spot from all directions except the east is just past the butchery where you can use the beach (covered at high tide) or the slipway to land a dinghy.

Last updated November 2013.

Docking

Anchor in 6-9 metres of water to the West of Navy Point. Vessels should anchor clear of the line of the Leading Lights on the South side of Stanley Harbour.

NO vessels shall anchor to the east of Navy Point without permission from the Harbour Authorities.

There are swinging moorings suitable for yachts and also a possible berth at the new floating pontoon.

Port William
This offers a sheltered anchorage in westerly gales. Avoid restricting the fairway along the centre of the sound and do not anchor close to the line of the Leading Lights through the Narrows into Stanley Harbour.

Last updated March 2012.
