Clearance

This is not recommended as a clearance port, a much easier and straightforward port for clearance is Utila.

To complete clearance in La Ceiba, proceed directly to the shipyard and dock there or anchor in the harbour basin for a few hours.

Avoid berthing in the commercial port as there is a "port handling fee" to pay here, payable by fishing boats, commercial boats and yachts alike, of US$100.

Cruisers report that clearance and an exit zarpe will cost approximately US$60.

Last updated September 2012.

Docking

The Lagoon Marina has been closed since 2010 and its future is uncertain, therefore the only secure docking option is to use La Ceiba Shipyard. Anchoring in the harbour is ok for a few hours while you complete clearance, however should be avoided overnight as there is a strong swell here. As this is a busy commercial harbour with lots of traffic, there is limited space for anchoring.

There is no fuel dock here but a tanker delivery can be arranged for large quantities, or there are gas stations.

Last updated September 2012.
