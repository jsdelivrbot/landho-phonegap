Arrecife - Profile

Location

Arrecife has been the capital of Lanzarote since 1852. The city owes its name to the rock reef ('Arrecife' is Spanish for 'reef') which covers the beach located in the city.

Marina Lanzarote, operated by Puerto Calero marina management, opened here in October 2014, and takes up the majority of space within Puerto Naos (the old fishing harbour).

In the approaches to port attention must be paid to the outlying reefs. Maintain a quarter mile distance off Punta de la Lagarta and round the south wall keeping the buoy to the south.

Full details of the approach to Puerto Naos can be found on the Marina Lanzarote website.

Clearance

If berthing at the marina, the Marina Lanzarote Entry Form and Border Police Form both need to be completed either beforehand or in the marina office and given to the marina staff, who will also request copies of the boat registration document, boat insurance and passport of the owner/skipper.

If staying on anchor in Arrecife, the onlly crew that really need to be concerned about checking in with Immigration and have passports stamped are likely to be non-EU citizens having previously checked out of the EU and now expecting to fly home via the UK. In these circumstances, crew should visit the “Policia de Fronteras” office in Puerto de los Mármoles, open Mon-Fri from 8am – 2pm (approx. 10€ in a taxi or about an hour on foot).

Docking

Puerto Naos, 1 mile north of the town, is the old fishing harbour and shares its entrance with the commercial harbour - Puerto de los Marmoles. For details of approach to the harbour see the marina website linked to above in Location.

Anchoring

Whilst previous anchorages included the Puerto Naos harbour and below the Castillo San José, these are no longer valid options. Many boats still enjoy the anchorage next to the Castillo San Gabriel (now a History Museum) in the original “Puerto de Arrecife”, (rocky) in the southeast corner of the harbour. Take care with the depth (max. 3m). Anchoring here is not recommended when the wind is between S and W, but is very secure in the prevailing NE winds. Dinghy access via the steps on the breakwater, which via the bridge leads straight into the town centre.

Bahia de Arrecife

Marina Lanzarote Berthing

Following the channel into the harbour, the buildings and marina pontoons are clearly visible to port. The fishing dock on the land side of the harbour where the fishing boats tie up to the wall is marked with green cylindrical tower with light sequence Fl.(3)G.9s.9m.1M.

Dredging work is currently underway to clear 5m draught along the length of the entry channel.

Once inside the harbour, the two hoisting slips are visible at most NE point of the marina and the first pontoon after these is the visitors’ arrival berth and fuelling pontoon. The marina office is clearly identified for arranging a berth. Pontoon gates are open during the day and closed at night.

English is widely understood here.

Other options

Visiting yachts may use the old quay in Arrecife for short stays, normally stern-to the southern pontoon. There are also a few moorings here.

Some pontoons have been installed close to the yacht club (just north of the tall hotel tower), but these should only be used at high water or by shallow draft boats.

Marina Lanzarote
Tel:+34 648 524649 ,VHF Channel ch.09 (24/7)
http://www.caleromarinas.com/marinas/lanzarote/en/ , marinas@caleromarinas.com
Opening hours: Every day from 08:00 to 20:00H
The marina has 432 floating finger berths (for boats ranging between 4m and 80m LOA) with over 300 slips available for visiting boats. Full service marina with free WiFi (reported to be good), water and electricity included in daily rate, laundry, toilet & shower blocks, shops and restaurants. Gym and info. centre offering car/bike hire, tourist info. and travel services. 10 minute walk from town centre.
Boatyard services include a considerable two hoists, one with a 100-tonne capacity and another of 820-tonnes for lifting multihulls.

Yacht Clubs

Club Nautico de Arrecife (http://rcna.info/instalaciones-del-club/): Primarily a social club; visiting sailors should call first at the office for permission to use the club facilities. There is no mooring available here.
