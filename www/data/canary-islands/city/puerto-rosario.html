Puerto Rosario - Profile

Location

This port lies on the eastern side of Fuerteventura. The attractive old town with narrow streets and traditional houses is close to the port.

Yachts are not encouraged to berth here, but are expected to use the marina at Caleta de Fuste.

Position 28° 29'23"N, 13° 51'27"W (harbour entrance)

Clearance

Yachts should come alongside on the inside of the southern fishing dock, if there is space, or raft on the outside of one of the larger fishing boats.

On arrival the captain should report to the port office in a grey and white building with wooden doors to the left of the gates leading into the commercial port. Working hours are 0700-1400 Monday to Friday and outside of office hours a watchman is on duty.

Last updated March 2016.

Immigration Office
This office is in the same building as the Port Control office.

Docking

Puerto del Rosario is more suited for commercial shipping or fishing vessels and yachts are better served by the small marina at Caleta de Fustes, a few miles further south.

There is water on the fishing quay and electricity on the pontoons, but otherwise the port has limited facilities for yachts. Short stays may be accommodated inside the south western mole.

The entrance is clear of any dangers. Steer towards the eastern breakwater and once past its end, make for the smaller southern wharf.

It is possible to anchor SW of the cruise ship terminal. The holding is sand and rock.

Last updated March 2016.
