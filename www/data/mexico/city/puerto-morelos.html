Puerto Morelos - Profile

Location

Puerto Morelos is a quiet, laid back type of place where traditional fishermen can make a living. Tourism, especially diving, is just developing but the town remains unspoilt by it.

It lies between Cancun and Playa del Carmen, and is a pleasant stop on the way to Isla Mujeres.

Position 20° 49'35"N, 86° 53'24"W (marina entrance)

Clearance

For full details of clearing into and out of Mexico, go to Mexico Formalities.

An agent is required for clearing in and out, which the marina office can assist with. Current fees are approximately $140.00 to clear in and the same again to clear out. The marina will refund a percentage of the agent fees if you stay with them for a week or more.

Authorities require 24 hour notice for outward clearance.

Last updated December 2015.

Docking

There is a clearly marked channel through the reef to the ferry dock area and the town quay, (which is 1/2 mile north of the dock).

The area off the town, once used for anchoring, is now part of a marine park and no anchoring is allowed. There are no longer any mooring balls available either, so the marina is the only option here for docking.

Anchoring and fishing are prohibited.

The entrance to the marina is 1/2 mile SW of the centre of the town and is well marked.

On the left/port side as you enter the marina are 6 mooring buoys inside the breakwater for vessels up to 45ft or 35 tonnes (approx. $12 per day).

You can leave your dinghy on the dock of the local restaurant Los Pellicanos.

Last updated December 2015.

A full-service, 350-slip marina, part of a resort complex with pool, bars and restaurants. Fuel dock, LPG refills, pump-out facilities, laundry, store, internet access. Boatyard. 2km from the town. Cruisers recommend this marina as "well run, safe, cheap ($1.20 a foot) and a good place to leave the boat while you explore the Mayan ruins of the Yucatan".
