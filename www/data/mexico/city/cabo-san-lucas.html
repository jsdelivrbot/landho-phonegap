Clearance

The port captain's office is on Matamoros Street.

Immigration is situated near the Pemex fuel station. The Immigration fee is to be paid at a bank.

Many cruisers use an agent in Cabo San Lucas, which can be expensive, but many feel is necessary. Officials do not make it easy or convenient to clear into Cabo San Lucas and it can take two days to get all of the approvals.

Obtaining a Temporary Import Permit here, if this is your first Mexican port, is reported to be straightforward.

Last updated October 2010.

Docking

It is possible to anchor off the beach north of the inner harbor, though the anchorage is not very protected and in order the land the dinghy you must travel through the busy narrow entrance into the inner harbor. Panga water taxis are available from the anchorage into the inner harbor. There is a small anchorage fee.

In the past, cruisers have reported that this port is not particularly cruiser-friendly. However in 2015 Marina Cabo San Lucas has a new marina manager who is keen to attract cruising boats.

Last updated December 2015.
