Puerto Escondido Baja - Profile

Location

This spectacular natural harbour is a useful stop on the eastern shore of the Baja Californian Sur. The surrounding islands provide excellent diving and snorkeling.

La Paz, Cabo San Lucas and Escondido suffered extensive damage during Hurricane Odile in September 2014. Read report here.

Position 25°48′38″N, 111°18′28″W.

Clearance

This is not  a Port of Entry.

Last updated September 2014.

Docking

Puerto Escondido Baja is one of two true hurricane anchorages in the Sea of Cortez.

This protected bay is an anchorage and the Port Authorities make a charge for anchoring. The average depth is about 3m.

The inner harbour has a small marina where there may be a visitor's berth available.

In 2013 a small private marina has been opened as part of a new waterside development. It approached through a narrow canal and may have a visitor's berth free.

If offered a mooring, check that it is safe.

Last updated September 2014.
