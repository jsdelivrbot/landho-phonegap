Yasmine Hammamet - Profile

Location

This port is located in the SE of the Northern peninsula of Cap Bon, on the northern edge of the Gulf of Hammamet. It is particularly known for its jasmine, which is how the resort (the first tourist resort in Tunisia) got its name.

This is a good place to stop if heading westwards and fighting N/NW winds. There is a very good boatyard here.

Position 36° 22'09"N, 10° 32'46"E (marina entrance)

Clearance

Customs and Immigration office are in the control tower on the port side of the marina entrance.

Although it has been previously reported that "no gifts" are requested by officials here, a 'gift' might avoid an  extremely thorough search of the boat.

Last updated July 2016.

Docking

Arriving yachts should come alongside the reception dock (max. draft 6m) on the portside of the entrance past the fuel dock (by control tower). The office is open 24 hours.

Last updated July 2016.

A self-contained marina complex with 740 berth next to a large tourist resort.
All facilities, fuel, LPG, laundry, car hire, swimming pool, restaurants.
Good boatyard with 150 ton boat lift, 40 ton crane and range of repair services.
One hour from Tunis international airport. Good security and a good place to leave a boat unattended for long periods. Fees higher than marinas further south. Discounts for longer stays.
