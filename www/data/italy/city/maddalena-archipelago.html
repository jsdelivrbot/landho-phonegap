Maddalena Archipelago - Profile

Location

The group of islands making up the Maddalena archipelago lie off the north east tip of Sardinia, opposite Palau. The southern end of Corsica is just 7 nm to the north.

Only two of the island are inhabited and they are a popular destination for day tourists.

The whole archipelago is a protected National Park.

Position 41°12.5′N, 09°24.5′E (Maddalena Harbour)

Clearance

There are restrictions on landing, anchoring, fishing, diving etc. Some yachts have been heavily fined for breaking the rules.

Pink Beach on Isola Budelli is entirely off limits.

A permit is required to visit the area and Park Rangers do visit to check them. A permit can be obtained online at National Parks Authority website.

The local Tourist Office has maps/charts of the restricted area.

Last updated February 2014.

Docking

Winds and sea states vary emormously around these islands. The heights of the land causing wind funnels, as well as wind shadows.

The area abounds in anchorages, so shelter can be found in most conditions. Within the National Park area there is now a charge for anchoring.

There are mooring at Lavezzi, Cala di U Crecu but is gets very busy there with tourist boats.

Last updated February 2014.

Cala Corsara (Isola Spargi)
An anchorage with good holding and space for 3 or 4 boats.
Cala Spalmatore (Isola Maddalena)
A good sheltered anchorage in all but NE winds, when a heavy swell rolls in. Buses to Maddalena town.
Cala Stagnali (Isola Caprera)
A good, uncrowded anchorage. Nature Reserve headquarters. A good chart is required as leading lines essential.
