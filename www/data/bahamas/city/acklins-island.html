Acklins Island - Profile

Location

One of the least known islands of The Bahamas, Acklins comprises the southern and southeastern part of the chain. The terrain is hilly and desolate, with unusual rock formations, and varied plant and animal life, including an occasional swamp turtle.

Position 22° 43'60"N, 73° 53'14"W (Attwood Harbour)

Docking

The anchorage on the north east coast,  Attwood Harbour, is a small semi-circular inlet. It is open to the north and care is required in the approach.

Elsewhere around the island, is mostly very shallow and only suitable for exploring by dinghy.
