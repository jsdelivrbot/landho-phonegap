Alice Town (North Bimini) - Profile

Location

Alice Town is the principle settlement in North Bimini and a tourist centre. Its main street is full of activities and interesting shops.

Great care need to taken when approaching the harbour entrance (see 'Docking below). A night entry is not recommended.

Position 25° 43'03"N, 79° 18'14"W (approx. harbour entrance)

Clearance

See the main Bahamas Clearance page for information.

Only the skipper should go ashore until clearance has been completed.

The Customs and Immigration are both based at or near the Government dock, which is north of the first marina (Brown's marina).

Docking

A new entrance channel has been dredged into the Alice Town harbour which is nowhere near the old range markers, more like 1/3 mile north. The old range markers still exist - DO NOT follow these.

July 2008: It has been reported that all but the outer two channel markers for the "new" entrance channel are currently missing. Depths are still 11-15 feet and shoaling doesn't seem to be a problem. The entrance channel starts relatively straight in towards the Bimini Sands marina and then curves off to the left (north). Since the inner markers are gone, a night time arrival is not recommended.

Cruisers offer the following waypoints to follow. Care should be taken on approach:
Outside: N 25:42.658, W 079:18.459
Turn: N 25:42.681, W 079:18.261
Inside: N 25:42.830, W 079:18.158

There are several marina along the western side of the harbour.

Anchoring

It is possible to anchor in about 4m, sand over rock, but the holding is poor and swinging room tight. The current in the anchorage is formidable. Berthing in a marina here is recommended.
