Naha / Ginowan (Okinawa Island) - Profile

Location

This large commercial port is located on the west coast at the southern end of the island. The yacht marina lies to the east.

Position 26°16′43″N, 127°43′20″E (Ginowan marina)

Clearance

See Japan Formalities for detailed clearance information.

This port must be visited first to clear into Okinawa.

It is important to call the Coast Guard 24 hours before arrival and fax the required forms to the Coast Guard, Customs and the Naha Port Authority. The Coast Guard will give you the exact GPS location as to where you must wait for officials to board for clearance.

All government officials will want to come on board, so be prepared to fill in a serious amount of paperwork.

In addition, you have to visit the Ministry of Land Office in Naha city to receive permission to enter any closed ports (like Ginowan). It takes 10 - 15 minutes by taxi to get there from the Port. Permission will take at least half a day, so you may have to visit the office twice.

Go to Ginowan Marina to complete clearance. The commercial port has no suitable berths for yachts.

Docking

The marina at Ginowan lies about 6nm north along the coast and  is regarded as a safe typhoon berth. Note the information above, Ginowan is a closed port, so permission to enter must be secured frist from the Ministry of Land office in Naha city.

An alternative, is the smaller marina at Itoman which lies about 10nm south of Naha.

The marina at Kadena (about 12nm north of Naha) is is part of a US Air Base and is only available to US military personnel. There are no guest berths.

Call the Coast Guard on VHF channel 16 to advise them of your entry or exit from the marinas.

To get tax-free diesel, you have to visit the Customs house at Okinawa city (located at the main gate of the US Kadena base).

Yacht Clubs

The American Seaman's Club, near the small boat harbour in Naha is very helpful. They offer temporary membership and can help find workshops for repairs.
