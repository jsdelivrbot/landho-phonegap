Osaka - Profile

Location

Osaka lies at the southern end of the Honshu (the main island of Japan), at the head of Osaka Bay. This bay lies to the east of the Inland Sea. It is a very large city surrounded by several smaller ones.

Location 34° 36.3'N, 135° 21'E.

Dejima (Sakai) Marina
34° 34' 30" N, 135° 27' E
http://www.op-yachts.com; , nozaki-k@op-yachts.com kosuke@op-yachts.com
The location is not inspiring a large highway flyover serves as the backdrop and the pontoons are a bit rickety, but the location is exceptionally well-protected and it may be possible to negotiate a much lower price here than at other marinas. No showers; public bath (sento) close by. Haulout possible: a crane visits every two weeks or so. The marina is also a working boatyard, run by Japanese yachting legend Katsuichi Nozaki and his son Kosuke. Very friendly, helpful people with good English.
