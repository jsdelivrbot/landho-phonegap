Shimoda - Profile

Location

Shimoda is a tourist destination. A modern version of the black ships shuttles tourists to beautiful white sand beaches. The town is famous for its Ryokans (Japanese traditional hotels) and Onsens (Hot Springs).

Although Shimoda appears to offer a protected anchorage it is not a safe place to be on a boat should a typhoon strike.

Position 34° 40'13.03N, 138° 57'07.85E.

Clearance

Docking

There are three berthing options here.

a) Moor alongside at the outer most boat of a chain of boats mooring at Inozawa River. Water is available at a gas station or toilets. The water tap near the river is locked.

b) On the pontoon managed by Shimoda Boat Service down stream in the Inozawa River. This pontoon has water and power with JPY 4,000 fee for a 33ft long boat or JPY 4,500 for 36ft.

c) At the long Pontoon in front of Shimoda Boat Service office in the east area of the port. This one is away from downtown.

The first two options are most convenient for the bath house and restaurants etc.

Cruisers reported that anchoring here "does not work".
