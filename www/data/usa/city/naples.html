Location

The area around Naples, Florida, is famous for high-end shopping, golf courses, fishing, and dolphin-sightings. It is one of the wealthiest cities in the United States with the second highest proportion of millionaires per capita. Real estate is also some of the most expensive in the country.

This high proportion of wealth means that the area is dotted with numerous marinas and yacht clubs. But do not be put-off by the idea that dockage may be equally expensive: at most Naples marinas prices are comparable with elsewhere on the Gulf Coast of Florida.

Last updated September 2016.

Clearance

This is a Port of Entry.

See USA Formalities for full details on clearing into and out of the USA.

All vessels returning from foreign ports to the Naples area are required to first call +1 (800) 432-1216 or +1 (800) 451-0393 and obtain an arrival number before discharging passengers, crew, cargo or garbage. This includes US citizens registered in trusted traveler programs, and must be completed before contacting the Fort Myers office for further clearances if required.
