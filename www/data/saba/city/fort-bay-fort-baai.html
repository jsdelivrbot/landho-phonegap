Location

This port of entry is located at the southwestern side of the island. It is the island's only port and is a busy place.

Clearance

See Saba Formalities for more details.

The Harbour Master is in the government building near the pier. Hours are 0600-1800 Monday to Saturday. You can check in and out at the same time, even if staying a few days.

In bad weather it may be necessary to carry on to the more protected anchoring area on the NW side of the island, between Ladder Bay and Well's Bay, where several moorings (yellow with blue stripe) have been laid for visiting yachts. From here, Fort Bay is best reached by dinghy.

Last updated February 2015.

Docking

The anchorage here is only comfortable in calm weather. There is (usually) one yacht mooring buoy and it may be possible to use the ship's mooring if it is available. Call the Harbour Master.

There is a harbour fee of $20 ( boats up to 40 tons)

There are two other anchorages at Well's Bay and Ladder Bay, where moorings marked yellow with a blue stripe are set aside for visiting yachts.
