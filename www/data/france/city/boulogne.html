Boulogne - Profile

Location

Boulogne is a major port approximately 15nm south west of Calais. It is no longer a ferry port but is still busy with fishing vessels. Inspite of the destruction caused during World War 11, the medieval town walls and castle survived.

Located 50° 44'95"N, 01° 34'11"E (harbour entrance)

Docking

The entrance is possible at all states of the tide and in most weather. Being a fishing port there are a large number of ship movements in and out of the port.

There is a marina on the west side of the river, opposite Quai Gambetta. If staying for a week or more, there is a more peaceful berth is in the locked Bassin Napoleon.

There is an exposed anchorage in the northern part of the outer harbour, Rade Carnot.
