Dives-sur-Mer - Port Guillaume - Profile

Location

The Norman harbour and town of Dives-sur-Mer, is from where William the Conqueror left for England to claim the throne. The medieval wooden market hall and the 13th-14th century church are the main attractions of the town.

The yacht harbour of Port Guillaume was built in the 1990's.

Sheltered by natural dune ribbons, Port Guillaume is tucked away in the estuary of Dives-sur-Mer. The marina has 600 berths for boats up to 15m LOA and 2m draft with 25 visitor berths. Accessible -2H/+2H. Visitor berths are located on pontoons B and V. Electricity, water, showers, laundry, fuel, disposal facilities and WiFi. Boatyard with 30 tonne hoist and 1.5 tonne crane.
