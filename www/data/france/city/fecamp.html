Fecamp - Profile

Location

This ancient fishing port only has a small inshore fishing fleet now and the harbour has been taken over by yachts.

Position 49° 45'55"N, 00° 21'43"E (harbour entrance)

Docking

Although this port can entered by day or night, access is difficult in onshore winds above F5. Do not attempt to enter if it is above F7.

Once inside the harbour, the marina is immediately to sta'bd in the Avant Port. Berthing anywhere on pontoon C. If staying longer than a couple of days, then locking through to Bassin Berigny would offer a more comfortable berth in strong SW'ly winds. The lock operates HW-0230 to HW+0045mins.

Weather forecast on a screen outside the capitainerie, updated every three hours

The entrance is at chart datum. It is dredged every few years to 1.5m. The shallowest spot is just inside the entrance. Anchoring outside is only advisable in the calmest of conditions.

Last updated June 2016.

Port has 600 berths of which 70 are for visitors. Access to the outer (sea) basin is possible at all states of the tide but check minimum draft. Fuel dock,water and electricity on all berths, toilets, showers and launderette, WiFi. 38 ton crane, scrubbing grid.
