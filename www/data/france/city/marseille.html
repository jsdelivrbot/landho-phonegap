Docking

In the Vieux Port, beware of the ferries; they charge in and out at high speed, taking no account of yachts moving about. It is a very sheltered harbour, although rather noisy. The pontoons on the southern side of the harbour run by Cercle Nautique et Touristique du Lacydon (CNTL) are recommended.

The marina at La Pointe Rouge is in the southern outskirts of the city.

There is also a large marina on the Ile du Frioul, 2NM west of the entrance channel to the Vieux Port. A useful map of this port area.

Fuel is available as you leave the Vieux Port on the starboard side.

Place Joseph Vidal , Port of Pointe Rouge , 13008 Marseille, France
Tel:+33 (0)4 91 73 13 21 Fax:+33 (0)4 91 73 33 09 ,VHF Channel 09
Large, sheltered marina with all facilities, fuel. 30 visitor's berth for max. length 15m. A few berths for up to 30m. Boatyard with 2 cranes, 30 ton travel hoist.
Entrance can be difficult in mistral winds.
