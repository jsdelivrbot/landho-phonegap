Brest - Profile

Location

Being a big important navel and commercial port, Brest might appear to not be very yacht-friendly, but this is not the case. Tucked in as it is into the NE corner of the Rade de Brest, it offers all the services required by yachts as well as a large area of sheltered water sailing.

Position 48° 22.5'N, 4° 28.2'W.

Clearance

Report to the Harbour office, although it is quite possible Customs might be waiting on the visitors' pontoon if the Marina du Chateau is your first stop.

Even if you are an EU boat your papers might still be checked here.

Last updated January 2013.

Docking

There is now a second marina in the heart of Brest, Marina du Chateau, It is on the site of the former naval facility and is entered through the large outer harbour. The promenent white-painted spy ship, just inside the outer harbour wall, provides a useful landmark. The entrance into the marina is not easy to identify until well into the outer harbour.

The long established Marina du Moulin Blanc lies 3 miles beyond the commercial ports.

Although further from the city centre, Marina du Moulin Blanc has a more visitor friendly atmosphere and most requirements are either on site or close by. It is also closer to the airport for crew changes.

Yachts must not enter the commercial docks.

Last updated April 2016.

Marina du Chateau
Quai Eric Tabarly, 29200 Brest, , 48°22'52.50N, 4°29'31.94W.
Tel:+ 33(0) 2 98 33 12 50 Fax:+33 (0)2 29 00 44 75
http://www.marinasbrest.fr/ , chateau@marinasbrest.fr.
120 visitors' berths. Pontoons N & M belong to the yacht club and there are further berths reserved for the navy. All facilities, fuel, 25 ton boat hoist, WiFi/internet, waste disposal, recycling, bilge and oil pumps, chandlery, bar and restaurant.
Visitors berth on the long pontoons to sta'bd, one of which runs alongside the wall and the other opposite.
This is a very large, rather impersonal marina and it is a very long hike to the marina office. There is an additional facilities block near the visitors' berths.

Marina du Moulin Blanc
Port de plaisance , BP 91039 29210 Brest cedex 1
Tel:+33 (0)2 98 02 20 02 Fax:+33 (0)2 98 41 67 91 ,VHF Channel 09
http://www.marinasbrest.fr/ , moulinblanc@marinasbrest.fr
Access is via a buoyed channel with a minimum depth of 2.5 metres. Large marina with 120 visitors berths for boats up to 30m LOA and 4m draught. Arriving yachts should moor at the visitors pontoon in the North Basin (Bassin Nord).
All (new) facilities, fuel, repairs, 3.2 tonne crane, 40 tonne boat hoist, LPG, charts, WiFi & internet, launderette, chandlery, bars and restaurants. Bus to city centre (20 mins).
