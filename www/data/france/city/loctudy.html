Loctudy - Profile

Location

This port in NW France is situated on the peninsula of Penmarc'h in the far southwestern part of Lower Brittany. The town still preserves elements of old Breton culture, and the Breton language is still in use.

This sheltered fishing port is well known for its langoustines, called "demoiselles de Loctudy".

The marina here was constructed in 1991.

Position 47° 50'14"N, 04° 10'16"W.

Docking

Take care on approach to keep to the channel. There are large areas of shallow mud banks at low tide.

There is a mooring field east of the marina channel. The buoys are allocated by the marina, so call on channel 09.

A small ferry makes regular trips across to Ile Tudy - a small peninsula well worth visiting.

Last updated May 2016.
