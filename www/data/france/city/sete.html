Sete - Profile

Location

Lying about 80nm west of Marseille, it is just 20 miles from the National Park of the Camargue. It is a very busy port with a huge number of boats of all types coming and going. It has a real buzz of its own, forming as it does, one of the gateways to Europe's inland waterways.

Position 43° 23.3'N, 3° 42'E.

Docking

It is required that yachts enter this port via the western entrance.

A useful plan of the port area.

The main marina (Port St Claire) is right round to port once through the entrance. The Vieux Port is in the canal  opposite Mole Saint-Louis. There is also berthing once through the bridges, in the canal near the train station.

It is possible tie up, free, for a night at the Quai D’Alger if there are no cruise ships in the port. The concession is made for boats supposed to be awaiting the opening of the bridges over the canal leading to the Etang de Thau.

If leaving the boat to over-winter here, be aware of thieves targeting boat electronics and other valuable equipment.

Last updated March 2015.
