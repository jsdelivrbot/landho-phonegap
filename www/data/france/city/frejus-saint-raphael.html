Frejus-Saint Raphael - Profile

Location

Lying approximately 10nm north east of Saint Tropez, this is an interesting and attractive town, and makes a good port of call.

Location 43°25´N, 06°47´E.

Clearance

The Customs building is situated midway between the North and South basins of Santa Lucia Nouveau Port.

Docking

Although further from the centre of the town, the marina at Santa Lucia (north of the Old Port) is the usual port of call for most visitors. When berthing here, the arrivals pontoon is immediately to sta'bord once through the entrance of the Bassin Nord and to port when entering the Bassin Sud. Call port control to be allocated a berth before entering.

The old port of St. Raphael has a few visitors' berths. It has recently been expanded to accommodate mega-yachts so the situation for 'normal' yachts is currently unknown.

The capintainerie is on Quai Albert, St. Raphael, on the eastern side of the harbour.

The harbour at Frejus is about 1nm NW across the bay and is about a 25 minute walk from the town of Frejus itself.

Monaco Radio gives a continuous forecast on Ch 23, in French and then English, for St Raphaël to Menton inshore waters. It also broadcasts on Ch 20 and 4363 kHz USB sea area forecasts for Lion, Provence, Ligure, Elbe, Maddelena, Corse, Sardaigne, Minorque, Baléares, Est Cabrera. The (local) times are 0903 and 1815 (French/English), 1403 (French).
