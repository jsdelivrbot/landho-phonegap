Dingle - Profile

Location

Dingle is the most westerly town in Europe, and sits in the western corner of County Kerry, on the Dingle Peninsular. It is well-known for its seafood, served in the many local restaurants.

In 2006 the name of the town changed to its Irish language name of An Daingean.

Position 52 ° 08’N, 10° 16’W.

Docking

Dingle Harbour is a well sheltered, though shallow, port with a dredged fairway (min depth 2.5m) approach to the fishing harbour and marina now marked by a sectored light visible both day and night. It is also supports a fishing fleet.

For vessels not wishing to enter the marina immediately, there is a safe and comfortable anchorage to be found close to the southern side of the bay at 52°07.51'N 10° 16.55'W, just south of R can buoy Fl.R.5s. (Be aware of pot markers)

For other berthing/mooring options see Valentia
