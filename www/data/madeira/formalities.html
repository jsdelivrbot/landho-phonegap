Clearance

Although Madeira is a member of the European Union, all boats must contact the authorities on arrival and departure, even if only moving to another harbour in Madeira. At Funchal this is a small office on the end of the outer mole. At Qunta do Lorde, clearance can be completed at the marina office.

Last updated March 2015.

Immigration

Madeira comes under Portugal's Schengen Area agreement and therefore has similar visa regulations.

Customs

Firearms must be declared on arrival.

Duty-free goods in excess of the limits allowed are permitted as long as they remain on board.

EU regulations apply concerning temporary importation. See EU VAT page for more information.

Health

A yellow fever vaccination certificate is required if coming from an infected area.

The Public Health services are very good. 24 hours emergencies at Frunchal Hospital.

Documents

A clearance document is issued to all yachts at the port of entry by the Guarda, and must be presented to the authorities when visiting any other island. Even if possessing a transit log from mainland Portugal or the Azores, clearance must still be done on arrival in Madeira. A new transit log may be issued.

The yacht's registration document and passports for all crew are required.

Fees

There are no overtime fees as clearance is done in working hours.

All anchorages can be charged a fee by the Port Authority - APRAM and does not include any support services.

Inside Porto Santo Harbour, the mooring fee for the buoys is charged by the marina. This covers the mooring as well as all onshore facilities: water & electricity (if required by the boat, a berth can be booked for a couple of hours just to refill the tanks and charge electricity), and also wireless internet, laundry, shower and toilets.

Last updated October 2009.

Restrictions

The uninhabited Ilhas Selvagem are administered from Madeira. They are a wildlife reserve, however access is no longer prohibited and it is now possible to obtain a permit to visit on arrival. The guards on site can now issue permits.

This new policy is much simpler than before, however, the Madeira Natural Park still recommends that yacht should ask for the permit prior to visiting the Islands.

Advance permits can be obtained from the Department of Fisheries at Parque Natural da Madeira, Quinta do Bom Succeso, Caminho do Meio, Funchal. Tel. 22 22 66, Fax. 22 25 89, as well as from the Marina offices at Quinta do Lorde and Porto Santo, free of charge.

There is a new subaquatic & terrestrial natural reservation on Porto Santo Island. The Reservation is located on the East corner (north & south) of the island and includes the sunken vessel "Madeirense" (an artificial reef). Diving is allowed but with limits within the natural reservation. Fishing is not allowed.

Last updated October 2010.

Local Customs

Smart appearance is considered indicative of good character, so dressing appropriately when visiting offices is advisable.

Pets

The Pet Travel Scheme (PETS) is in operation in Madeira and requires all pets to have passport, have been microchipped and have current Health Certificate and Rabies Vaccination Certificate issued by appropriate Veterinary authorities
