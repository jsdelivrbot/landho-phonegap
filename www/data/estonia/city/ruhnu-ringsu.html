Ruhnu (Ringsu) - Profile

Location

This isolated island, sitting in the Gulf of Riga is now settled by Estonians, but prior to 1944, its population was ethnic Swedes. There is an interesting wooden church dated 1644.

Position 57°46.8'N, 23°163'E.

Clearance

Clearance is available here.

Last updated December 2015.

Immigration and Customs
VHF Channel 16
Contact by VHF radio.

Docking

The inner harbour has been dredged and pontoons for about 20 yachts are in place. There are also less sheltered berths alongside the quay opposite the ferry berth.

Services are limited, but there is a village store, medical services and a post office. Cycles can be hired and there is a cafe.

No fuel available at the harbour.

Last updated December 2015.

Good shelter. Water, electricity, toilets and showers, laundry, WiFi. Bike hire, cafe.
Channel markers are illuminated during dusk only and entry at night is not advised and it can also be difficult in strong on shore winds.
Depth in the harbour is 2.7m. Max. length is 20m. There are moorings in slips or alongside the quay.
