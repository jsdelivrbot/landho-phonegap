Tallinn - Profile

Location

Tallinn is the biggest port on the Baltic Sea. It sits on the southern shore of the Gulf of Finland, on the north coast of Estonia. Its medieval  'Old Town' is a World Heritage site and attracts many tourists.

Position 59º 27'N,  24º 44'E

Clearance

See Noonsite page countries/Estonia for Clearance details.

Clearance is possible at both the Old City Marina and Port Noblessner, who will summon clearance officials on request.

Last updated January 2016.

Docking

There are two good marinas here, located close to the city centre

The Old City Marina is about 10 minutes' walk from the City's historic "old town". Entry to this marina is through the port, heavy with cruise ship and passenger ferry traffic. Before reaching the outer breakwater of the harbour, yachts should call the Old City Harbour Port Control on VHF Ch: 14 for permission to enter. Small craft may enter the Port Basin and depart the navigation channel only during the green traffic light. See comments about this marina at bottom of page.

One mile west of the Old City marina is Port Noblessner which has the added advantage of a boatyard with shore storage.

Pirita (5km from Tallin Old City) offers 2 marinas for berthing and clearance is possible here.

The other harbours in the Gulf of Tallinn (Rohuneeme harbour, Miiduranna harbour, Vanasadam, Patareisadam, Kalasadam, Lennusadam, Peetri harbour, Miinisadam, Hundipea harbour, Paljassaare harbour, Katariina quay) do not serve pleasure craft.

Last updated January 2016.
