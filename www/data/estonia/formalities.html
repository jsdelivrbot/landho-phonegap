Estonia - Formalities

Clearance

All Estonian harbours are now open to foreign yachts, including the old Russian naval base at Paldiski.

There are no formalities required for yachts arriving from or departing to other countries within the Schengen area and all ports with clearance facilities are "Ports of Entry".

Yachts travelling between Estonia and Russia and from outside the Schengen Area are still subject to the usual formalities but check which Estonian ports have clearance facilities.

See this useful web page on www.marinas.nautilus.ee for more information about visiting Estonia by yacht.

Last updated December 2015.

Customs

The Boarder Guards carry out Customs duties and only need to be contacted if there are goods to declare.

Firearms must be declared.

All valuable equipment on board, which can be removed must be declared.

EU regulations apply concerning temporary importation of yachts. See EU VAT page for more information.

Last updated December 2015.

Restrictions

Holding Tanks is obligatory for Estonian boats. Discharge of sewage is not permitted within 5NM of the coast. Note: Not all harbours have pump out facilities.

Local Customs

When in harbour, crews are expected to be quiet after 23:00.

Pets

PET REGULATIONS - Animals coming under the Pet Travel Scheme (PETS) are allowed in and out of Estonia providing they have a Pet Passport, microchip, Health Certificate and Rabies Vaccination Certificate issued by a recognised Veterinarian and have been blood tested as required.
