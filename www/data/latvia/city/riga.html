Riga - Profile

Location

Riga is a major city with a large Russian speaking population. It has a car-free medieval city centre, with lots of well-kept buildings from different periods. There are cathedrals and museums, outdoor tavernas, music cafes and restaurants.

Clearance

The last basin before the bridge, by the ferry terminal, is where Customs and Immigration have their offices. Yachts may go directly to the Andrejosta Yacht Centre (the former Latvian Shipping Company Yacht Club). Officials will visit the boat and complete formalities.

Docking

The approach to Riga is via the river Daugava. The river is wide and the stream weak, so it is possible to sail the 8 miles up to the centre, but it is a busy commercial waterway. Watch out for unlit moorings.

Call traffic control (Riga Radio) on VHF channel 16 and 09.

There are several yacht clubs in the city, but some of them are situated far from the centre. The most convenient, is the "Andrej Osta", on east side of the river, behind the ferry terminal and just down stream of the bridge.

When cruising in the Bay of Riga, unmarked fishing nets are a constant hazard.

This is a private marina, and at the moment, the main guest yacht harbour in the heart of the city. It is located within 15 minutes walking distance of the Old Town (Vecriga), on the right bank of the river Daugava 7 miles from the sea. It is an all weather, secure yacht harbour with 50 visitors' berths available. It provides all the necessary services for yachtsmen, including passport clearance and customs. Well-equipped showers, sauna and two swimming pools, bar and restaurant. This safe and quiet harbour with its friendly staff will ensure a pleasant stay.
