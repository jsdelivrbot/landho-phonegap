Clearance

If entering from outside the EU or are a non-EU yacht then Clearance formalities need to be completed. Riga has adequate facilities for yachts but Roja is a more convenient stop and has good facilities.

Last updated June 2015.

Immigration

Latvia is a member of the Schengen Agreement Area. See Noonsite's Schengen page for more details on the immigration rules.

For a list of countries whose citizens may also enter Latvia without a visa for stays up to 90 days, see www.mfa.gov.lv/en

For up-to-date visa information and also addresses of Lativa consulates and embassies, look at Latvia government website

You are required to have health insurance when you enter Latvia. This must include repatriation costs.

Last updated June 2015.

Customs

Guns, narcotics, pornography, etc. are strictly banned. There are no restrictions on the amount of hard currency you can bring in or take out.

Objects dating before 1945 are subject to tax and require an export permit from the Ministry of Culture (C-3) on Pils 22; tel. 721-4100, fax 722-7916; open:08:30-17; Sat., Sun. closed. Make sure you take along the receipt to prove it was paid for and not stolen.

Customs rules appear to change frequently, with officials sometimes interpreting them as they see fit. Customs info is available from the Customs Department, (G-1) K. Valdemara 1a, tel. 732-3858, fax 732-2440.

EU regulations apply concerning temporary importation of yachts. See EU VAT page for more information.

Last updated June 2015.

Health

If you are struck down by a major illness while in Latvia, it is probably best to fly to Western Europe if you can. Many doctors are under trained and most lack up-to-date equipment. Diagnostics is especially uncertain. Emergency services can sometimes be slow.

There is an increasing number of private facilities with Western-trained doctors.

Documents

A certificate of competence may be requested.

Restrictions

A listening watch must be kept of VHF channel 16 while in Latvian waters.

Anchoring is not permitted in the navigation channels and rivers approaching the ports.

Drinking alcohol in public is prohibited and may lead to detention and a financial penalty.

Pets

PET REGULATIONS - Pets require Passport and must have been vaccinated against rabies at least 30 days prior to travel. Latvia requires the bi-lingual EU Form 998 Veterinary Certificate. www.pettravelstore.com/store-pet-immigration-forms1.html.
