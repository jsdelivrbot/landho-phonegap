Isla de San Andres - Profile

Location

This is a larger sister island to Providencia and much busier being a major holiday destination for mainland Colombians.

Position 12° 34' 84"N, 81° 41'93" W (marina)

Clearance

Full details on the formalities required for clearing into and out of Colombia can be found at Colombia Formalities.

For a list of agents see Clearance Agents.

Remember, an agent is required to clear into and out of Colombia, so no attempt at contacting the Port Captain should be made, except through your nominated clearing agent (Agencia Maritima) on arrival.

Clearance formalities must be completed even if coming from mainland Colombia. Visas are issued on arrival for 30 to 90 days depending on nationality.

Customs and Immigration will come to the marina.

The Coastguard patrol the harbour on a regular basis.

Useful website: www.sanandres.com

Spare parts can be imported without tax if using "yacht in transit" feature.

Docking

There is quite good protection in the anchorage here, however if winds reach 20+ knots from the East or SE it will get a bit bumpy.The anchorage is north of a small island of Cotton Cay.  Most of this anchorage is filled up with fishing boats on moorings, but there is space to anchor to at the east end of the anchorage, just off Nene's Marina. The anchorage can get very busy in the afternoons with jet skis and taxi boats - Providencia is quieter.

An alternative anchorage is north-east of Cotton Cay, just off the San Andres Yacht Club (12°34.67N  81°41.55W). To reach it you must go round to the south of Cotton Cay.

There are not many places to leave your dinghy because most of the shore line appears to be private and is fenced off.  You can leave your dinghy at Nene's Marina or the Yacht Club for weekly fee per person (non-negotiable).

Provisioning is limited here. Fuel is available from Nene's Marina or do a jerry jug run from the fuel dock.
