Volos - Profile

Location

Volos is one of the largest commercial ports in Greece, and is, therefore, very busy with cargo boats. The city is bustling and varied with all the resources a yacht could wish for in provisions and repairs. The harbour front is traffic-free and the evening "passagario" is very popular with the locals and visitors alike. Fish can be bought directly from a number of the local boats in the mornings.

Position 39°21.1'N, 22°56.19'E.

Clearance

For full details on clearing into our out of Greece see Greek Formalities.

This is a port of entry. All the authorities are based here as it is a major commercial and ferry port. the Port Police do not visit visiting yachts as they are busy with the comercial ships and ferries, so you are obliged to go by yourself to their office.

Last updated July 2015.

Docking

Yachts can moor where indicated on the map by the green coloured lines (our thanks to SY Funny Girl for the map). There are alot of local boats and charter boats everywhere in this harbour, however normally a space can be found.

The Southern breakwater has laid lines and is often the only place to find a space for visiting yachts, however this is a poplular place for local youths to congregate so can get noisy and security is questionable. In addition, how often the laid lines are checked is dubious, so you may be better off using your own anchor. Water is available here.

The eastern town quay can be used however this part of the harbour is subject to swell from the ferries. Water and electricity are available.

Anchor stern-to. Holding is good, although some parts of the harbour have rather soft mud.

The "yacht harbour" in the north is normally full of fishing boats. If you do find a space here, it is still close to the town and restaurants and there is no swell at all. Water and electricity are available.

The closest roadside petrol station for fuel is marked red on the map. There is also drinking water available here.

An Irish/Italian called Carlos acts as an unofficial "Harbourmaster" and will direct you to a berth. He has excellent local knowledge and is very helpful.

The Yellow Pin on the map in the SW corner of the bay marks the Pafkakia boatyard. There is also a concrete wall/quay here where a yacht can moor.
