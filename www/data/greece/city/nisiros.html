Location

This square island lies just south of Kos. It is the remains of an extinct volcano and its rich soil supports many olive and citrus groves. Palon, in particular, is an attractive spot to spend a day or two.

Docking

This island has two small harbours, Mandraki and Palon, both on the NW side of the island.

Mandraki

The harbour here lies a short distance from the village itself and is an attractive place to stop in calm weather. There are several sights of interest including the Castle of the Knights of St. John overlooking the harbour, a monastery and the Paleokastro - a very well preserved acropolis from the Classical period.

This harbour is busy with fishing and day-tripper boats, so berth on either the quay or at the root of the mole where you can find space. Beware of mooring near the ferry as it creates a lot of wash as it uses its engines to hold steady against the shore lines while loading. This harbour is untenable in strong N or NE winds.

There is a daily ferry to Kos from here.

Palon

Palon (Palos) is located 2 miles to the east of Mandraki harbour. It's a popular place with yachts as it's very picturesque, sheltered and a good base from which to explore the island. (Photo courtesy of Con Sprenger, SY Big Sky, who visited May 2016.)

The harbour here is continually dredged to approx 3.5 mtrs (although the entrance is a little shallower here and there - stay close to the breakwater on entry for more depth). The entrance has also been turned around so that yachts must enter from the east, not the north. This makes the harbour much better in northerly winds and offers good shelter in a "meltemi".

As a result of the dredging it is possible to moor safely on either the north side or the south side of the harbour, stern or bows-to. The bottom is mud, sand and rocks, so good holding.

Both sides are now equipped with water and electricity (possible daily charge of 5 Euros a day) and most tavernas offer showers. There is also fire fighting equipment. The moles have been concreted and the harbour feels a little more like a marina now. The locals are usually keen to help you in as you may want to eat in their taverna afterwards, although there is no obligation.

There is scooter hire right next to the quay for exploring the island and fuel can be delivered by mini-tanker.
