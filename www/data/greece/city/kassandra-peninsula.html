Kassandra Peninsula - Profile

Location

A long, narrow peninsula on the eastern side of the Gulf of Thermai, the Kassandra Peninsula is the most western of the 3 "fingers" of the much larger Khalkidhiki Peninsula to the north. (The others being the Sinthonia and Akti Peninsulas).

It is connected to the mainland by a sliver of land just half a mile wide. This is bisected by a waterway, the Portas Canal.

Docking

On the western side of the peninsula there is a new C&N Marina in Sani, developed as part of an exclusive spa resort, and the fishing port of Nea Skioni. The eastern coast has several good anchorages and possible jetty berthing at Nea Fokaia, approx. 8 miles SE of the Portas Canal.

Portas Canal

This shallow canal is 1/2 mile long, min. 36m wide, and is spanned by a bridge with 17m clearance. It is dredged to 2.5m but beware of silting along the sides and at the western end. At the eastern end of the canal it is possible to anchor. There is a fuel station nearby, provisions and tavernas.

Latitude 40°05.846N Longitude 23°18.374 E (6 miles south of the Portas Canal). Part of the 5 star Sani resort. Good shelter. Entrance channel dredged to 3m (however call ahead to confirm depth). All facilities, laundry, fuel berth, mini-market, bars and restaurants, ATM, taxis. Beautiful beaches adjacent.
Nea Fokaia
Latitude 40°08.01N Longitude 23°24.02E. The village here has a short jetty protected by a detached breakwater. Berthing for visiting yachts alongside the northern side of the jetty. Open to winds from the north. Water, provisions and tavernas.
Nea Skioni
Latitude 39°56.81N Longitude 23°31.59E. A fishing harbour on the SW coast of the peninsula, between Ak Posidhion and Ak Paliouri. Reasonable shelter. Moor where possible, recommended west or south quays due to unreliable depths in the east. May have to moor alongside a fishing boat. Water on the quay and fuel in the town, also provisions.
