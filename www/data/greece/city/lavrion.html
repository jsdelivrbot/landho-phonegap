Lavrion - Profile

Location

Lavrion is the only real port on the southern tip of mainland Greece. It lies on the eastern coast, about 10NM from Sounion. It is a large commercial and fishing harbour.

Having been cleaned up for the 2004 Olympics to host a number of cruise ships, it now presents a much more attractive port of call.

Position 37°41.9'N, 24°03.3'E.

Clearance

For full details on clearing into our out of Greece see Greek Formalities.

This is a port of entry and all the authorities are based here. The Harbour Authority Office is at the far end of the harbour in a cream coloured building.

A non-EU yacht reported being charged an obscure tax here on departure - see comments at bottom of this report.

Docking

Within the harbour, adjacent to the small fishing harbour, there is a section reserved for yachts on the town quay (on the outside of the "L" shaped mole). The Harbour Authority have also installed a new pontoon off the end of the mole, which has water and electricity (photo courtesy of SY Funny Girl).

Several charter companies operate from here so most of the berths are used by these companies. Midweek however, you may be lucky and get a berth. Call the Harbour Authority on arrival and they will allocate you a berth. There are fixed laid lines here and you should not anchor.

Be sure to pay the Harbour Authorities only for your berth. Other people, including the charter companies, may ask you to pay them, but the Harbour Authorities are the only office you should visit with payment.

The town quay is subject to swell in strong southerlies.

A better alternative is the marina just south of the town. The Olympic Marina is large and has an extensive range of repair facilities. At one time the Carter 33's and 37's were built here. Shelter is good in the marina.

The marina and adjacent boatyard offer a full range of services. Repair services, especially fibreglass work, are of good standard as the yard used to build a range of sailing yachts. There is a large area for hard standing and the yard is a popular place for winter storage ashore. The marina is very busy in summer and advance bookings are strongly advised. Similarly, a place should be booked in advance for winter storage. Three travelifts of 40, 65 and 200 tons, fuel berth. Bar, restaurant, café, mini-market, chandlery, laundry, first aid post, ATM, , chandlery, free WiFi, 24hr security.
The Port Police and Customs are situated within the marina.
