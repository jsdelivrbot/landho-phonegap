Location

This island is the most westerly of the Southern Cycladese group. It is popular with young greek tourists but still manages to retain its tranquility. It is the home of the original statue of the Venus de Milo.

Clearance

Port Police, Customs and a Harbourmaster are based here.

Docking

The port of Adhamas is in the NE corner of the deep bay, Ormos Miliou, on the northern coast of the island.

Moor on the north quay or to the west side of the pier on the eastern side of the harbour. There are about 50 berths in total here. Good shelter even in a meltemi, but it is advisable to moor several metres out due to the chaos caused by the twice daily arrival of the ferry.

In a strong southerly wind, one of the anchorages close in on the western side of the bay would be better.

The two off-lying islands of Kimolos and Poliagos provide several good anchorages.
