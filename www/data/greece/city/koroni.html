Location

Known as Corone by the Venetians and Ottomans, the town of Koroni sits on the southwest peninsula of the Peloponnese on the Gulf of Messinia in southern Greece.

The impressive Venetian castle houses part of this attractive town which livens up at night.

Works to the breakwater here are on-going.

Docking

The town quay in general is too shallow for docking (photo courtesy of SY DeDanann who visited here in 2016) however there may be space right at the end of the NE breakwater.

Failing that, yachts must anchor off the beach where holding is good (in mud). Take care close to the breakwater where there are many stray boulders.

There is good shelter here from the prevailing wind.

In settled weather a picturesque spot to drop the hook is just below the castle walls on the eastern side of the harbour breakwater. Note that improvement works to the breakwater are on-going.

Currently there is no water or fuel available to cruisers.
