Siros - Profile

Location

Siros is a fairly rugged, barren island in the Northern Cyclades. The north is bare and largely uninhabited, while the south is more populated with gentle cultivated slopes.

The Port Siros (Ermoupolis) is midway down the eastern coast and is a busy, interesting town. There are a few boatyards here where yachts can haulout.

The western coast offers several small ports/anchorages, but only Finikas has anything more than tavernas and some basic provisions (it also has water).

Position 37°31'N, 24°53'E.

Docking

Port Siros (Ermoupolis)

This is a bustling harbour and town and a lively alternative to its quieter neighbouring islands. Moor on the north east side of the harbour using your own anchor. The ferries create quite a bit of wash here so take care. When approaching, keep to the east to avoid the commercial activity close to the entrance. The bottom is mud and the holding, good.

Excellent shelter from northerly winds, but southerlies can cause an unpleasant swell, and strong winds from this direction can make the quay a dangerous place for yachts. It can also get quite smelly here in the summer.

Water, electricity and fuel are available on the quay.

The marina, in the south of the bay, offers good protection but is some way from the town.

Finikas

The harbour here is located in the NW corner of the bay and is a more peaceful harbour than Ermoupolis with good shelter. You may find space inside the harbour, however the majority of yachts tend to moor bow/stern-to off the south side of the outer mole, where there is good protection from the prevailing wind. Be sure however to pull your boat a good distance back from the mole, as the wash from passing ferries can cause a surge.

It is also possible to anchor off the beach outside the marked swimming area in sand and weed with good holding. Watch out for laid moorings.

There is water on the quay and a simple shower and toilet block. Finikas has some small chandleries and yacht service companies (see harbour noticeboard for details).

It can get crowded in Finikas at the weekends as it is a charter base.
