Location

Patras is one of the largest cities in Greece and its harbour is a lively, noisy place. It is situated in the northwest Peloponnesus on the Gulf of Patras, an inlet of the Ionian Sea. This gulf connects the Gulf of Corinth with the Ionian Sea.

Clearance

For full details on clearing into our out of Greece see Greek Formalities.

The authorities needed for clearing in/out are all close to the quay.

Docking

Visitors should make for the yacht harbour, north of the harbour, where the the old caique basin has been converted into a marina. Berth where directed but beware of the shallows close to the quay.

The visitors' pontoon is subject to some swell. The minimum stay is 2 nights with discounts for longer stays. Water, electricity and shower facilities are included in the charge.

Alternatively, if may be possible to moor stern-to or alongside on the northern side of the central mole (opposite the castle). Shelter is good but the wash from the constant movement of tugs and other workboats does cause a lot of surge. The holding is good.
