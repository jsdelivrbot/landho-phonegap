Immigration

All visitors need a passport valid for at least 6 months beyond their intended stay.

No visas are required for a stay up to three months for nationals of the ECWA (Economic Community of West African States) i.e. Cape Verde, Central African Rep, Chad, Congo, Gambia, Ghana, Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Liberia, Mali, Mauritania, Morocco, Niger, Nigeria, Senegal, Seychelles, Sierra Leone, Togo & Tunisia.

All other nationals require a visa.

There are Ivorian representatives in Paris, Dakar, Rabat, Algiers and Tunis.

Last updated November 2015.

Health

November 2015: The outbreak of the Ebola virus disease (EVD)  in West Africa is now under control, but it is possible that there will continue to be more extensive health checks than previously.

Find out more at Ebola World Health Organization Fact Sheet.

Vaccination against yellow fever is compulsory; cholera vaccinations and malaria prophylaxis are recommended.

Malaria is endemic

Restrictions

Photographing military or government installations is forbidden.

Local Customs

Dress is conservative for men and women. Shorts, tight clothing and strapless tops are frowned upon anywhere except on the beach.

Religions have a strong influence on life in Côte d’Ivoire. You should respect local religious customs and traditions at all times and be aware of your actions to ensure that they do not cause offence. (U.K. FO advice).
