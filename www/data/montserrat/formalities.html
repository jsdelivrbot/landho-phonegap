Montserrat - Formalities

Clearance

SailClear

The SailClear advance web notification service for clearance is now operational in Montserrat. All duly appointed officers stationed at Little Bay port have been fully trained on the SailClear system, and any new officers will be trained on the system.

You can complete all the SailClear online forms in advance, or there is a computer terminal at Customs in Little Bay.

In the near future visiting vessels will be able to connect to a WiFi system with reach of approximately ¼ mile offshore.  This will allow access to the SailClear system before landing.

On Arrival

Little Bay is now the island's only port of entry.

Before arriving, it is recommended that you radio the Port Authority on VHF Ch.16 to check on conditions here. VHF Ch:16 is monitored 24/7 by the Port Authority.

On arrival anchor in the southern part of the bay, keeping clear of the channel to the ferry/cargo wharf. In future this channel will be clearly marked.

No crew or passengers must land before clearance is granted.

Inward Clearance

Check in at the Customs and Immigration office which is in the Port office. As you come off the jetty you will see a sign over a door at the warehouse.

Customs and Immigration are open 08:00-16:00, but not at weekends or public holidays. Any vessel arriving outside of these hours can either wait aboard at anchor until C&I reopens, or request an ‘out of hours’ check in. If you arrive outside of working hours there is an overtime charge.

Next check in with the Port Authority who are also in the warehouse. Clearance from the previous port must be submitted to the Port Authority.

All fees are paid to the Port Authority (see Fees below).

To avoid being stopped at the port gates, check in with Port Security.

Cruising Montserrat

If a visiting vessel intends to stay a few days in Montserrat, they can request and obtain a Coastal Cruising authorization from Customs and Immigration, which gives them permission to land at any authorized beach outside of the official Exclusion Zone for a period of up to 7 days.  There is no cost for this permit.

Even if you intend to dinghy ashore to the beach in Little Bay you will require this authorization.

Outward Clearance

Yachts can obtain clearance in and out at the same time if staying 72 hours or less. If staying longer, there is no additional charge, but you will have to return to Customs to do the paperwork again. Only Customs needs to be visited for departure. There is no departure tax.

Last updated December 2015.

Immigration

Passports are required and be valid for at least the period of your stay.

Visa rules apply (as described below) if staying ashore.

No visas are required for nationals of the European Union, Commonwealth countries, Japan, Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, San Marino, Sweden, Switzerland, Tunisia, and US citizens.

All other nationals require visas although if the stay does not exceed 14 days the visa exemption may apply for most countries (except East European and communist countries). Visas can be obtained from British consulates.

Stays of up to six months are permitted for Canadian, UK and US citizens.

Last updated May 2015.

Customs

Firearms must be kept sealed on board.

Yachts can stay indefinitely in Montserrat without paying duty. Residents of Montserrat can import private boats duty free.

The Customs declaration form can be downloaded from here

There are Customs regulations on the importation and exportation of agricultural products and the protection of marine and animal life.

There are a number of marine and animal specimens that may not be taken from the islands. If in doubt you should check with the local customs authorities prior to the purchase, importation or exportation of such items.

Last updated May 2015.

Health

Basic medical needs can be met in the Glendon Hospital in Lookout which is about an 8 minute drive from Little Bay.

Documents

A coastwise clearance permit is required is wishing to land anywhere, including Little Bay.

This permit can be requested from Customs on arrival and it will be granted for a certain period. If wishing to stay longer, apply for an extension.

Clearance will depend on volcanic conditions prevailing at the time.

Also see Restrictions for the details.

Last updated May 2015.

Fees

Customs fees are based on tonnage:

15 ton boat:$35EC

Over 20 tons: $100EC

All fees must be paid in cash only (EC$).  No credit or debit card payments accepted.

If you arrive outside of working hours (or on a Public Holiday) there is an overtime charge. This is EC$50/hour with a minimum of 2 hours charge, and based on a maximum of 8 passengers per vessel.

Last updated December 2015.

Restrictions

To enter controlled access areas, you will require a police licence from the Royal Montserrat Police Force. Applications should be made at the Salem Police Station and should contain the reasons why access is being requested.

There are two Maritime Exclusion Zones. Maps showing these areas are available from the Montserrat Volcano Observatory (www.mvo.ms) or the Montserrat Port Authority. Maritime vessels should not enter the Exclusion Zone.

Last updated December 2013.

Pets

Pet Regulations Animals must stay on board until they have been checked by the veterinary officer. Pets must have Veterinary Health Certificate and inoculation records and will be quarantined between three and six months.
