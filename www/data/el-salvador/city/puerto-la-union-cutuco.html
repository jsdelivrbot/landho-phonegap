Puerto La Unión (Cutuco) - Profile

Location

The town surrounding the port is also called La Unión, all within the Gulf of Fonseca waters.

Few yachts visit La Union, as it and nearby Meanguera are reportedly quite dangerous places. Theft is common in La Union, even under the nose of the navy, and we have received reports of boats at anchor having deck gear stolen at night.

The situation has improved recently and it is now regarded as safe to walk around during the day. The development of a new, but small, marina facility 14nm south of La Union has also improved berthing options here.

Clearance

The port lies 2 miles from the town of La Union.

The Autoridad Maritima Portuaria handle all the clearance procedures for yachts. The AMP representative in La Union city must be contacted by phone to receive an arriving vessel in La Unión. See details below.

Anchor near the navy dock and the port captain will come out on a small boat. The officers are reported to be very professional and friendly. They will advise you to only come ashore through their base for safety.

A departure certificate (zarpe) will be issued by the port captain and this must be shown at subsequent ports.

Last updated December 2014.

Docking

New in 2014, Marina Velazul offers easy access to La Union city as it's just a 20 minute drive to downtown shopping with multiple ATM's, banks, 2 high-end supermarkets ("Super Selectos"), all sorts of medical services, veterinary services, hardware stores and logistical and communication support.

The marina itself has a small snack bar with toilets and showers. A laundry service is offered locally. There are small rustic restaurants, neighborhood food markets, bars, a pool-hall, and liquor stores within walking distance of the marina.

The naval base in La Unión city, has a "syncro-lift", and can haul out most cruising sail boats and motor yachts. Marina Velazul can help coordinate this service. Bottom paint and repairs can also be carried out with local sub-contractors.

Last updated December 2014.
