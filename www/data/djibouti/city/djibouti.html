Djibouti - Profile

Location

This large commercial port is a crossroads for sea base commerce. However, political unrest in in this area and the activity of pirates around the Horn of Africa, do not make this an advisable port of call.

Position 11°35'57" N, 043°07'56" E (anchorage)

Clearance

Upon radioing to enter the port of Djibouti you will be met and escorted in by a coastguard launch and a heavily armed RIB of a French naval vessel.

Formalities are reported to be straightforward.

See Noonsite/Djibouti/Formalities page for the details.

Last updated April 2016.

Docking

The Yacht Club of Djibouti has now been closed. If there is space available, visiting yachts are docked on the floating pontoons in front of the former yacht club building.

It is also possible to anchor inside the Port (see photo courtesy of SY Alytes who visited in 2016). Good holding in mud at about 4.5m (N 11°35,953  043°07,908).

On request, dinghies can be safely left at the red and white Coast Guard dock on the north-western end of the fishing harbour. If you leave your dinghy in the fishing harbour itself, it is recommended to hire a guard for 100 to 200 francs per day.

Obtaining fuel here is not easy. The main wharf has a high volume of traffic bringing in food supplies and refugees so it is likely access to the wharf for fuelling will be denied. There is a petrol station nearby and jerry cans can be trucked to your dinghy.

Last updated April 2016.
