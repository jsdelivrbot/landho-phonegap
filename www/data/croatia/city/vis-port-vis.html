Vis Port (Vis) - Profile

Location

The island of Vis is the most westerly of the islands belonging to Croatia. It is a quieter and less developed than most due to it being off limits to foreigners until 1989.

The port of Vis is at the head of the bay, Viska Luka and lies on the north eastern part of the island. It is the main ferry port.

Position 43°03.6'N, 16°11.8'E (port).

Clearance

Port of Entry in summer only.

In winter, Split must be used.

The customs quay is marked by a flag and the police office is just across the road. The Harbour Master's office is opposite the ferry pier, next to the tourist office.

Last updated August 2011.

Docking

This is a port popular with Italian yachts, so an early arrival is advised to obtain a berth.

Berth stern-to on the SW side of the harbour. There is water and electricity on the quay. There are several bars and restaurants here.

If a quieter berth is preferred, moor to the quay at the village of Kut where shelter is better in a south westerly.

There are several anchorages in the outer bay but ensure your anchor penetrates the weed. Anchoring is not permitted in the inner bay.

Last updated March 2014.
