Komiza (Vis) - Profile

Location

This attractive port is in the large bay at the western end of the island of Viz.

Position 43°04'N, 16°14.9'E.

Clearance

This is a summer Port of Entry.

Docking

Moor to the breakwater on the SE side of the bay. The berths at the outer end have a depth of 5 or 6m which decreases to 2m at the SW end.

There are now mooring balls just outside of the inner harbour.  These are strong, well spaced moorings anchored to 5-ton blocks.  The charge is 150Kn for boats 10m or less and 200Kn for boats 10-15m.  The price is half the cost of mooring on the inner mole.

Anchor in the bay, to the south of the stone bollards. There is good holding in the mud.

Shelter can be found in some part of the bay in alnost all winds, although the entrance can be difficult in strong W and SW winds.

Last updated May 2013.
