Dubrovnik - Profile

Location

This medieval walled town is a major tourist attraction which, after being severely damaged in the early 1990's, has now been restored to its former glory.

Position 42°38'25"N,18°06'52"E (harbour entrance)

Clearance

Gruz (Dubrovnik Harbour) is a year round port of entry.

Boats are directed into Gruz harbour to complete formalities. Berth at the Customs quay near the light, avoiding the ferry terminal.

The Harbour Master is across the dock from the ferry berth. Customs are in the large building on the ferry quay, opposite the Hotel Petka.

The cruising permit has to be paid for in cash. There is a cash machine in the Customs building.

Last updated February 2014.

Docking

Yachts are not allowed into the small harbour in the old walled city. Gruz Harbour, which is a commercial port, or Dubrovnik marina, in the Rijeka Dubrovacka, are the options available to visiting yachts.

Gruz - Moor stern to and use your own anchor. Subject to surge from passing vessels.

Dubrovnik Marina - Call the marina in advance (VHF channel 17), or wait at the entrance to be allocated a berth. Good shelter.

Anchoring
Once formalities are completed in Gruz, a nice place to anchor is outside the walled city, just a short hop around the bay from Gruz. Sometimes boats are asked for an anchoring fee here (4 euro/metre), however others have reported not having to pay anything.

Note: It is not permitted to land on Lokrum Island before 09:15. Nor is it permitted to fasten mooring warps to the island.

Last updated August 2016.
