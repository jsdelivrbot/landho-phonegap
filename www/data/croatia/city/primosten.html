Primosten - Profile

Location

This town is famous for its vineyards. It sits on a peninsula conected to the mainland by a narrow causeway. It is an attractive town of narrow, mediaeval streets.

Position 43°35′N, 15°55′E. (Premosten)
Position 43°34′02"N, 15°50′06"E. (Kremik Marina)

Clearance

Clearance is available at Kremik marina during the summer.

Last updated February 2014.

Docking

There is a quay on the southern side of the old town where visiting yachts have the option of mooring on the short town quay, where there are laid moorings with water and electricity, or picking up one of the dozen or so mooring buoys laid along the beach on the E side of the bay. Shelter is good in most conditions except strong SW winds, when it becomes very poor to untenable.

There is also a good anchorage in the bay, although again this is open to the south. A more sheltered berth may be found in the inlet to the east.

In settled weather, the bay just north of the town isthmus is a good anchorage.

About 2nm south of Premosten, is the very sheltered Kremik marina.

Last updated July 2015.

This is a summer port of entry and sailing permits can be issued at the marina where police and Customs are in attendance.
Open all year. Well protected marina, all facilities, WiFi, chandlery (limited), provisions, restaurant, max depth 5m, taxis. Fuel available summer only 0600 to 2100. Boatyard with good range of services, 5 ton crane, 80 ton travelift and 50 ton slipway.
