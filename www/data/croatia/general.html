Croatia - General Info

Time Zone

UTC +1. Summer time, UTC +2, end March to end September.

Electrical Supply

Voltage: 230 V

Frequency: 50 Hz

Opening Hours

During the tourist season most shops are open from 0800-2000 on weekdays, and many are also open on weekends. Public services and offices work from 0800-1600 Monday to Friday. Post offices are open from 0700-1900 on week days, in smaller ports from 0700-1400. In larger towns they are open on Saturdays and Sundays.

Money

Official currency - Kuna (1 kuna = 100 lipa).

Whilst Croatia is now a member of the EU, adoption of the Euro has been delayed and is not expected until at least 2019.

Foreign currency can be exchanged in banks, exchange offices, post offices, travel agencies and hotels.

Some hotels etc. may take Euros as payment.

Communications

International Dialing Code for Croatia is +385.

For post office opening times see opening hours.

Phone cards are used in all public telephones and may be purchased from post offices and newspaper kiosks. International calls may be made from any public telephone.

Cellphone Sim Card: .01Euro/min for international calls (2011)
Dongle Sim Card: VIP - Good coverage (1 month 1Gig = 70 Kuna) (2011)
Local sim cards can be purchased from any gas station or tobacco shop, but, you need to get someone to act as a local contract person.

A prepaid SIM card for Croatia with an international cell phone is the most convenient and economical solution,with all incoming calls while in Croatia are FREE, regardless of where they originate. Pay the local rate for local calls and use your cellular phone in Croatia. Croatian Republic SIM card and cell phone service is prepaid so there is no need for a contract.
