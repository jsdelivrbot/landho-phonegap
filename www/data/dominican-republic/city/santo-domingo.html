Santo Domingo - Profile

Location

Situated at the mouth of the Ozama River on the south coast, Santo Domingo has well preserved historic buildings and fascinating narrow cobbled streets. The city also has a wealth of historic relics in the many museums.

Position 18° 27'42"N, 69° 53'18"N (river entrance)

Clearance

See DR Formalities for detailed clearance information and fees.

This port is a commercial port and not suited to visiting cruising yachts. It is best visited by land.

Docking

Unfortunately Santa Domingo is not an attractive place to moor.

The small "marina" here is mostly full of local boats and the current and river debris make it unattractive.

Haina, just west of Santo Domingo, is a commercial port. There is a "marina"  there that is used by local boats, however it is probably the dirtiest place in the DR and also a high crime area.

Last updated March 2015.
