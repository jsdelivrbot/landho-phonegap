Location

Greymouth is the commercial centre for the west coast region of South Island. A useful stopping off point for exploring the Southern Alps and their spectacular glaciers.

Spectacular views of the port entrance, coastline and Southern Alps can be had from the breakwaters, which are accessed from Blaketown or Cobden.

Docking

This is a river port and the entrance is between breakwaters and across a shifting bar, where bad weather can create dangerous conditions. Beacons indicate the entry "leads" or routes, and a flashing blue light at the entrance indicates hazardous conditions.

It is advisable to contact the Port Authority, Harbour Supervisor, before entering.

Greymouth has all the retail, banking and medical facilities required in a regional capital.
