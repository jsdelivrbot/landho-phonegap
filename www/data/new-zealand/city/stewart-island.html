Stewart Island - Profile

Location

This 50 by 30 mile island lies 30km off the southern end of South Island, and is home to a small permanent population. It is almost bisected by a large area of sheltered water known as Paterson Inlet. On the northern shore is the only settlement on the Island, Oban, sitting at the head of Halfmoon Bay.

Much of the island is a National Park and is visited for its wildlife and hiking trails.

Position 46° 53'53"S, 168° 08'E (Halfmoon Bay)

Docking

Oban is the port for the ferries plying the route across the Foveaux Strait to Bluff.

It is possible to anchor in the bay.

On the south west coast of the island, protected anchorages can be found at Port Pegasus. However, the weather is always a major concern this far south in the 'Roaring Forties'. There is no settlement here.
