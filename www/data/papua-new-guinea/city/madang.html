Madang - Profile

Location

This harbour lies on the north coast of the main island. It is a very pretty area with numerous anchorages available in the large harbour which is safe all year round.

Security:
Madang is a popular stop with cruisers on their way to Indonesia, however security problems have increased here in the last 2 years, similar to those afflicting Lae and Port Moresby. A luxury motoryacht was boarded by thieves here in 2014 and a cruising yacht was boarded and attacked in Hansa Bay, October 2015.

Clearance

Customs are just outside the Madang Yacht Club on the right. Officials are said to be friendly.

Docking

Madang is landlocked and provides a sheltered harbour and good holding. The best anchorage is just west of the steamship slipway, at the southern end of the town.

A safe facility for boats is at the Jais Aben diving resort at Nagada Bay, where the boat can be left if touring inland.

Anchoring outside the Madang Club can get a little rolly during northerlies but there are plenty of options further in.

Last updated June 2013.

Madang Yacht Club
(to port, just inside Madang Harbour )

This is a safe place to moor. The floating pontoon here has been upgraded so that smaller yachts can tie up with permission. There are showers, toilets, fresh water, a good swap library, icy cold beer, a small bistro and the Club is within easy walking distance of the market, supermarkets and the local resort.
