Lae - Profile

Location

Located on Papua New Guinea's north east coast, opposite the island of New Britain. It is the second largest city in PNG but is still considered the 'Wild West' of the country.

Lae has been particularly affected by the recent surge in criminality in this part of PNG and it is inadvisable to wander the streets of Lae alone, even in crowded shopping areas.

Position 6°44′S,147°0′E.

Docking

The marina here is a secure place to berth but it is best to use the transport offered by the marina when visiting places in the town. The staff are reported to be very helpful and friendly.

Last updated September 2016.

A vibrant resort-type marina with good facilities (Max LOA 70ft), fuel, powered berths, free water, laundry service, restaurant & bar. The Marina is in a secure guarded compound. Assistance with provisioning by offering transport to local suppliers/banks/shops etc. Contact in advance for availability or if you have any security concerns on the approach to or from Lae. Visiting Yachts must be insured.
