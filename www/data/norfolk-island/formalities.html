Clearance

When approaching the island, call NORFOLK CUSTOMS on VHF Ch 16. Customs and Quarantine officials will meet yachts on either Cascade (north side) or Kingston (south side) jetties.

Customs monitor VHF Channel 16 during working hours only.

There are public phones on both Cascade and Kingston jetties.

A small craft report form is required on arrival at Norfolk Island. It can be downloaded from www.customs.gov.nf/

No one must land until clearance has been granted.

There is a charge for clearance outside of office hours or on a public holiday.

Last updated March 2015.

Immigration

Passports must be valid for 1 month beyond intended stay.

Visa requirements are the same as for Australia.

All nationals, with the exception of Australians and New Zealanders, need to obtain a tourist visa valid for a period longer than 60 days from the intended date of arrival in Norfolk Island.

This visa must be obtained prior to arrival in Norfolk Island. These can be obtained free of charge from Australian high commissions or embassies. A multiple entry visa should be requested if a yacht is en-route for Australia.

It is a good idea to email ahead, if possible, to let them know you are coming.

Last updated March 2015.

Customs

Firearms must be declared on arrival and be kept on board during the yacht's stay.

Landing of fruit, vegetables, flowers and seeds are prohibited. Pork and poultry from New Zealand are also prohibited.

Be sure to radio Customs first before coming ashore.

Last updated March 2015.

Health

Norfolk Island Hospital has extensive facilities including a dental clinic and a prescription dispensary. Emergency services are provided on a 24-hour basis and in serious emergencies, evacuations are provided by the RAAF.

Doctor - Tel (6723)22 09 (hospital)

Dentist - Tel (6723) 22 910.

Updated March 2013.

Fees

Overtime is charged for clearance outside of working hours or on public holidays.

There is a departure tax of Aus$25 if a crew leaves the yacht and departs the island by other means.

Currently there are no fees for customs or anchoring.

Last updated March 2015.

Pets

Animals must be declared to Customs on arrival and remain on board
