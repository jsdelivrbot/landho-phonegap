Himare - Profile

Location

This port is now open to private yachts. It consists of a small fishing port on the coast and a town on the hilltop, a stiff 3km hike away.

Position 40° 06'01"N, 019°44'33"E

Clearance

This is no longer a port of entry.

Last updated June 2015.

Docking

The quay at Himare is concrete and very high. It doesn't offer good wind or swell protection. Yachts should check where to moor with the Harbour Master and may have to move if a fishing vessel requires the space.

A few kilometers south of Himarë along the Albanian Riviera is Porto Palermo (25 nm north of Sarandë). This bay is still used as a military base and yachs have reported not being able to berth here. There is a high concrete pier just behind the small island/fort used by local fishing boats. If there is space yachts may be able to use it as well. There are no facilities here. There is a bay to the east of the entrance which offers good shelter for anchoring and some mooring buoys off the pier.

Warning from http://www.sail-albania.com/157364010: 
Porto Palermos is state owned and a free port. Beware of pirate agents acting illegally here.

Last updated August 2015.
