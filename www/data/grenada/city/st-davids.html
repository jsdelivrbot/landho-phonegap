St Davids - Profile

Location

St. David’s Harbour has natural barrier reefs and a peninsula on either side, which makes it extremely safe. The low rolling hills surrounding the bay are covered with indigenous trees and plants including almond and palm trees. The bay enjoys clean water and a white sandy beach.

There is no main town here, however the Grenada Marine Boatyard has a general store, beach bar, on site Island Water World chandlery and craft shops.

This boatyard offers extensive facilities and hurricane storage. See Boatyards for more details.

The nearest main town is St. George's, approximately 45 minutes from St. Davids by road. There is a frequent public bus service (every 3 to 7 minutes) or the boatyard can organise a taxi/car rental.

Position 12° 01'12"N, 61° 40'45"W (bay)

Docking

International port and traffic signals apply as you travel through the two green and two red flashing channel markers. You are asked to monitor the radio (VHF 16) for navigational information prior to leaving and whilst underway within the harbour.

Yachts can anchor in St. David's Bay and are welcome to use Grenada Marine Boatyard dinghy dock and shoreside services. The boatyard also has moorings available to rent.

The boatyard has a service dock, where boats can come alongside for services and works needed before and after hauling. Use of this dock can be arranged through the boatyard office.
