Tyrell Bay & Hillsborough (Carriacou) - Profile

Location

Carriacou like approximately 40nm north east of the main island of Grenada.

Tyrell Bay is on the western end of the island and is an attractive anchorage. It is the yachting centre for the island of Carriacou.

Hillsborough lies on the north western shore.

Position 12°27'12" N, 61°29'14"W (Tyrell Bay)

Position 12°29′N, 61°28′W (Hillsborough)

Clearance

See Grenada Formalities for detailed information on clearing into and out of Grenada, including downloadable forms.

A new office has opened in Tyrell Bay to process clearance for yachts. This is at Carriacou Marine (the old Yacht Club) and is open 7 days a week - but extra charges apply at weekends and holidays.

The Hillsborough office is still clearing yachts and latest reports are that there are no plans to close it. Officials are reported to be friendly.

Last updated December 2015.

Docking

Tyrell Bay: This is a good, protected anchorage and is the one usually used by yachts. It has a good selection of restaurants/cafes as well as boatyard services, provisions and transport into town.

Hillsborough: Not a particularly sheltered anchorage and subject to swell. Anchor away from the commercial concrete dock which is used by the ferries to Grenada and other islands. There is a dinghy dock on the south side of the concrete pier.

Sandy Island: At the Sandy Island/Oyster Bed Marine Protected Area (SIOBMPA), which encompasses Sandy Island and the mangrove lagoon in Carriacou, an anchoring area has been designated and marked at the four corners with buoys, with the possibility of additional anchoring areas to come. The mooring balls have all been checked and renewed if necessary, and pick-up lines lengthened. Park rangers collect a $10 per day fee for staying here. Yacht with questions should contact SIOBMPA at siobmpa@gmail.com.

Yachtsmen are cautioned to check moorings for reliability.

Duty free fuel (Diesel & Gasoline) plus water are available at the new fuel dock at the Boatyard on the south side of Tyrell Bay.
Gas refills at the Shell Garage in Hillsborough.

Security - Tyrell Bay

June 2016: A dinghy/outboard hoisted on the port side was stolen overnight from an occupied yacht. It was not locked to the yacht.

December 2011: An armed boarding and robbery took place, the first of its kind here (and thankfully nothing since). Read report here.
