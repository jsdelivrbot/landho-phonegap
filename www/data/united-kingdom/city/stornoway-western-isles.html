Location

Stornoway is the capital and largest town in the Western Isles (Outer Hebrides), located 30 miles off the NW coast of Scotland. It is a busy port on the sheltered, eastern side of the island of Lewis and its ferry provides a vital link to mainland Britain. It is also the gateway to the beautiful Outer Hebrides as well as providing an opportunity to explore the unique heritage of Lewis.

Docking

The inner harbour has been dredged and a new marina installed at the northern end of the inner harbour.

Contact the Harbour Master' office in advance to ascertain berth availability prior to arrival.

One hour prior to arrival, contact the Harbour Master on VHF channel 12.

A small anchorage is available between Goat Island and the RO-RO pier. Protected from north wind through easterly to southeasterly.

Glumaig Harbour, approximately 1nm south of the marina, provides a good, sheltered anchorage, although more open to the north. Beware of the fairway that leads into this anchorage. Ships still come occasionally to the fabrication sheds of Arnish.

The inner harbour has no room for anchoring and all the mooring buoys are private.
