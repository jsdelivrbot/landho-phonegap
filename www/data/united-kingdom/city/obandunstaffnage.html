Location

Located on the west coast of Scotland, opposite the Isle of Mull.

Docking

There is no marina at Oban itself. Oban Marina is, in fact, on the opposite shore across Kerrera Sound.

( photo of Oban marina from Andrus of S/Y Suwena)

The marina at Dunstaffnage is just 4NM away, NE of the town.

There are visitors' moorings off the town and at Ardantrive, across Kerrera Sound. The latter are more sheltered in strong SW/NW winds.

The "landing" near visitors' buoys (5deg29'.1W) is a substantial pontoon. (For 30 mins £2 charge or free if on a buoy) (Sept 2010).

There are anchorages at Brandystone, Gallanachbeg, Horseshoe Bay, Little Horseshoe Bay and at Dunstaffnage.

Following lobbying by World Cruising Club and local yachtsmen, CalMac, the operators of the Railway Pier in Oban, have agreed to introduce a reduced short stay tariff. The Railway Pier is normally very congested with fishing boats but there is an area kept free for refuelling (diesel only) behind the new CalMac ro-ro extension to the pier.

Short Stay: Pleasure craft requiring a short stay visit to a maximum of 4 hours between 08:00 and 18:00 will be subject to a nominal charge of £5.00, irrespective of length of vessel. (Non commercial activity only.)
