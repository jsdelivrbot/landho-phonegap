Newlyn - Profile

Location

Lying just to the west of Penzance, Newlyn is the nearest all-tide mainland port to the Scilly Isles. It is primarily a fishing port.

Position 50°06.2'N. 05°32.6'W.

Docking

Newlyn does not have a permanent berthing facility, although it does have finger pontoons usually available for visiting yachts for up to a week or more (if needed) with all tide access. Can be a bit roly in strong S to ESE winds as the Harbour entrance is open in that direction, and the bay is exposed to a long fetch from this quarter.

A useful port of refuge, however fishing boats always have priority here.

In offshore winds, anchor 200m north-east of Newlyn entrance in Gwavas Lake in depths of around 2m.

Last updated July 2011.
