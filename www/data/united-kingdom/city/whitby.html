Whitby - Profile

Location

This ancient fishing port, birthplace of the famous explorer and navigator, James Cook, is becoming increasingly popular with yachts.

Whitby : © Andrus S/Y Suwena

Docking

There are now extra pontoons in the Upper (inner) harbour. The harbour is dredged to a minimum depth of 1.5m which allows most yachts to enter at any state of the tide. A useful feature on this wild stretch of coast. Beware of the strong cross tide at the entrance.

Whitby Yacht Club has a pontoon in the Lower (outer) harbour, near the RNLI lifeboat station, which visiting yachts can use with prior arrangement. There is also usually plenty of room to berth alongside the harbour wall to sta'bd. The bridge into the inner harbour opens on request each half hour during HW +/-2hrs.

Only natural harbour between the Tees and the Humber some 20 miles north of Scarborough on the River Esk past Endeavour Wharf. The marina is to starboard. Swing bridge divides the harbour into upper and lower sections with the marina being in the Upper Harbour. The bridge opens on request (VHF Ch 11) each half hour for 2 hours either side of high water. Electricity and water to each berth.
