Location

Situated on the east coast of the Isle of Harris where the southern part of the island is almost separated from the northern part, Tarbert is a ferry terminal for Skye and North Uist.

Docking

Anchor to the WSW of the ferry pier in about 2-3m.

There are mooring buoys at the southern tip of Harris. See the map for the details.

An alternative refuge can be found in the northern part of the harbour at Scalpay, about 5nm SE of Tarbert.

Tarbert is situated on the west side of Lock Fyne, at 70km the longest sea loch in Scotland opening to the south in the Firth of Clyde. The harbour is a natural amphi-theatre which has easily navigated narrow entrance at the east side opening into Lock Fyne, accessible at all states of the tide. Visiting berths for 65/100 yachts of varying sizes.Water and electricity available on all pontoons, laundrette and chandlery.
