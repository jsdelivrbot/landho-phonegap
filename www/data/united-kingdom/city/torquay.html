Torquay - Profile

Location

Tucked into the northern shore of Tor Bay, Torquay is a typical seaside resort. Holidaymakers are attracted by the wide sands and the better than average amounts of sunshine which occur in its micro-climate.

Position 50°27.4'N, 03°31.7'W.

Docking

In settled weather, there are several anchorages around the Bay, but none providing all-round shelter close to Torquay.

The harbour has a marina and a town dock, both of which provide visitors' berths. There is also a floating pontoon alongside Haldon Pier, which protects the southern side of the harbour.

The inner harbour (access over a sill), is usually full of local boats.

A nice alternative to the town is to take a mooring in Babbacombe Bay.
