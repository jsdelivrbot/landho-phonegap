Blyth - Profile

Location

This busy commercial port lies about 12nm north of the entrance to the Tyne river. A useful bolt hole if the weather or situation requires it, but reported not to be the most attractive of towns.

Position 55° 7.4'N, 01° 30.1'W.

Call on VHF for clearance to enter or leave the port.

Docking

This port offers very good shelter and 24hr acess, but beware that at low water the seas can break across the entrance in strong south easterlies.

There are visitors' berth on the Royal Northumberland yacht club pontoons at the southern end of South Harbour. The entrance to which is at the far end of the western breakwater.

The town is about a 20 minute walk from South Harbour, but there are buses as well as taxis available.

Last checked January 2013.
