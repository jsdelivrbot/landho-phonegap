Location

Port St Mary, on the southern tip of the Isle of Man, is a drying harbour.

Docking

There are 4 visitors moorings in the deep water in the outer harbour and it is possible to raft alongside Alfred Pier. The inner harbour dries to hard, level sand. Call the Harbourmaster for advice on where to berth. There is a landing near below the IOM Yacht Club.

Shelter is good except in SE or E winds. The holding south of Gansey Point is poor. There is a facilities block with toilets and showers close to Alfred pier.
