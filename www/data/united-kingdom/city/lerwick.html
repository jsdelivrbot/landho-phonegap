Lerwick - Profile

Location

Lerwick lies on the east coast of the Shetland Mainland and is Shetland's only town. It is a modern harbour providing support for the offshore oil and gas industry. It is also a busy fishing and ferry port. With two entrances, it is accessible in all weathers.

Docking

Berthing is on the yacht pontoons in the Small Dock (located each side of Victoria Pier) or on visitors' buoys.

( photo of Lerwick harbour from Andrus of S/Y Suwena)

Showers available for visiting yachtsmen are located on the lower floor of the Lerwick Boating Club clubhouse. An access key is provided when paying harbour dues at the Port Office.

A useful visitors' pack is available at http://www.lerwick-harbour.co.uk/assets/files/yachtpack.pdf

For connecting to the electricity supply, contact the Port Office. Initial connection can be made Monday to Friday (0700-2000), Saturday (0800-1200) and Sunday (0800-1000). There is a charge.

Busy club with the races, training and International yacht races - including the Shetland-Bergen and Round Britain and Ireland races. The club welcomes visiting yachtsmen. When settling your harbour dues on arrival in Lerwick Harbour, you can obtain a Clubhouse key (subject to a deposit). You can then have access to the Club's 3 showers and the washing/drying facilities. If no keys are available at the Harbour Office, please call along the Clubhouse during bar opening times, where they will issue you with a key.
