St Mary's - Profile

Location

This is the main island in The Scillies. The port, Hugh Town, is on the western side and is the centre of all services for the islands.

Position 49°55'N, 06°8.7'W.

Clearance

Customs clearance - there is no Customs Office in Scilly.

Non-EU yachts or yachts with non-EU crew should fly flag Q and telephone 0800 7231110 to clear in

Last updated February 2016.

Docking

The easiest and best marked passage into the main anchorages of St Mary's from the mainland and also from the south is through St Mary's Sound between Gugh and St Mary's.

An alternative passages for experienced yachtsmen approaching from the south are Smith Sound, Broad Sound and the North West Passage (formerly known as the "North Channel").

Hugh Town Harbour

In the harbour, there are 28 yellow visitors' buoys for boats up to 40ft and 10 green buoys for boats up to 60ft. Rafting is permitted (with consent from the original boat).

There is good shelter except in W/NW gales.

The mooring buoy charges are £18.50 per night for vessels to 40ft and £24.50 to 60ft. For vessels paying for three nights the fourth night is free

Alongside charges are £25/night for up to 40ft and £30/night for up to 60ft.

A water taxi operates from 8am to 5pm.

There is no anchoring within the harbour itself.

The harbour wall provides room for 3 alongside berths (depth 1m over CD) and a tender landing pontoon. There is water and electricity here.

Garbage bins are provided but for any waste from boats arriving from outside the EU, contact the Harbour office.

Porth Cressa (south of Hugh Town)

There now are 14 private yellow visitor's moorings here for boats up to 45ft.

Charge is £20/night. Showers ashore by the information centre. WiFi also at the moorings.

Anchorages
In W to NW winds, Porth Cressa provides a good anchorage in 2m. It is exposed to any swell from SE to SW.

Watermill Cove, on the NE corner of the island, offers good protection in winds from S to NW. Anchor in 5m.

Last updated February 2016.
