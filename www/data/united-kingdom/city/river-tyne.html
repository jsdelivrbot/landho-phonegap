Location

The river Tyne, about 60nm south of the Scottish border, is one of the major rivers on England's eastern coast. It allows yachts to sail the 7 miles inland to the heart of the city of Newcastle.

Docking

Although not the shipbuilding centre it once was, the River Tyne is a very busy waterway and there are no really safe anchorages here.

There is a marina close to the river entrance at North Shields and another two at Newcastle itself.

An alternative berthing can be found at Blyth, about 10nm north of the entrance to the Tyne or at Sunderland about 7nm south.

Close to the entrance to the River Tyne allowing easy access to and from the open sea as well as being ideally placed for cruising further up the Tyne. Just over an hours motoring upstream beings you to heart of the city of Newcastle. High reputation marina accommodates 350 pontoon berths, 50 of which are fully serviced visitor berths. Facilities include free WiFi, fuel, toilets, showers, laundry, water, electricity. Full boatyard service with 40T hoist. Accessed via double sector lock gates which open at all states of the tide and 24 hours a day.
