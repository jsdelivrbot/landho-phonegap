United Kingdom - General Info

Time Zone

UTC

Summer time UTC +1 March to October.

Electrical Supply

Voltage: 230 V

Frequency: 50 Hz

Opening Hours

Most businesses are open 09:00-17:00 (Mon-Fri)

Marine businesses also open Saturdays, and some on Sundays too, especially during the sailing season.

Supermarkets open 7 days a week, often from 08:00 until 20:00 or later (Mon-Sat), Sundays hours are restricted to 10:00-16:00.

Money

Pound sterling (£) of 100 pence.

Communications

International dialing code for the United Kingdom is +44

Wi-Fi is available at almost all harbours in the UK, sometimes at no charge. You can register and pay on-line for harbour Wi-Fi services and you will encounter some of the same companies in different areas. Internet is available at local public libraries and internet cafes and some marinas and harbour masters have an office internet-connected computer available.

Note: THE CENTRAL SOLENT
Due to the heavy amount of traffic using VHF channel 16 in this area, uniquely, an initial call to Solent Coastguard should be made on Ch 67 for routine traffic and radio checks.
Radio checks may, of course, be conducted on any working channel with other stations – marinas, yachts etc. Weather messages ... .


Mobile phones - easy access to the network coverage for every major UK mobile phone network, for both 3G and 2G systems. Prepaid SIM Card recommended for visitors to the UK

Transport

There are good transport facilities throughout the U.K.

Most towns and all major ports have a train station within a reasonable distance.

Small ports may only have a bus connection.

Rental cars are available everywhere.

Diplomatic Missions

Foreign Embassies in United Kingdom

Events

If visiting historic sites is planned, consider becoming a member of English Heritage (www.english-heritage.org.uk), or the National Trust (www.nationaltrust.org.uk), which, for a modest annual membership fee entitles you to free, or reduced cost, admission to a huge number of historic sites in England, Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland. Many of these sites also hold annual concerts and events, see the websites for further details.

Lochs and Lights: The West Coast of Mainland Scotland
By Patrick Roach and Gordon Buchanan
Publisher Imray,Laurie,Norie & Wilson Ltd (1 Jun 2010)
ISBN-13: 978-1846231698
This addition to the range covers the mainland part of the West coast of Scotland, one of the most attractive sailing areas in the British Isles. Starting from the estuaries of Galloway on the Solway Firth, the tour leads northwards across the Firth of Clyde, taking in Ardnamurchan, Oban, Loch Linnhe, Mallaig and past Skye to reach the lonely and dramatic cliffs of Cape Wrath. Patrick Roach's outstanding aerial photos, taken in a typical range of weather and tidal conditions, are matched by a commentary and description by West coast based yachting journalist, Gordon Buchanan. These wonderful views from the air give an idea of the drama and variety in the long and complex Scottish coast, and for anyone at sea as well as walkers and other landside visitors, Lochs and lights is the ideal companion.
