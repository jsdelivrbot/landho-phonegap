Charlotte Amalie (St Thomas) - Profile

Location

This sheltered harbour lies on the southern coast of the island of St Thomas. Charlotte Amalie is the capital of the US Virgin Islands and is a popular port of call for cruise ships.

Position 18 20'17"N, 64 56'17"W. (opposite Reporting Office)

Position 18 19'57"N, 64 57'04"W (Crown Bay marina)

Clearance

If arriving outside local working hours, report your arrival leaving a brief message with boat name, registration number, date and time of arrival and a return telephone number.

The captain should go ashore and obtain preliminary clearance at the offices on the western end of the waterfront next to the seaplane landing. All crew must then go with their completed papers to customs and immigration at the ferry dock. One crew member is allowed to stay on board as an anchor watch. However, if not a US national he or she will have to clear in person later at the same place.

Docking

There are two IGY Marinas in St. Thomas, American Yacht Harbour and Yacht Haven Grande.

Cruising yachts tend to gather at Crown Bay, with anchorage across from the mouth of the marina. Close to Tickles Pub.

At the eastern end of the island, there are several small marinas in Benner Bay, Vessup Bay and around Red Hook.
