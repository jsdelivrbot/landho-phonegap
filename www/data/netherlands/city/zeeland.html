Zeeland - Profile

Location

This area of Holland which borders Belgium is also known as the Delta area. It consists of a series of linked islands, much of which are below sea level, and provides a huge area of sheltered sailing water.

All waters are clearly buoyed during the season (April - September). There are numerous small harbours, free yellow moorings (installed during the season) and numerous quiet and protected anchorages.

Docking

Access to this area is through locks from the North Sea or via the canal from Amsterdam into the Ijsselmeer.

There are many marinas and yacht havens in the Zeeland region, as well as unlimited anchorages.

Grevelingen Meer

Port Zeland is situated adjacent to Brouwerdam in the northern part of the Meer.

Scharendijke Haven is on its eastern shore, adjacent to the town of that name.

Port Den Osse is just east of Scharendijke.

Jachthaven Bruinisse is close to the lock at the southern end of the Meer.

Herkingen Marina is on the eastern side of the Meer by the town of that name.
