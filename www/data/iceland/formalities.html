Iceland - Formalities

Clearance

The captain must inform Customs of the yacht's arrival who will then give clearance to all places within Iceland.

No cruising permit is required; however, yachts must obtain from Customs a permit to temporarily import the vessel for exemption from duty.

Customs and Border Control work together and Customs will often perform both functions.

It is advisable to leave the yellow Customs form visible in a window when leaving the yacht unattended.

Customs require 2 hours notice of departure.

Last updated March 2016.

Immigration

Passports must be valid for three months beyond the intended stay.

Although not a member of the EU, Iceland is a member of the Schengen Agreement Area. See Noonsite' Schengen page for more details on the immigration rules.

In most cases, Danish embassies will handle visa applications on behalf of Iceland. A list of these embassies is available on the Directorate of Immigration web site, www.utl.is or contact The Directorate of Immigration, Borgartún 30, IS-105 Reykjavík, Tel. +354-510-5400, Fax: +354-562-3375, E-mail: utl@utl.is

Last updated October 2014.

Customs

Firearms must be declared on arrival and remain under seal while in Iceland.

Duty-free allowances are 1 litre spirits, 1 litre wine, and 200 cigarettes. Alternatively, the alcohol allowance can be 1 litre spirits, 6 litres beer or 1 litre wine, 6 litres beer or 2 litres of wine. The minimum age for these allowances is 20 years.

The sale of alcohol in Iceland is strictly controlled.

Uncooked meat and eggs may be prohibited.

Last updated February 2015.

Fees

Overtime fees are charged 1700-0800 Monday to Friday, and all of Saturday and Sunday.

Port charges, 2500 icelandic crowns for up to one week's stay, includes berthing and use of facilities.

Pets

Animals which come under the Pets Travel Scheme (PETS) with current Pet Passport, microchip, Health Certificate, Rabies Vaccination Certificate and have been blood tested by recognised Veterinarian can be taken into Iceland.
