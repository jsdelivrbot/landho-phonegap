Reykjavik - Profile

Location

Reykjavik, the capital and largest city in Iceland, lies on the south western corner of the island. Half the population of Iceland live here. It is a useful place from which to explore inland.

Position 64°09′09"N, 21°55′51"W (harbour entrance)

Clearance

Contact the Harbour Master's office on VHF Channel 12 if this is your Port of Entry. They will contact Customs who will meet you on the dock. Customs also visit the harbour regularly each day.

Last updated March 2016.

Reykjavik Customs

Docking

Call the Harbour Master on VHF channel 12 on arrival. They can advise on a suitable berth.

Other berthing for visiting cruisers may be found at the Brokey Yacht Club, which is to port on entering the harbour. It has a floating visitor pontoon with room for 2 yachts and a detached floating pontoon with room for more. For longer stays, the yacht club may be able to find a free berth. It is open May to September. The YC is under the large glass Harpa Building.

Last updated March 2016.
