Isafjordur, West Fjords - Profile

Location

This is a small, friendly,  ice-free port on the northern side of Westfjords. It is a useful staging post for a trip to Greenland, allowing extended cruising there. It supports a large fishing fleet and has most boating services.

View of Isafjordur © http://en.wikipedia.org

Position 66°04′N, 23°07′W.

Docking

Isafjordur is one of the best winter harbours in Iceland.

Both visiting and local boats are kept in the protected inner harbor along a massively rebuilt floating pontoon. The harbormasters office overlooks the pontoon. Lines are taken across to the opposite dock during the winter to suspend the boats off the dock. The boats are well tended and they are safe. Both local people and officials watch the boats and tend the lines.

There are plans for a cruiser’s lounge with toilets, showers, washer and dryer and WiFi for the 2014 season.

It is possible to haul out here and have shore storage.
