Kyrenia (Girne) - Profile

Location

This port is call Kyrenia by Greek Cypriots and Girne by the Turks.

Situated on the north coast of Cyprus, this port has a 6,000 year long history and the remains of countless civilisations. Bounded to the north by the sea and to the south by the greenery of the Besparmak (Fivefinger) Mountain range, it offers the most magnificent scenery on the island.

The beautiful old harbour is dominated by a majestic castle which houses a museum containing the remains of an ancient ship which was salvaged from the sea.

This is a good place to provision.

Position 35°20’32"N, 34°19’31"E (old harbour)

Clearance

For the full details of clearing into and out of Cyprus, go to Noonsite Cyprus Formalities.

The authorities are based in the commercial harbour, but the Delta marina office can also arrange clearance.

The marina office can only get the passports from the Immigration office and permission for departure from the Harbour master if notified early of date and time of departure.

Last updated March 2016.

Harbour Master
Opening hours: 8:00-17:00 (Mon-Fri)

Docking

It has been reported the yachts are no longer permitted to berth in the old harbour.

Berths are to be found in the Delta Marina, situated in the commercial harbour. It lies about 1nm to the east and provides good shelter.

The Harbourmaster will provide pilotage information on VHF channel 16.

In calm, settled weather it is possible to anchor just outside the marina.

Last updated March 2016.
