Puerto La Cruz - Profile

Location

This well-known stop on the crusing route is an interesting town to visit.

Position 10º 12' 24" N, 64º 40' 05" W.

Clearance

The marinas in the El Morro Tourist complex offer help with clearance formalities. All of the marinas have 24 security and safety here is reported to be the best in all of Venezuela.

On departure, the Port Captain requires you to file a sail plan before being allowed to leave a Marina.

Last updated August 2012.

Docking

The marinas are the only safe place to berth here. There are several to choose from.

Anchoring is not advisable for safety reasons.

Due to several fatal boating accidents in the El Morro Lagoon area, the local authorities have implemented a number of regulations to be followed if using a dinghy in this area. The Costa Guardia may stop and fine you if you are not compliant. All occupants of dinghies should be wearing life preservers, and proof of ownership of the dinghy and outboard should be on board. Ask for the latest copy of the rules on arrival as they are subject to change.

Last updated August 2012.

Note October 2014: The Caribbean Safety and Security Net reported a burglary from a yacht left unattended at Bahia Redonda Marina.
