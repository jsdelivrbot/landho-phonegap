Slovenia - Formalities

Clearance

There are no formalities for EU registered boats with only EU citizens on board arriving from an EU country.

If arriving from outside the EU, fly the Q flag when 12 miles off shore.

If required to report on arrival, enter at one of the official Ports of Entry. Wait for the officials to come to the yacht for clearance. In marinas, the staff will call the relevant officials.

If no official arrives, the captain should report to the police for passport control, as well as Customs and Harbour Master. The crew must remain on board until formalities are completed.

Last updated February 2016.

Immigration

Visitors must have a valid passport.

Slovenia is now a member of the Schengen Agreement Area. See Noonsite's European Union page for details.

Citizens of the EU are not limited in the length of their stay, but after 3 months must apply for a residence permit.

Visas are required by nationals of countries with whom there is no reciprocal agreement. See Slovenian Visa Requirements.

Last updated February 2016.

Customs

Firearms must be declared on arrival and will be sealed and checked again when leaving.

Radio equipment must be declared and an operator's permit shown.

Yachts left unattended for longer periods, such as for wintering, will be bonded by Customs.

Last updated February 2016.

Health

Tick-borne encephalitis is common to Slovenia, so take precautions especially if you intend to visit forested areas during the summer months.

Documents

Documents needed are the registration certificate, certificate of competence, insurance certificate, crew lists and radio licence.

A list must also be prepared of all dutiable items such as alcohol, tobacco and any movable objects not part of the yacht's equipment, such as cameras, portable radios, diving equipment or outboard engine.

When clearance is completed, a cruising permit will be issued for a small fee (around US$15 for a 40 ft/12 m boat).

Restrictions

Certain restrictions apply to diving and these should be checked when clearing in.

Pets

The Pets Travel Scheme (PETS) applies in Slovenia and all pets require a Pet Passport, be microchipped, a current Health Certificate and a current Rabies Vaccination Certificate issued by recognised Veterinarian and to have been blood tested prior to travel.
