Palau (Belau) - Formalities

Clearance

Yachts inbound to Palau are invited to contact the Royal Belau Yacht Club at Sams Tours for information and or assistance with Entry Clearance. Send your vessel details to rbyc@samstours.com with list of your last 3 ports, your next port, crew list and ETA and RBYC will provide courtesy notice to authorities of your pending arrival. There is no charge for their assistance.

An advance permit is no longer required.

Palau Port Control monitors VHF Channel 16  from 8:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., Monday to Friday. Vessels must CHECK IN immediately upon arrival at the official Port of Entry on the East side of Malakal Harbour at the Commercial Dock. Call +680 775 0419 or +680 488 5789 to contact Port Control Duty Officer.

Anchoring before Clearing In is prohibited.

Arriving vessels must contact Palau Port Control on VHF Channel 16 before mooring at the Commercial Dock.

Do not enter the harbour in the dark or in poor conditions  as paper and electronic charts are not accurate for Palau. The GPS position for CHECK IN at the Commercial Dock is: 07° 19.84' N , 134° 27.48' E.

A yacht may not stop at any other place before clearing in at Malakal, unless the Chief of Immigration grants permission, if it is in the public interest of that place or an emergency situation.

A yacht in distress may anchor or land at any port in Palau but must immediately notify the nearest government official to contact the Chief of Immigration. Yachts stopping anywhere else other than Malakal will be instructed to proceed to Malakal immediately. It is very important to keep in touch with the Authorities.

On arrival, an Entry Permit is issued for 30 days with two extensions of 30 days each possible, making a maximim stay of 90 days. US registered boats are permitted a one year permit.

Be prepared for lots of Government officials to participate in Vessel Entry Clearance. Officials are generally very courteous and polite, but there’s just a lot of them including: Customs, Immigration, Sanitation, Quarantine, Transportation, Health Dept, Public Safety (Police), and possibly a few others.

Departure

Twenty-four hours notice is required before departure. Call the Department of Transportation at 488-4224 to inform them of your pending departure. Customs and Immigration officials will meet you at Sam’s Tours on departure day at an agreed time.

On departure, a fee per person is payable.

Immigration

All vistors require a valid passport valid for at least 6 months.

No visas are required and visitors are granted a 30 day visa on entry.

Those wishing to stay longer must apply to the immigration office in good time and not later than 7 days before the expiration of the 30 day period.

No more than two extensions of 30 days each will be granted and there is a fee of $US50 for each extension.

Citizens of the USA, Federated States of Micronesia, the Marshall Islands, Guam, and the Northern Marianas Islands can now stay in Palau for one year before needing to apply for a visa.

If crew will arrive in Palau by air on a one-way ticket and depart by sea, Immigration must be notified in advance so they in turn can advise the airline(s) to permit the passenger(s) to board, otherwise the passenger(s) may be denied boarding on their flight in to Palau. A minimum of 48 hours notice is required and must be done during government business hours.

Customs

All Customs charges (see Fees below) apply uniformly to all arriving yachts and vessels that are not locally owned or registered.

Firearms must be declared on entry and will be removed and held by the Bureau of Public Safety until departure.

Anti-drug laws are strictly enforced. Prescriptions should be carried for any special medicines or drugs in your possession.

Any other items carried that were not declared and appeared to be concealed can, and will, be seized. Example: Illegal Drugs, Artifacts, Firearms and ammunition, etc.

As Palau's unique island eco-system is very susceptible to invasive specifies there are tight restrictions on fresh fruits, vegetables, plants, meats and other possible sources of contamination. Any such items will be confiscated.

Prepare an itemized inventory of ship's stores for review by Quarantine. Dispose of any organic trash properly before arriving in Palau. Quarantine may confiscate for incineration any organic trash, fresh foodstuffs and any meats of concern to them.

On Departure
Any firearms and ammunition removed on arrival will be returned. Customs will conduct a physical inspection to ensure that departing vessels do not have on board artifacts and remnants of historical assets, or stowaways.

Health

Cholera and Yellow Fever immunizations are required for those arriving from infected areas.

Belau National Hospital’, in Koror, has an operating suite, dialysis machines and an outpatient clinic. The hospital also houses a pharmacy that dispenses basic medicines.

Fees

Clearing in:

Ensure that you receive a receipt for each fee paid.

Vessel Entry Permit: £50
Valid 30 days. $50 for 30 day extension. Max of 2 extensions. US boats get 1 year.

Vessel water tax/Cruising permit: $50 Valid for a year.

Immigration: $50 per person.
If staying more than one month, there is a $50 per person per month visa extension fee. Maximum stay is 3 months. US citizens are permitted a 1 year stay for no charge.

A minimum of three hours of overtime is charged per Immigration officer on duty for all arrivals and departures outside of normal working hours (7:30am to 4:30pm Mon-Fri). The amount is calculated by multiplying the average hourly overtime rate by the number of officers on duty for a vessel and the sum of the number of hours worked or multiplied by three hours, whichever is greater.

Line Handlers Fees: If the Port Office is not informed that these are not required, then the port will attempt to charge you a fee based on your vessel’s tonnage, typically $80 or more for an average 40 foot yacht. The Skipper may be able to negotiate this down to a flat $20. If you do not throw any lines to the dock handlers, you are not required to pay a fee.

Dockage: All vessels are assessed a fee for use of the Commercial Dock at a rate of $.06/Net Registered Ton. The Skipper will be instructed to go to the Koror State Ranger’s Office – about a quarter-mile walk each way – to pay this fee.

On Departure
Customs charge a $35 per boat departure tax (green tax).

Other Fees

Koror State Cruising Permit: This is required if wishing to cruise outside Malakal harbour. The permit costs approximately $10/foot of boat length, up to a maximum $80, and is valid for 30 days.

Rock Island Permits: Each individual wishing to go swimming, snorkeling, diving, kayaking, etc. or using the beaches within the Rock Islands. The fees are:

$50 per person – valid for 10 days EXCLUDING access to Jellyfish Lake.

$100 per person – valid for 10 days INCLUDING access to Jellyfish Lake.

Fishing Licence: $20/person.

An incineration charge of $35 per bag applies to any confiscated trash.

Last updated November 2014.

Restrictions

Some parts of the Rock Islands, including the so called 70 islands, are nature reserves and access by any boat is strictly prohibited. The port captain will show on a chart the prohibited areas.

All individuals are required to purchase a Rock Island Permit from Koror State Government if swimming, snorkeling, diving, kayaking etc. and or using the beaches within Koror State. See the above Fees section for costs.

An individual Koror State Fishing License showing the bearers name is required for each person fishing.

Peleliu State has a similar Permit requirement which applies to divers only (not snorkelers).

Local Customs

It is illegal to drink alcohol in public, except on licensed premises.

Pets

Animals must be declared to Customs on arrival.
