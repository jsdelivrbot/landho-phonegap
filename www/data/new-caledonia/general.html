New Caledonia - General Info

Time Zone

UTC +11

Electrical Supply

Voltage: 220 V

Frequency: 50 Hz

Opening Hours

Most shops and offices open from 7:30 - 11:30 am and 1:30 - 6pm closing for lunch. Banks and the Post Office stay open during lunch but close earlier at 3:45 pm. Some shops open on Saturday and supermarkets open on Sunday morning. The Noumea Market with its local produce is held daily at the Baie de la Moselle. It opens from 5am-11am and is closed the 3rd Monday of the month.

Last updated December 2008.

Money

French Pacific franc (CFP Fr) of 100 centimes. It has a fixed rate to the Euro [1 Euro = 119.332 CFP).

Credit cards are accepted by most businesses and tourist resports.

Communications

International Dialing Code for New Caledonia is +687.

New Caledonia has a modern satellite communications system allowing direct calls overseas. Most places have mail, internet and telephone facilities.

New Caledonia Internet
Access to the Internet is easy in Noumea, where there are numerous Internet Cafes. The Rocket Guide to New Caledonia includes the locations of the most popular Internet Cafes. The cost of Internet in New Caledonia is very high, between 500 and 1000 cfp per hour, and the "high speed" connections are miserably slow compared to broadband connections in other countries.

OPT, the New Caledonia telecom service, has a "Carte iZi" that lets you log onto the Internet with a special number from any phone at all - including mobile phones or hotel phones. This is the most economical way to get connected to the Internet in Noumea (4.5 cfp per minute). The connection however is reported to be painfully slow.

SSB
See Pacific List of Radio Nets
The Namba Net operates now on 8101 because 6 megs became too noisy. It is at 0815 hrs local time for vessels cruising New Caledonia and Vanuatu.

Please note that the Namba net only operates when there are sufficient cruisers prepared to act as net controllers. Ideally 7 people but it usually starts with 4. It starts usually sometime in June and finishes in October. The 8 meg frequency covers Vanuatu. New Caledonia, the Solomon Islands and the Louisaides.

Mobile Phones
GSM mobile phones work almost everywhere near population centres throughout New Caledonia. The Rocket Guide to New Caledonia includes a coverage map and full details of mobile phone use.

International roaming is available from most countries so your normal mobile phone and telephone number should work in New Caledonia. Ask your GSM provider before you leave to arrange for roaming in New Caldeonia.

Last updated June 2012.

Transport

La Grande Terre, Loyalty Islands and the Isle of Pines all have an excellent network of roads. Major roads are all sealed. Driving is on the right hand side of the road and rental cars are widely available. A valid driving licence is required to rent a car - the visitor centre has a list of companies. Taxis, mopeds and motorbikes are also available.
