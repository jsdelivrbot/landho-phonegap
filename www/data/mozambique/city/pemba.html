Pemba - Profile

Location

Located about 120nm south of the border with Tanzania in the northern section of the country.

This popular tourist port occupies the peninsula on the southern shore of the entrance to Baie de Pemba. Much of its popularity is due to the fact that there is a coral reef close to the shore and the town contains an interesting local market selling silverware as well as local crafts. Whale watching here in September is a spectacular show.

Position 12°58′S,40°33′E.

Clearance

See Noonsite/Mozambique/formalities page for the details.

Clear in at the Harbour Master's office where the Immigration agent will stamp passports (containing a valid visa) and an entry document issued which will permit cruising the area and clearance to Maputu, with stops on the way.

Harbour Master is in a big building just outside the exit from the port on the left.

It is not necessary for private vessels to visit Customs.

Some Portuguese is an advantage as very little English is spoken by officials.

Total cost of entry is about $225US (2013)

Last updated October 2013.

Docking

Yachts can anchor by Wimbe beach (12° 58.147' S, 40° 32.432' E) which is open to the north but has good holding and nice surroundings.

There is a good anchorage east of the harbour at 12° 58.179' S, 40° 29.476' E, opposite the green roofed Wilson’s Wharf restaurant.

Last updated October 2013.
