Sweden - General Info

Time Zone

UTC +1, Summer time UTC +2 from the end of March to the end of October.

Electrical Supply

Voltage: 230 V

Frequency: 50 Hz

Money

Swedish Krona (Skr) of 100 öre.

Credit cards widely accepted.

Besides banks, money can also be exchanged at Forex exchange offices, and at post offices showing the PK Exchange sign.

Communications

International Dialing Code for Sweden is +46.

Mobile telephones are widely used in Sweden. You can link up to the Telia, Tele2, 3 or Telenor GSM networks ( 900 & 1800 Mhz).

Maps showing the coverage of these networks can be accessed on the Internet.

Mobile phones cannot necessarily be used for emergencies – in the mountains, for example, there might be no coverage!

Internet & mail services - Most airports, train stations, public libraries (free) and hotels will offer the service of computers with Internet access. There are also various cafés with internet terminals in most cities.

Foreign Embassies in Sweden

Emergencies

Dial 112 (free from payphones) includes police, fire, ambulance and sea rescue.

Distress and urgency signals are broadcast on VHF Ch 16 or 17.

24-hour NHS emergency number Tel. 112. The casualty department at a hospital is called Akutmottagning or Vardcentral in more remote areas.

The Swedish equivalent of the RNLI is Sjoraddningssallskapet provides an assistance service as follows - Free assistance for offshore breakdown - Free tow to the nearest port - Help in contacting boatyards, maritime engineers or relatives. For further details contact Gunnar Arwidson, Sjoraddningssallskapet - Tel +46 31 29 00 90 or email gunnar.arwidson@ssrs.se

Arholma - Landsort and Gotland
By Lars Hassler and Lars Granath
Publisher TBC - Edition: 1st (English)
ISBN 9789189564442
The English edition of the Swedish pilot covering the Stockholm archipelago and the island of Gotland. Full coverage of harbours and anchorages with navigational detail, descriptions of facilities. and lots of background information. The guide is well illustrated with coloured harbour plans, charts and aerial photos. See www.nautiska.com (in Swedish only).

Landsort to Skanor
By Catharina Soderbergh
Publisher Nautiska Forlaget
ISBN 9789189564749
Edition 1st (2009)
The pilot Landsort-Skanor covers the coastal area from Landsort and the whole southern coast down to Skane, a total of 400 nautical miles. The pilot also includes the island of Oland, the Danish island of Bornholm and Christanso, the Swedish heartland water passage Gota Canal and the large lake Vattern. This is an English version and presents harbour facts and detailed information of some 190 natural harbours and 147 guest harbours.
