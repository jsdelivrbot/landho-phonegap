Australia - General Info

Time Zone

Western Australia UTC + 8. South Australia, Northern Territory UTC + 91/2. Eastern Australia UTC + 10. Summer time + 1 from late October to late March in South Australia, NSW, Tasmania and Victoria.

Electrical Supply

Voltage: 240v - (Outlets typically controlled by adjacent switch)

Frequency: 50 Hz

Money

Australian dollar (Aus$)

Communications

See Pacific List of Radio Nets

International dialing code for Australia is +61.

Cell phone coverage and service in Australia is surprisingly good and uses the GSM service in order to provide mobile phone users with cellular capability. As Coverage is available where there are people or major access roads. Amaysim have an easy to acquire SIM card and a reasonable pre-paid plan.

Telstra's network is the largest in the country and provides excellent coverage, even in the most remote parts of the country. Reported to support high quality skype almost anywhere. The Elite Modem comes recommended. Various plans available for internet access through the cellular phone system. Telstra do require a number of proofs of identity and a credit check.

All McDonalds in Australia have free Wi-Fi, however, don't provide electrical sockets. Therefore be sure to arrive at McDonalds with a fully charged laptop.

Cruising the Coral Coast - Classic guide to the East Coast of Australia and the Great Barrier Reef
By Alan Lucas
Publisher: Alan Lucas Cruising Guides - Edition: 9th edition 2014
ISBN: 9780958176835
First published in 1968, this book has reached its eighth edition and remains the only guide to cover the whole of the east coast of Queensland, the Torres Strait and the Great Barrier Reef. The current edition, updated to 2014, includes notes on the Gulf of Carpentaria and the Coral Sea. This edition is the biggest yet with larger, more detailed charts and more ports and anchorages than in previous editions. It's as close to a must have guide as your ever likely to find if your plans include cruising along the Queensland Coast.

Cruising the New South Wales Coast
By Alan Lucas
Publisher Alan Lucas Cruising Guides - Edition 6 - October 2010
ISBN: 9780958580748
New edition of what has long been the Bible of this coast. First released in 1976, this is the sixth edition of Cruising the New South Wales Coast. Surveyed and produced aboard the author's vessel Soleares, it describes the entire coast between the Victorian and Queensland borders including all navigable rivers, many well beyond their first fixed bridge for the benefit of those with motor boats, trailer sailors, large tenders, runabouts and kayaks. This edition also includes Alan's popular Sydney to Central Coast Waterways Guide.

Save the Great Barrier Reef
Posted December 2012
50 years ago, Australians ran a successful community campaign to create the Great Barrier Reef World Heritage Area. The Great Barrier Reef is now under threat once again with massive port expansions planned Reef-wide. The Great Barrier Reef is the world’s largest reef system and hundreds of cruisers visit there every year. To be a part of this campaign, take a look at the website and help raise funds for a run of advertisements across Australia to stop massive port expansions that threaten the reef.
