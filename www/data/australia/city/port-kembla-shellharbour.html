Port Kembla / Shellharbour - Profile

Location

Port Kembla is situated in a very popular holiday area, about 50 miles south of Sydney. Although Port Kembla itself was originally a steel town, it is adjacent to Shellharbour and Wollongong. These towns both have sheltered harbours, and the warm water and coral make it a very popular scuba diving area.

Position 34° 27'45"S, 150° 54'48"E (entrance Port Kemba)

Clearance

On arrival, yachts should pick up the Small Craft Reporting Buoy between Jetty No 3 and No 4 (when it is installed).

The current arrangements are to drop anchor between Jetty No 3 and No 4 until further advice from Customs.

For full details of Clearance into Australia, see Noonsite/Australia/Clearance page.

Last updated August 2013.

Docking

Wollongong boat harbour is about 2nm north of Port Kembla and provides excellent shelter. Moor to the wharf.

Shellharbour is 8nm south of Kembla. It is small and the entrance can be dangerous in strong on shore winds. Anchor in the harbour.
