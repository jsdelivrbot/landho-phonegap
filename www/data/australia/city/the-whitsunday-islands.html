The Whitsunday Islands - Profile

Location

This group of islands off the Queensland coast is one of Australia's most well-known cruising areas. The largest of the islands is Whitsunday Island itself, and it is surrounded by many others. The biggest of these being Hook, Border, Haslewood, Hamilton, Lindeman and Shaw.

Position 20°14'S, 149°01'E (eastern side of Whitsunday Island)

Clearance

The nearest Port of Entry is at MacKay.

Docking

There are many safe anchorages, usually protected by reefs, among The Whitsundays, so in finding a safe haven for the night, you are spoilt for choice.

There are marinas at Hamilton Island and at Abell Point (Airle Beach - see photo). At Abell Point there is a private anchorage available outside the breakwater under seperate management to the marina.

Note that even though the channel to Laguna Quays Marina has silted, it is still accessible with sufficient rise of tide.

In 2012 cruisers reported that AUD$10million public liability insurance was required at Abell Point Marina, however not at Hamilton Island Marina. The marinas further north however do require it. See noonsite report for further details.

Laguna Quays Marina
Kunapipi Springs Road , Midge Point, Queensland, Australia 4800
Tel:+61 7 4947 7844 Fax:+61 7 4945 4597
Although the resort has closed, the marina is open. Water, electricity, showers and toilets only. Best suited for long-term in water storage. Car required as no public transport and taxis difficult. No repair, fuel or other services.
The 1km channel, has silted but still allows access to the marina in most tidal conditions for boat with a draft of up to 6ft. (Dec 2013)
Port of Airlie Marina
Muddy Bay , Airlie Beach , The Whitsundays
Tel:+61 (0)7 4948 0643
http://www.portofairlie.com.au , info@portofairlie.com.au
This is a newly opened marina. Water and electricity to all berths. The complex contains a resort, shops, restaurants & bars. Close to downtown and the Whitsunday Sailing Club.
Anchorage is available in Muddy Bay.
Shute Harbour
Tel:+61 (0)7 4946 9557
This is a sheltered port for small vessels located approximately 10km east of Airlie Beach. It is also a busy commercial harbour for ferries and charter boats of all types departing for the Whitsundays.
During the middle of the day, it is possible to go alongside the jetty, but check with the jetty master first. All moorings are private, but the anchorage is secure.
