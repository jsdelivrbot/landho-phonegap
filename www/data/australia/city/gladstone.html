Gladstone - Profile

Location

Gladstone lies about 100nm north of Bundaberg. It is a busy mineral exporting port. Situated at the southern extremity of the Great Barrier Reef, it is a useful base for exploring the islands and atolls off this coast.

Position 23°49′57″S, 151°14′40″E.

Clearance

Call Port control before entering the harbour and then berth at one of the International Yacht berths (G28 & H30) in the marina to await clearance.

Docking

This is one of Australia's busiest commercial ports and several large vessels can be manouvering at the same time. Call Port Control on VHF channel 13 before entering the harbour.

There is a marina here with both slips and moorings available. Anchoring is only permitted among the islands north of the river, about 2 miles from the marina.

Be aware that Gladstone is in the cyclone belt.
