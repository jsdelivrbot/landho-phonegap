Eden - Profile

Location

This city is on the northern shore of the magnificent harbour of Twofold Bay. It is a principal commercial port of the south coast of Australia as well as a major fishing town.

Originally this was a whaling town, but now it is a centre for whale-watching. Many species can be seen just off shore here during their annual migration (September to November).

This is a good point from which to explore the Gippsland Lakes, Australia's largest inland waterway. This is an area that is well worth visiting and possible even with a larger yacht. Visit www.nineofcups.com/NewSouthWales for more an interesting report.

Location 37° 04'21" S, 149° 54'27" E.

Docking

Snug Cove is not an all weather, safe harbour and yachts must not be left for extended periods here.

Visiting yachts can moor at the Mooring Jetty which is on the western side of Snug Cove. It is a concrete decked, wooden fended jetty, 150m long. It gas both water and electricity outlets.

There are no public mooring buoys available in Twofold Bay.

It is possible to anchor in Snug Bay.
