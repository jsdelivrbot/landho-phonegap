Gold Coast - Profile

Location

The Gold Coast area of Queensland includes the marina facilities of Southport and a number of marinas within the Broadwater - an area of protected waters between South Stradbrooke Island and the mainland. Two navigable channels connect this area with southern Moreton Bay, accessible to vessels of 2m draft and 22m height. The ocean is accessible through the Gold Coast Seaway, navigable in all except extreme swell conditions.

Position 28°1′S, 153°24′E.

Nearby coverage includes: Scarborough and northern Moreton Bay, Brisbane and the Brisbane River, Manly and southern Moreton Bay.

Docking

There are many marinas which are included in this section of coast which stretches from Gold Coast city itself, through Southport and Hope Island to Woongoolba which is opposite the southern end of North Stradbroke Island.

Much of the area is protected by off-lying island and provides many sheltered anchorages in addition to the numerous marinas.

Last updated November 2014.
