Carnarvon - Profile

Location

This harbour lies about 600nm north of Perth, at the mouth of the Gascoyne River. It is a centre for an inshore fishing fleet.

South of Carnarvon, is Denham and Sharks Bay. This area has a huge range of wildlife, not only sharks and whales, but manta rays and turtles, a prolific bird population, and at Monkey Mia, wild dolphins approach the shore to be hand fed.

Position 24°52′02″S, 113°39′40″E.

Clearance

Boarding Station for arrival - vessels awaiting clearance should tie up to a mooring on the starboard side in the North Eastern corner of the Boat Harbour.

Last updated February 2014.

Docking

There is an anchorage off the town, known as the Fascine. It is about 2 miles from the open sea and is approached via the Teggs channel, which has a minimum depth of 3 metres.

The Fascine, Carmarvon :© Dave Bris

Just south of the town is the Fishing Boat Harbour which provides mooring for both the fishing fleet and resident boats. There are two service jetties in the harbour, both of which have fuel berths.

There are a number of piles in the harbour for temporary moorings.

Last updated February 2014.
