Albany - Profile

Location

Albany lies on the far south western corner of Australia. It has a large natural harbour. It was once a whaling port but now acts as host to whale watching cruises. The town has some interesting historical buildings and is surrounded by some great beaches.

Position 35°01′22″S, 117°52′53″E.

Docking

There is still a commercial port here in Princess Royal Bay, which is dredged. A large part of the Bay is shallow (< 2m).

There is a new marina (with a min. depth of 4m) just north of the commercial port in Princess Royal Bay and a more sheltered one tucked inside the entrance to Oyster Bay.

Last updated February 2014.

Albany Waterfron Marina
Princess Royal Drive, Albany
Locate just west of the commercial dock on the north side of Princess Royal Bay. This is a new marina.
All facilities, fuel berth, laundry. On arrival, moor to the Town jetty and contact the Harbour Master.
Close to downtown Albany.
