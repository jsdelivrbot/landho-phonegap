Geraldton - Profile

Location

Geraldton is a major exporting port lying halfway down Australia's western coast. It is the departure point for visiting the protected Houtman Abrolhos Islands.

Position 28°46' S, 114°36' E.

Docking

This port is well known for its excellent fishing and the Port of Geraldton is a major exporter of crayfish. It is about 300 miles north of Perth.

The new marina encloses a large area within its overlapping breakwaters. There are three pontoons, one of which is for recreational boaters.

The Abrolhos Islands
Written notice must be given to the Department of Fisheries at the Dongara or Geraldton district offices (phone for further details) of boats intending to visit the islands between 15 July and 20 February the following year. Visiting boats must carry enough food, fresh water and fuel to last the length of the intended visit. Boats must be fitted with communication devices and waste-disposal storage and equipment. See the Visitors Guide for full details.

There are a limited number of public mooring available at the Islands.
