Mataura, Tubuai - Profile

Location

Tubuai is the administrative centre for the Austral islands, which is the southernmost group in French Polynesia. It lies midway between Rurutu, to the NW and Raivavae to the SE and some 500 miles south of Tahiti.

Clearance

It is NO LONGER compulsory for all yachts visiting French Polynesia to visit Papeete Customs and Immigration to complete the clearing-in process.

Yachts can now clear in or out of FP with the “gendarmes” offices (French Police) at the ports of entry in the other islands.

The Gendarmerie Nationale in Tubuai can deal with Immigration and Customs formalities.

For full details on clearance into and out of FP go to French Polynesia Formalities.

Last updated July 2014.

Docking

The main pass is well marked and presents no danger.

The anchorage in front of the townhouse (mentionned in cruising guides) is not the best anchorage. It is better to anchor in the protection of the small dock, about 1 mile away, even if you may have to move your boat a little to let the Tahiti ferry dock there.

There is also reported to be an anchorage on the southern side of the island.

Last updated January 2011.
