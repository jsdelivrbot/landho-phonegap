Port Ghalib - Profile

Location

Port Ghalib is on the southern part of Egypt's Red Sea coast, 85 km from Kusier and 60 km from Marsa Alam.

The Port Ghalib Marina is an artificial harbour, part of a tourist resort. Located in a quiet desert-looking landscape, it opened in 2002 and can accommodate up to 1000 yachts. Now part of the Camper & Nicholsons group, facilities are greatly improved with Harbor Master, Customs and Immigration offices on site and a fully equipped repair and fueling facility.

Position 25°32'N, 34°38'E (entrance channel)

Clearance

Port Ghalib is an official port of entry to Egypt.

See Egypt Formalities for full details on clearing into and out of Egypt.

Call port control on VHF Ch:10 or 16, preferably 2 hours before arrival, otherwise well before reaching the entrance channel.

This is an easy place to clear in to the country and get visas as all entry formalities can be completed at the marina office without the need to hire an agent or travel to any other place.

The Marina provides clearance formalities at these costs:

Government fee - formalities for foreign yachts up to 18 metres (valid 1 mth): USD 30 (USD 50 for larger yachts);

Immigration stamps - Egyptian visa (per person): USD 25

Processing fee (marina acts as agent): USD 80 per yacht

Berthing up to 8 hours in the marina (to complete formalities): USD 13 (15 m LOA).

Note:
Fees are payable only once per visit to Egypt at at any licensed port of entry. The marina will issue a receipt which should be shown at subsequent ports or marinas so as not to have to pay additional fees for formalities.
This applies to tourist marinas (such as Port Ghalib, Abu Tig Marina, Wadi Dome, Hurghada Marina). It does not apply to Sharm El Sheikh, where you will be liable for further fees.

Yachts owners should only fill a Customs declaration form and present it to the marina management for processing.

Docking

Entrance to the channel is at 25°32.1'N  034°38.6'E. The channel is well marked with buoys on both port and starboard sides, free of danger with depths in excess of 25m. The channel to the marina runs E/W through the reef.

A prior reservation is required before entering the marina. Entrance is permitted after sunset.

Berthing is stern-to the quay with electricity and water on the quay. Slipway service for boats up to 20m. Very limited repair services available.
