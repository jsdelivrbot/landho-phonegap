Basse Terre - Profile

Location

Basse Terre is the capital of Guadeloupe and is on the western side at the southern tip of the island of the same name.

Position 15° 58'55"N, 61° 43'W (marina entrance).

Clearance

There is a Customs office in town, however it is easier to use the clearance computer at the marina.

The whole of the northern side of the Marina is full of restaurants and bars on the waterfront. The Barracuda, in the middle, has a computer for clearance.

Last updated March 2015.

Docking

The marina is approx. a mile south of the town. Whilst it does not have space to accommodate catamarans, there is a pontoon at the entrance to the marina reserved for visiting yachts, which usually has space for a few nights. There is also a good fuel dock.

It is possible to anchor just north of the entrance. The holding here is good, but take care to remain on the shallower bank as the water around it is very deep.

Around the marina is a bakery, butcher, vegetable shop, small chandlery, mini-mart as well as a plethora of bars. There is a nice little beach behind the marina. A half hour walk along the promenade takes you into town with larger supermarkets for full provisioning.

Last updated March 2015.
