Musket Cove - Profile

Location

The legendary Musket Cove Fiji Regatta is staged here at the beginning of September every year for six days. It is open to all, cruisers and racers, and every yacht that sails to Musket Cove becomes a member of the Musket Cove Yacht Club.

Clearance

No port of entry (nearest is Lautoka) except at the end of the Musket Cove Fiji Regatta Week (on Malolo Lailai Island in the Mamanucas) in September, when resort owner, Dick Smith, arranges for customs and immigration to come to the island for outward clearance. Contact the resort office for details [see below].

Docking

Marina berths and moorings are available or you can drop anchor in the bay.

Yacht Clubs

Musket Cove Yacht Club

Tel:+679 6662215 Fax:+679 6662633 ,VHF Channel 68 and 87 for Yasawa and Viti Levu areas

Those who arrive by yacht can become lifelong members of the Musket Cove Yacht Club on payment of one Fiji dollar (captain) and 5$ (crew). Membership gives the privilege to use the resort facilities (providing proper conduct) and also offer deep discount on the ferry service. Having the yacht's name carved into one of the overhead beams is no longer done as the beam is full. The club organises the Fiji Regatta Week in September every year. Showers, toilets, water, garbage bins, mail, telephone, fax, and e-mail at the office. There is a dinghy dock and a small number of berths are available on the club pontoon, but need to be booked in advance.
