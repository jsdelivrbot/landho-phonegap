Agadir - Profile

Location

A useful port on the Atlantic coast of Morocco, being just 220 NM from the Canaries. This is a reasonable stopping place with good provisioning in town and officials are reported to be friendly and efficient. Prices are very reasonable.

It is a good place from which to visit Marrakech and southern Morocco.

Position 30° 25.11'N,09° 36.59' W.

Clearance

Entry procedures here can take time, in particular clearing out. Customs, Immigration and police are officially on duty 24 hours at the marina, however cruisers have reported that in fact some of the authorities only work during daylight hours, and opening times be "flexible".

It's likely the authorities will come on board as soon you are are berthed (although note that Customs don't work on Saturdays and Sundays). They are friendly, efficient and require no more paperwork than elsewhere on the Moroccan coast.

Papers must be originals and not copies.

The return of stamped passports can cause a delay as the stamp is kept in the main port.

On departure, you will not be permitted to clear out the night before and depart at first light, you will have to wait until the offices open in the morning.

Note: Movement in this port is restricted. If you wish to go for a day-sail and anchor elsewhere, you will need to clear out, and clear back in again on return.

Last updated January 2016.

Docking

The entrance to the marina is rather "hidden" and is towards the bathing beach, NOT through the main harbour. Finding the entrance is difficult at night until you are adjacent and it opens up.

There is reported to be plenty of space in the marina.

Alternatively, boats can anchor off the yacht club in the NE of the Grand Bassin, which welcomes visitors. The dinghy can be left at the yacht club pontoon. Water can be obtained at the same dock, which has sufficient depth at high tide. Unfortunately, the harbour tends to be very dirty and polluted.

Last updated November 2015.

This 300 slip marina with floating pontoons, is located south of the commercial port. Full facility marina including clearance service, fuel, showers, WiFi (at marina office). ATM and restaurant/cafes situated on edge of marina. Good security. Note - only half the berths have power and water. Boatyard with 500 ton travel lift. Staff reported to be friendly.
