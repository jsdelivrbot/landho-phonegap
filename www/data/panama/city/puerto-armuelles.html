Puerto Armuelles - Profile

Location

Located on the eastern side of the long peninsula close to the border with Costa Rica. This port is about 70 NM west of Boca Chica.

This is a commercial port for banana export and tankers transferring crude oil etc. to the Caribbean via the Transpanama oil pipeline. There are no facilities for yachts.

As this is a not a Port of Entry frequented by yachts, in the main clearance charges are higher than those found elsewhere.

Position 8°17.0'N,82°52.0'W.

Clearance

Read Panama Formalities for detailed information on procedures for clearing into and out of Panama.

Clearing In

If you stop here overnight having not yet cleared into Panama, the authorities will insist you check in here. As mentioned above, fees are high and it is reported to be a very slow process. Be sure to arrive early as anything after 15h30 is overtime.

Note: Some cruisers clearing in here have reported that they were not able to get the Mariners visa here, and had to go to Panama City to get it.

Clearing Out
Clearing out of Panama here however is reported to be simple and straightforward. The port captain, Aduana and health will come out in a panga. They will then bring you in to process paperwork and immigration as well (cost $15 payable to the panga driver).

It is not recommended to take your own dinghy ashore as the pier is rough with surge and will wreck your inflatable.

Last updated August 2014.

Docking

There is a small marina here and it is reported to have water and fuel.

Yachts can anchor close to the Pier.

The Port Captain can assist with getting hold of fuel by jerry cans.
