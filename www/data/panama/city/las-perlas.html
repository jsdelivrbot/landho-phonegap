Las Perlas - Profile

Location

Located some 35 miles from Panama City, Las Perlas (Pearl Islands) are a popular stopover for yachts en route to the Galapagos. Only a handful of the archipelago’s 200-plus islands are inhabited.

Clearance

If one intends to stop at Las Perlas after clearing out from Panama on the mainland, this should be mentioned on the clearance paper by the relevant officer.

Docking

There are plenty of anchorages to choose from, mostly deserted, though one should watch out for the 5 metre tidal range especially when landing the dinghy on the beach.

Contadora Island is one of the larger islands and is home to a small, elegant resort, with a small airport, hotels, restaurants, a couple of shops and a service station.

Security Note: Be vigilant, an outboard motor was stolen from a dinghy on davits whilst the owners were on board here in February 2013.
