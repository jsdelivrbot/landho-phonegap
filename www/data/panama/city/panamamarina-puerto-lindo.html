Panamamarina & Puerto Lindo - Profile

Location

Panamamarina is located towards Isla Grande and 2nm west of Isla Linton (Puerto Lindo). It tends to be used by cruisers who are leaving their boat for the season or to travel to other locations in Central or South America.

Position 09°36´90"N 79°36´70"W (entrance channel)

Clearance

Read Panama Formalities for detailed information on procedures for clearing into and out of Panama.

El Porvenir AMP (Port Captain from the San Blas) has relocated to Linton Bay Marina and is open every day, included Saturday and Sunday. Saturday, Sundays and Holidays there is an extra $ 20,00 Charge. Hours are 8am to 4pm. The office is in the same building as the marina office, just before the entrance to the docks.

Costs are $ 12,00 for a Zarpe to exit Panama and $ 195,00 to enter, including a Cruising Permit (payable in cash).

Important Note: There is no bank in Linton Bay (Puerto Lindo) or in Portobelo, but there are banks and ATMs in Sabanitas, which is on the way to Colon or Panama city.

There is currently no Immigration here - however there should be very soon. The closest is in Portobelo (a short inexpensive bus ride away) or Colon/Panama City.

The stamp given on arrival is good for 72 hours, giving you enough time to visit Immigration for your visa.

Last updated June 18, 2016.

Docking

The entrance channel to the Panamarina (see photo above) is equipped with green (starboard) and red (port) buoys and the first 4 are fitted with a flag for better visibility from offshore.

Once inside the anchorage, two stand-by mooring buoys (painted white) can be found in the middle. Call the marina on VHF 69 and a tender will come and assist. Whilst this is called a marina, there are no docks, just mooring buoys.

The new marina at Puerto Lindo (photo above) is proving to be very popular.

A new marina (May 2015). Now operational with approximately 12 slips available for visitors. Limited water and internet access. Currently no electricity, although there is a major power line upgrade in progress from Sabanitas through to Isla Grande that will eventually allow full power in the marina. Fuel available by jerry can.
There is a boatyard with a dry dock and a 160 ton travel hoist which is now operating, however currently no boat stands available. Marina Manager's name is Adam Baite. Call Adam by phone with regard to bookings, prices and any other information you might need.

Moorings buoys for 28 monohulls and 27 multihulls. Services include 24h/day security, laundry, showers (cold) and toilets, restaurant, workshop, water fill-up, dinghy dock, weekly fill-up of gas bottles, garbage disposal. Haulout trailer (see comments below). Following a number of thefts from boats on buoys in October 2015, the marina have increased the number of guards vigilant ashore and on the water 24/7. They have also installed 32 surveillance cameras with alarms and automatic sensor spots.
