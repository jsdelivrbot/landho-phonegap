Denmark - General Info

Time Zone

UTC +1, summer time UTC +2 from end of March to end of October.

Electrical Supply

Voltage: 230 V

Frequency: 50 Hz

Plug types C & K

Denmark's connectors have slight differences from those used elsewhere. While pin diameter and spacing is standard, outlets may have different housing depths which could interfere with standard adaptors
One report says this is due to "childproofing." Also, Plug C fits into K-type outlets (but not vice versa).

Money

Danish krone (Dkr) of 100 ore.

Credit cards are widely accpted.

Communications

International Dialing Code for Denmark is +45

Telephones - the telephone system is excellent. Public telephone boxes accepting credit cards and prepayment cards (available from shops and kiosks) are widely available. It is quite difficult to find public telephone boxes that accept coints.

The coverage is good for GSM mobile phones authorised for "roaming".

Fax - generally available at marina offices and hotels.

Navtex - provides English language broadcasts of weather forecasts, navigational warnings etc. is worth having particularly if cruising beyond Denmark to other Baltic destinations.

Stockholm radio (stations D and J) can usually be received in Denmark. (See Admiralty publication NP 291 for transmission times and other details).

Internet connections are available in most public libraries.

Transport

Public transport is good and cheap.

Comprehensive and reliable network of comfortable bus services.

Denmark's excellent rail network is connected to the UK via the Channel Tunnel and the Great Belt Bridge.

Copenhagen Airport is the major "hub" airport.

Diplomatic Missions

Foreign Embassies in Denmark

Emergencies

Dial 112 for Police, ambulance, fire department
(From public booths calls are free)

Dial 70 13 00 41 Doctor (between 8:00 am and 4:00 pm)

Dial 35 38 02 51 Dentist

Distress and urgency signals are broadcast on VHF Ch 16 and 70 in the usual way. Rescue is co-ordinated by the Danish Maritime Rescue Co-ordination Centre. The lifeboat service is provided by a govenment agency, the Royal Danish Administration of Navigation and Hydrography. (January 2009).

Publications

IMRAY AND ADLARD COLES PILOT BOOKS are available at a discounted price for Noonsite.com users via World Cruising Club.

Danish charts are produced by the Royal Danish Administration of Navigation and Hydrography in special editions for yachts. It is essential to have good charts for the smaller channels.

Information sheets and other brochures for cruising may be obtained from the Danish Tourist Board, 55 Sloane Street, London SW1X 9SY - Tel 0171 259 5958 or www.visitdenmark.com

Several local tourist associations publish leaflets for yachtsmen, which are available from Danish Tourist Association offices.
