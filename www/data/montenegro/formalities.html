Montenegro - Formalities

Clearance

Yachts approaching Montenegro coast should expect to be intercepted by naval craft patrolling the area.

VHF Channel 16 should be monitored permanently and, if contacted by a naval vessel, the radio operator of the yacht will be expected to give the following information: name of yacht, international callsign, flag, port of registration, last port of call, destination. The name of the owner and the general description of the cargo is sometimes also requested.

In all cases yachts must proceed to the nearest Port of Entry and anounce their arrival to the Harbour Master at either Bar or Kotor through the Coast Radio Station ("Barradio") using VHF channel 16. Do not go to any other  port prior to clearing in at either of the above 2 ports, or you will be fined.

Yachts must purchase a cruising permit (a "vignette"), valid for 1 week, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months or one year. This has to be purchased at the Harbour Master's office. See fees below for details. There is a fee for clearance and allow half a day for formalities.

Note: Visiting yachts, for whatever reason (fuel, water etc.) will be required to clear-in and will be obliged to pay for a cruising permit.

Immigration

All visitors require a valid passport except those of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Slovenia, Croatia and Macedonia who only require an ID card. EU citizen who enter the country with only an I.D. card may stay for 30 days only.

Note: With the current refugee crisis, it is advisable that everyone has a valid passport. (Jan 2016)

Ensure your passport is stamped otherwise you may face difficulties when leaving.

No visas are needed for members of the European Union, Former Yugoslav Republics (Slovenia, Croatia, Bosnia and Herzegovina and Macedonia), Albania, Bulgaria, Romania, Russian Federation, Ukraine, Belorussia, the United States of America, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Norway, Israel, Rep. of Korea, Japan, Singapore and Switzerland for stays of up to 90 days.

Citizens of Albania, Russian Federation and Ukraine may stay for up to 30 days without a visa.

Other nationalities require visas, which cannot be obtained on arrival. There are consulates in Bucharest, Sofia, Athens and Tirana.

Last updated January 2016.

Customs

It is essential to declare all hard currency to avoid it being confiscated on departure.

A firearms certificate must be held if there are any such wepons on board, including a Very pistol.

Last updated January 2016.

Health

Only bottled water should be used for drinking.

The health system in Montenegro can suffer from a shortage of medicines and other essentials. Medical staff do not always speak English.

Documents

Documents required are;
Boat registration document, 3rd party insurance document, crusing permit, skipper's certificate of competence, crew list (with name, nationality, passport no., date and place of birth, position on board), list of boat equipment (nav. aids etc), list of other items such as cameras, cell phone, diving equipment, outboard motors etc.

Note that without the correct documents you will be denied entry into the country, however may be permitted to refuel and re-provision whilst on the Customs berth.

Fees

Cruising permits (vignettes) can be purchased at the Harbour Master's office of the Port of Entry. Costs seem to be changed annually .

If you are planning on keeping your boat in a marina or on the hard and not moving, a cruising permit is not required.

Current Cruising Permit fees (quoted in Euros by the Maritime Safety Department of Montenegro) can be calculated at http://vinjete.luckakapetanija.me

For example: A vignette for one week for a 17m boat costs 38 Euros.
