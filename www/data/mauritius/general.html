Mauritius - General Info

Time Zone

UTC +4

Electrical Supply

Voltage: 230 V

Frequency: 50 Hz

Money

Mauritian rupee (MRS) of 100 cents. Only MRS 350 may be exported.

Communications

The international phone code for Mauritius is +230

The whole island is covered by a GSM mobile network.

Mobile phone: Roaming agreements with international mobile phone companies are available and coverage extends over the entire island. Local mobile calls are the same as the price of landline calls, so it’s worth buying a local SIM card for your handset if you plan to make more than a few phone calls on the island.

Internet: There are Internet cafés in both main towns and tourist centres such as Grand Baie and Port Louis. Most hotels offer wireless Internet access. Internet cafes are widely available.

Parts and equipment which are not available locally may be ordered and quickly shipped from the US or Europe via FedEx, UPS, or DHL or other carriers. Yachts in transit are not subject to the 15% VAT but customs clearance and delivery fees may add up to more than that amount. Talk to the courier company before your parts arrive to decide whether you wish to pay the vat or the customs clearance fees.

Transport

Car hire is simple but not cheap. Traffic is on the whole quite heavy, so allow plenty of time to get anywhere. Mauritians use their horns liberally!

Emergencies

Police (emergency)- Dial 999 or 112
Fire Service - Dial 995 or 115
Medical emergency service - Dial 999 or 114
Coastguards - Dial 212 27 47
Police headquaters in Port Louis - Dial(230) 208 12 12 .

By Rod Heikell
Publisher: Imray,Laurie,Norie & Wilson Ltd (2nd edition 2007)
ISBN-13: 9780852889725
General cruising guide covering the routes from Europe to Australia and points in between, Indian Ocean Cruising Guide covers all the usual points of interest for cruising yachtsmen, from history to climate, weather patterns, formalities, route planning and so on. The general sections are followed by a country-by-country round up in which key ports are described in detail with the aid of harbour plans and photographs, with additional information on Malaysia and Thailand, the Seychelles and Chagos archipelago. This second edition has been thoroughly revised and contains a lot of additional information on Malaysia and Thailand, the Seychelles and the Chagos archipelago.
