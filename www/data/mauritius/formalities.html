Mauritius - Formalities

Clearance

Port Louis is the only official Port of Entry on Mauritius. Detailed information about arrival and clearance procedures in Port Louis can be found on the Port Louis port pages.

It is necessary to clear in here even if you have previously cleared in at Port Mathurin. No agent is required.

Rodrigues Island is part of Mauritius, yet they have their own unique rules and fees. See Port Mathurin, the Port of Entry for Rodrigues, for clearance information.

St Brandon (Cargados Carajos Shoals) and Agalega should not be visited without prior permission in writing from the Ministry of External Affairs & Emigration in Port Louis, except in an emergency.

Although not an official port of entry, yachts have been allowed to stay in Grand Baie and leave from there after clearing out in Port Louis. There does not seem to be a strict rule about this and a yacht wishing to clear out may be required to return to Port Louis.

To clear out of Mauritius, it is necessary to buy a Customs clearance form from the chamber of commerce in Port Louis. Stamps must then be bought at the post office to be affixed on the form by Customs.

Initial immigration clearance is either 1, 2 or 3 months but visa extensions of up to 180 days are usually granted without any problem.

The maximum stay is for the boat is 6 months in any 12 but stays of more than 90 days require a bond of 10% of the value of the boat.

Officials in Mauritius are courteous and professional, will speak English as well as French and Creole, and they will not ask for any fees or other payments.

Be sure to get a copy of your clearance papers because it may be required by coast guard boarding parties if you visit other ports in Mauritius.

The latest official information can be found at Mauritius/VisitingSmallCraft

Note: Due to an outbreak of Foot and Mouth disease in Mauritius, all meat and dairy products on board are being confiscated on arrival at Reunion Island. This may also be applied in other countries.

Immigration

Passports must be valid for the length of your stay.

Travellers who arrive and depart with the same yacht, do not require a visa.

Citizens of most countries will be given a 30, 60 or 90 day visa on arrival.

Nationals of several countries including Afghanistan, Bangladesh, Indonesia, Iran, Iraq, N. Korea, Libya, Mali, Pakistan, Philippines, Somalia, Sri Lanka, Sudan, South Sudan, Syria, Taiwan, Vietnam and Yemen must obtain a visa before travelling to Mauritius, even in transit.

Nationals of Algeria, Nigeria, Myanmar (Burma), Madagascar and Comoros are given a two week visa only.

See Mauritius Passport Site for full details.

If a visa is required, it may be obtained abroad from Mauritius High Commissions or embassies and where these do not exist, from British embassies.

Visa extensions can be obtained by applying to the Immigration office at Sterling House, a short distance from the waterfront in downtown Port Louis. Several forms must be completed and documents provided, so it is worth going there a day or two before to get the list of what is needed. Visa extensions of up to 180 days for owners and crew are commonly approved.

You can stay a maximum of 6 months, during any 12 month period, in Rodrigues, Saint Brandon, AND Mauritius (combined). If you want to stay longer than 6 months you must request an "exemption" from the Immigration Department. Your exception request must be in writing and submitted near your 50th day of visitation. Your request will be sent to the Prime Minister in Mauritius for consideration. There are 13 legal exemption categories for extending your stay.

Leaving your boat in Mauritius:-

The yacht's skipper (not the crew) has to get a letter from the visa section in the Immigration main office in Port Louis, permitting him to fly out. To get this letter (which one has to show to passport control at the airport) you have to employ someone who will be responsible for the boat while you are away. You have to bring a letter from that skipper as well as a copy of his skipper's certificate and national I.D. Also needed, are copies of the first page of your passport, the page with the entry stamp, a copy of the health check document, your agreement with the Caudan marina, (or any other place you plan to leave the boat) plus the receipt for the payment to it. Lastly, a copy of your confirmed airline ticket out and back again at specific dates. It is possible that the airport Immigration officers may not enforce this rule but a possible penalty could be losing your flight.

One important point to note is that you need the letter from Immigration when you clear out of the country, but you may well need some documentation when you check in at the airport on your return to prove that you have a yacht (e.g. boat registration and a copy of the letter from the guardianage company).

Customs

Under most circumstances yachts are permitted to stay in Mauritius/Rodrigues waters for a maximum of 90 days. A bond worth 10% of the value of the yacht is required for boats staying longer and must be presented at their offices (New Customs House in the Free Port area).

No firearms are allowed, and this includes spear guns. Declare your firearms and spear guns and the Mauritius Customs will hold them for the duration of your stay in Mauritius. You will be given a receipt and told to call for them 24 hours prior to departure, so that the weapons can be delivered to the waterfront office for you to collect when you check out.

Underwater fishing is prohibited.

It is prohibited to import fresh fruits from Asia east of 60°E (including India and Pakistan), plants, fresh vegetables and sugar cane.

Any personal medicinal drugs should be covered by a prescription. Scheduled drugs, such as tranquillisers, morphine and other strong painkillers require by law, authorisation before import.

No wild protected medicinal plants, fresh or dried, may be exported.

Health

Update August 2016: An outbreak of Foot and Mouth disease in Reunion and Rodrigues has resulted in strict food import restrictions. See this Noonsite report
Any meat or dairy products may be destroyed.

There will be a Health Inspection on arrival, for which there is a charge.

A yellow fever inoculation is required if coming from an infected area.

There is a very low risk of Malaria in Mauritius. If you have travelled from a country where malaria is common, the Ministry of Health may ask you for a blood sample.

Take precautions against mosquito bites, as there have been several cases of the Chikungunya virus, which is spread by mosquito bites. This is more common from October to May.

Travellers should stick to bottled water.

Good private healthcare in Mauritius is available, although this can be costly if you are not insured. More complex cases could require evacuation to Reunion or South Africa. Medical care is free in public hospitals.

Stonefish stings are uncommon but can in some cases be fatal. You should obtain urgent medical attention if stung.

Fees

Monday to Friday (normal business hours):

Health: 647 MR (approx. $18 USD)
All other agencies are free.

Saturday, Sunday, and Holidays (overtime charges):

Health: Fees as above plus approx. $30 USD

Customs, after 12:00 Saturday: $45 USD
Customs, Sundays and holidays: $55 USD

Note: Some officials will expect a "consideration" of about $8 USD for clearing out prior to 0830 on a weekday.

Restrictions

The use of harpoons for fishing is forbidden as is any underwater fishing.

Pets

For Mauritius - all animals need:-

(a) Import Permit from Ministry of Agriculture & Natural Resources & the Environment, obtained in advance.

(b) Health certificate from country of origin.

They must be declared to Customs on arrival and landing is allowed if (a) and (b) agree, otherwise animals must remain on board.

Quarantine for dogs and cats is six months, for birds it is three weeks. Dogs and cats from areas where rabies has occurred in the last 12 months are prohibited.

Further information from Ministry of Agriculture Division of Veterinary Services, Reduit, Mauritius - Tel 00(230) 454 1016-17/637 Fax 00(230)240 4479 or www.mauritius.net.

Rodrigues

Pets are welcomed at Rodrigues, with no prior paperwork. During the clearance procedure, state that you have a pet on board and the officials will contact the local veterinarian for a brief health assessment. There is no charge for the veterinarian visit to your yacht.
