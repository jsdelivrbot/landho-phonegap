Eregli - Profile

Location

This is a large commercial and naval harbour on the Black Sea coast and is home to Turkey's largest steel plant. Consequently it is a well developed, lively coastal town with a bustling waterfront and well tended public spaces.

Position: 41º16'N, 31º24'E.

Clearance

This is a Port of Entry.

For the full details on Turkey Formalities see Noonsite/Turkey/Formalities page.

An agent is now required to check in and out of Turkey here.

Last updated February 2016.

Docking

Yachts should use the north harbour. Berth alongside on the west side of the central pier.

In strong winds from any Westerly direction mooring alongside the western pier might be untenable, even anchoring in the designated area in the Western part of the harbour is uncomfortable. A better option is to try and anchor in the fishing harbour on the east side of the central pier if there is room, good shelter and holding.

The area is very popular with local fisherman in the late afternoon and early evening when the quay is very crowded. It is advisable to be on board at that time to stop them from fishing off your boat.

The shipyards near the harbour are a good place for most repairs.

Last updated June 2011.
