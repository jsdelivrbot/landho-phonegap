Sinop - Profile

Location

This historic fishing port often forms the eastern limit for many cruising yachts as it provides a useful point from which to cross to the Crimean Peninsula. The town centre is to a large extent surrounded by a wall (often fallen into ruins or taken down) that at some gates dates back some 2500 years.

Position: 42º 01'N, 35º 08'E.

Clearance

This is a Port of Entry.

If this is your first Turkish port a new transit log is an advantage.

Customs and Immigration are on the commercial quay. An agent is not required.

Docking

This is predominantly a fishing harbour and space is difficult for visiting yachts. Boats reported damaged by careless fishing vessels.

Berth in the Western harbour; either ask permission to go alongside a fishing boat or follow instructions to med-moor to the outer quay.

There is water on the quay and the town has most facilities.
