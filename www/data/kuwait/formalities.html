Kuwait - Formalities

Clearance

Call Port Control on VHF channel 16 on approach and follow instructions.

All vessels must enter Kuwait waters between the Jabriya buoy to the north and the Ahmadiya buoy to the south, with any attempts to enter the country's territorial waters to the north or south of this area being forbidden and subject to prosecution.

Vessels must go directly to the Oaha Buoy (29.16.00N - 48.26.00E) before proceeding in a straight line towards the KCG patrol vessels stationed in the area between Ras Al-Ard and Failaka Island.

Last updated August 2015.

Immigration

Passports must be valid for 6 months on entry.

Visitors arriving by sea require a visa in advance. If arriving by air, then nationals of many countries are granted a visa on arrival. See www.dgca.gov.kw/en/travellers/traveller-information

Visas are usually valid for one month.

Israeli passport holders and those with evidence of a visit to Israel will be refused entry, including those with a Jordanian or Egyptian entry stamp in their passport, issued by an office bordering Israel.

Last updated August 2015.

Customs

Firearms, alcohol, pork products, unsealed milk products, unsealed salty fish, unsealed olives and pickles, food prepared abroad, fresh vegetables, shellfish and its products, fresh figs, mineral water and goods of Israeli origin are prohibited imports.

Videos, DVDs and books are subject to censorship depending on the content.

Alcohol is prohibited in Kuwait.

A permit from the Ministry of Information must be obtained to take photographs.

Last updated August 2015.

Health

Hepatitis B is endemic in the region. Inocculations for Hepatitis A is advised.

The water is treated and normally safe to drink as is milk and milk products.

Restrictions

The Iraq-Kuwait border area is dangerous and must be avoided. There are still unexploded mines along the coast and in the sea, although mine clearance is gradually making the area safe.

Local Customs

This is a Muslim country and Islamic law must be respected. Sensitivity regarding standards of dress should be observed.  Women should dress conservatively and men must not wear shorts or go shirtless in public. During the month of Ramadan, one should not eat, drink or smoke in public.
