Location

Scarborough is the principal town on Tobago and lies towards the western end of the island's southern coast. If you want more peace and quiet, then Charlotteville would be a better place to clear in.

Clearance

See Trinidad & Tobago Formalities for complete clearance information.

Anchor in the designated area to go ashore to clear in. It may also be possible to berth at the Government Dock.

It is also possible to anchor in Store Bay, on the south west corner of Tobago, and then take a bus or taxi to Scarborough to clear in.

The Immigration office is at the cruise ship terminal. Customs are available 24/7 and can contact an Immigration officer for you if arriving outside office hours (08:00-16:00 Mon-Fri).

Last updated January 2013.

Docking

Note that the holding in the harbour is poor so ensure you anchor is well set before leaving the boat.

Only basic facilities are available. There is a water tap (potable) on the pier in town (although a filter is recommended), and it is possible for a yacht to go alongside if the local boats are out.

Last updated May 2014.
