Cascais - Profile

Location

This was originally a quaint fishing port, but is now a colourful, elegant town just 10nm west of Lisbon. The daily fish auction, its colourful boats and excellent fish restaurants are a present day reminder that it is still an active fishing port.

Position 38°41.6'N, 09°24.8'W.

Clearance

All officials (Capitania, Customs and Guarda Nacional Republicana) are situated in the marina.

Berth at the hammer-head immediately to sta'bd at the entrance (reception pontoon) to complete clearance.

Last updated January 2016.

Docking

The marina has all-tide access and is well set up for hosting major sailing events. Whilst service and facilities are excellent, prices are high and many cruisers feel that the marina is trying to appeal more to the superyacht market. On arrival, berth at the reception pontoon to be allocated a berth. The visitors' berths appear to be exposed to westerlies, but the substantial breakwater provides ample protection.

Anchorages

There are good anchorages in the bay north of the port which is sheltered from the prevailing northerlies but is open to the south and east.

A modern marina with all facilities, fuel, laundry, ATM, free WiFi, internet, chandlery, mail pickup, bars, restaurants, provisions. Boatyard with 70 ton boat hoist, 3 ton crane, shore storage, repairs (including sails).
Access to the marina is available at all states of tide through a well-protected harbour entrance. Dredged to 8.5m. Access to pontoons is controlled by electronic security cards.
