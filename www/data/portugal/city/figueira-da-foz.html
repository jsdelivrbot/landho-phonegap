Figueira da Foz - Profile

Location

A lively resort town on Portugal's west coast, midway between Lisbon and Porto. It is located at the mouth of the Mondego river.

Position 40º 08' 32" N, 08º 52' 38"W (river entrance).

Clearance

No longer necessary for EU registered yachts to visit the GNR-Brigade Fiscal and Policia Maritima unless arriving outside office hours. However skippers of non-EU registered yachts or with non-EU nationals on board should report to the officials, located in a new building in the centre of the marina.

Last updated December 2015.

Docking

If a large sea is running at low tide this entrance can be difficult. Conditions over the bar at the entrance are broadcast on VHF channel 11 (Maritime Authority). The river entrance has been dredged to about seven metres, but one should aim to cross the bar during a rising tide.

In case of poor visibility, it is advisable to contact Figueira Pilots on channel 14 or the the Maritime Authority on channel 16, before entering.

The marina is on the north side of the entrance, a short distance up the river in the middle of the city. Maximum depth is 3m.

Note that there is frequently a strong current across the marina entrance, and sometimes there may be nets strung across the river.

Visitors should berth at the pontoons in front of the reception building (centre of the marina). Beware of cross currents only a few meters from the eastern-most (largest) pontoons in the main marina when approaching.

The marina access gates at Marina Figueira da Foz are opened with the use of a proximity chip. These chips are available for registered berth holders and visitors.

New pontoons have been installed in the eastern part of the marina and all the area to the west is now a huge parking lot.

Last updated December 2015.
