Portugal - General Info

Time Zone

UTC, Summer time UTC +1 from the end of March to the end of October.

Electrical Supply

YACHTING ESSENTIALS

Electricity

Voltage: 220/110 Volts AC
Frequency: 50 Hz
Round two-pin plugs

Gas/Propane/Butane

It is illegal to re-fill cooking gas bottles in Portugal.

Camping Gaz bottles can be exchanged at marine supply stores.

Opening Hours

Shop hours are generally from 09:00 to 12:00 and 14:00 to 18:00. Supermarkets usually 10:00 to 20:00 daily.

Banking hours: 08:30 to 15:00.

Money

Euro

Credit cards are widely accepted.

ATMs in all towns.

Communications

International Dialing Code for Portugal is +351.

All Portuguese marinas listen on VHF Channel 9.

The prepaid SIM card for Portugal with a world cell phone is the most convenient and economical solution while in Portugal. All incoming calls while in Portugal are FREE, regardless of where they originate. Pay the local rate for local calls and use your cellular phone in Portugal like you do at home.Your Portugal SIM Card (TMN) will require a SIM-unlocked GSM 900 / 1800 compatible international cell phone.

Post Offices
Opening hours: Workdays 08:30-12:30 + 14:00-18:30
Recognised by the sign Correlo or CTT

Public Transport

Portugal's hydrographical office (Instituto Hidrográfico) which is run by the Navy is an extremely professional organisation, they produce excellent charts and much of the information on them is in English. They have a very good web site (www.hidrografico.pt) which although only in Portuguese is very easy to understand. Their "Notices to Mariners" (Avisos aos Navegantes) giving information on changes to buoyage and lights etc. is updated every day on the web site.
