Bonbonon (Negros Island) - Profile

Location

Tambobo Bay is at the southern tip of Negros Oriental and behind it the cruisers' haven of Bonbonon. This beautiful deep cove with a “dog leg” entrance makes it fully enclosed, very comfortable and calm in most weather.

The bay is surrounded by mangroves and tall green jungle growth and in the background, the tall peaks of two mountains appear, though they are often covered in cloud.

Bonbonon is one of the principal hurricane holes in the Philippines being far enough south to keep it in the safer portion of the island group and fairly shallow with good holding in mud.

The mouth of the river is shallow so it is recommended to enter as close to high tide as possible. The approach to the entrance may be difficult to spot but most people use a track on openCPN or Maxsea to get in to the harbour, which probably helps. The reef on the approach to Bonbonon is now buoyed with small white drums.

Docking

Moorings are available from Boy and Arlene's Cafe, phone 09173612437 (who do a Friday buffet and simple meals during the week), or from Emma (who has a buffet too on Wednesday). Both are situated on the waterside. Arlene and Boy have a dinghy dock which they are happy for cruisers to use.

Good potable water is available free at the pump opposite Emmas.

Anchoring is in mud, good holding in about 5-10 metres. This is one of the safest anchorages in the Philippines, but can be a little crowded at times, with some yachts staying indefinitely.

Fuel is not available except if you take your jerrycans to Tambobo, the village at the entrance to Bonbonon. See the fuel section for more details.

Basic stocking up can be done in Siaton or Zambuguita (approx. 14km away), but for more selection and things like yogurt, good cheeses and other delicacies, it is necessary to go to Dumaguete, about 30 km away.
