Puerto Galera (Mindoro) - Profile

Location

Puerto Galera is at the NW tip of the province of Oriental Mindoro. It is located at the SW end of the Isla Verde Passage, about 130 kilometers south of Manila. It's a popular tourist destination with many beautiful "pocket" beaches and dive spots and stunning mountain scenery.

Be cautious if stopping on your way to Puerto Galero. A popular bad weather shelter before rounding the Cape on the NW point of Mindoro is Paluan Bay. A cruising couple were boarded by armed men and robbed at gunpoint here in February 2013 (see report adjacent). Cruisers are advised to stay away from this bay and be wary of anyone approaching your boat at night.

Clearance

A new system for clearance is in place if you are traveling to Puerto Galera. Arriving yachts will only be able to enter at either Muelle Pier or Balatero Pier. A Php50 Environmental User Fee is levied on every visitor, on a per visit basis.

Yachts can contact the Yacht Club in advance for information on anchorages and port clearance.

The Immigration sub-office is above the wet markets, and is open Monday, Tuesday and Wednesday mornings.

An alternative is to take a ferry to Batangas, which takes about 75 minutes and costs about 250 pesos one way, and visit the Immigration Office there at Diversion Rd, Barangay Bolbok (Ph (043) 723-3032).

There is no Customs office here.

Last updated October 2013.

Docking

Puerto Galera Yacht Club :© Karin Stubbs

The Yacht Club maintains 20 visitors' moorings very close to the western end of Muelle Bay, next to the town. the charge is 336 pesos per day (2013). Max. length 50ft/20 tons. This includes a free water taxi service and use of the yacht club facilities.

The Yacht Club moorings lie within Puerto Galera's natural typhoon shelter and are regularly maintained in order to provide high levels of security.

Anchoring

Yachts are not allowed to anchor in Muelle Bay as this is a protected marine environment, except for the fixed moorings. Anchoring is permitted in Boquete Bay or Dalaruan Bay, but take great care when anchoring so as not to disturb the protected corals and the indigenous giant clams.

Pandan Island is the only resort on the West coast of Mindoro and is the gateway to Apo Reef, and also a good waypoint for yachts travelling to Coron, Basuanga. It has a restaurant and dive shop. There is a good anchorage south west of the beach, in fair and medium weather conditions. For vessels with less than two metre draft there is a sandy area about four metres deep, vessels with more draft need to anchor further offshore in about 25 metres of water (good holding in mud). Avoid dropping your anchor between the two areas as the steep slope may cause your anchor to drag in northerly winds.
