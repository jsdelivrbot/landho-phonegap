Longyearbyen - Profile

Location

Longyerbyen is the administrative centre of Svalbard, with a permanent population of about 2,000 people. It is located on the west coast of the island. It is the most northerly city in the world.

Position 78°13.68’N, 15°36.58’E (pontoon)

Clearance

The Governor’s office (Syselmannen) deals with all formalities. Visiting yachts must obtain a cruising permit before arrival and make arrangements for special insurance to cover the possible cost of a search and rescue operation. Also compulsory is having a gun to protect the crew from possible attacks by polar bears, of which there have been several in recent years. Those who do not possess a gun can hire one locally. SAR insurance cover is also available locally but is probably much cheaper if it is arranged through one’s own insurance company or bank.

See the website https://lovdata.no for regulations for visitors.

Last updated January 2016.

Docking

There is a pontoon for visiting yachts next to the wharf with water, electricity, toilets, showers and laundry facilities. A charge is made for the use of the pontoon.

Anchoring is free and the dinghy can be left at the pontoon.

The town has all the usual services (fuel, provisions etc.)

Last updated January 2016.
