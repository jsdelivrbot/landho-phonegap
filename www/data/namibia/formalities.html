Namibia - Formalities

Clearance

Request permission to enter the port.

Clear in with Customs, Immigration and the Harbour Master's office. Clearance is straightforward.

If arriving or leaving at the weekend, give prior warning. There may be a small overtime charge.

Being unable to provide clearance papers from your previous port, does not appear to cause a problem.

Last updated April 2016.

Immigration

Note: Visitors arriving from countries affected by Ebola virus disease will not be allowed entry into Namibia. Those who are allowed to enter will be subject to screening.

All visitors must be in possession of a passport which is valid for at least 6 months beyond intended departure date from Namibia.

The citizens of the following countries will be permitted a visa free entry for a maximum period of 90 days (extensions are possible on application to immigration):-
Angola, Austria, Australia, Belgium, Botswana, Brazil, Cuba, Canada, France, Germany, Italy, Ireland, Japan, Kenya, Liechtenstein, Luxembourg, Russia, Spain, Switzerland, UK, USA, Mozambique, New Zealand, Portugal, Iceland, the Netherlands, Malaysia, Singapore, the Scandinavian countries, South Africa, Zambia and Zimbabwe.

Check the length of your permitted stay as this is sometimes shorted than expected. Overstaying the time granted or an incorrect or missing entry stamp could lead to detention, arrest and a fine.

Last updated August 2015.

Customs

Firearms must be declared on arrival.

Plants & plant material and most kinds of seeds cannot be imported.

Foreign currency must be declared on arrival.

Last updated August 2015.

Health

Yellow fever vaccination is required if coming from an infected area.

Malaria prophylaxis is recommended, but the coast, the Namib Desert and the south are regarded as almost risk-free.

Medical services in Namibia are of a very high standard. However, the availability of most services is restricted to the main towns.

Water is generally safe to drink but is often recycled and heavily chlorinated.

Restrictions

East of Lüderitz, do not enter the prohibited diamond area, mainly south of the road to Keetmanshoop. This area is patrolled by well-armed guards.

A permit is required to visit the Cape Cross Seal Colony.
