Namibia - General Info

Time Zone

UTC +1 (Western Africa Time Zone)

UTC +2 September to April.

Electrical Supply

YACHTING ESSENTIALS

Electricity

Voltage: 220 V, Frequency: 50 Hz

Water

Drinking water is safe to drink throughout Namibia. Drinking water is piped 180 kilometers from deep desert wells to Luderitz. At Walvis Bay Yacht Club be sure you are using the correct tap for drinking water as some only provide water suitable for watering etc.

Fuel

Neither of the ports have a convenient fuel berth, so fuel has to be jerry jugged from a service station, some of which require a taxi ride.

LPG

Propane is available from hardware stores.

Opening Hours

Most business are open 8am to 1pm and 2pm to 5pm.

Apart from large supermarkets, everything is closed from 1pm Saturday and all day Sunday,

Money

Namibian dollar (N$).

The import/export of N$ is restricted to N$500.

The South African rand is legal tender and is the most useful currency with which to arrive. Namibian currency is not accepted outside the country.

Foreign currency must be declared on arrival.

MasterCard and Visa are the most widely accepted credit cards.

The local banks all have ATMs and credit cards (VISA and MasterCard) are accepted at the major retailers (service stations, grocery stores, hardware store, etc.).

Prices

Prices are generally the same or a little above those of South Africa. Accommodation and tourist meals are about double.

Many purchases in Namibia will include 15% "sales" tax (VAT).

Communications

English is widely spoken and is the official language of Namibia.  Most subjects in school are taught in English. Afrikaans and local indigenous languages are also widely spoken.

International Dialing Code for Namibia is +264.

Mobile telephone roaming agreements exist with most international mobile phone operators. Coverage is best in urban areas and along major roads. Elsewhere is can be patchy.

MTC has the widest coverage and supposedly best coverage in Namibia.

It is possible to buy a 'pay as you go' sim card package on arrival.

There are Internet cafes situated throughout the country but speeds are generally slow.

Postal and Courier Services

Most goods arrive in Namibia via South Africa with duty added.  The "average" import duty is 19%, with the duty range running from 0% for some items to 45% for other items.  Goods which "should take 3 days" for delivery from South Africa, often take over 10 days..

Transport

Private taxis and hire cars are available.

Mini-buses operate between towns. They do not run to a timetable and leave when full.

Medical Services

Medical care is covered by the local public clinics and local private doctors, which are very affordable.
