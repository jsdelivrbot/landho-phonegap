Money

East Caribbean dollar (EC$). US$ also widely accepted.

Effective 1 July 2015, the I cent and 2 cent EC coins will be withdrawn from circulation. The coins will no longer be valid either as consumers will not be able to conduct transactions using these coins. Persons can present the coins at any of the commercial banks which operate within the Eastern Caribbean Currency Union (ECCU) to receive face value.

A price rounding system will be implemented within the ECCU countries for the settlement of cash transactions only, whereby the total payment, inclusive of duties, fees and taxes, will be rounded symmetrically to the nearest five cents. Non-cash payments using cheques, debit and credit cards will not be impacted and therefore will not be rounded.

Transport

Airports

There are two airports on Saint Lucia:

George Fl Charles Airport (code SLU) for local flights and inter island travel (Vigie Cove). Approx. 25 minutes from Rodney Bay Marina. Tel: +1 758 452 1156

International Hewanorra airport (code UVF) is at the south end of the island at Vieux Fort with direct flights to the UK and a number of US cities. It is 50 miles and 90 minutes by taxi from Rodney Bay Marina. Expect to pay US$85 one way (up to 3 passengers). For 4 people or more, US$25 per person. Many cruising boats stopover at Vieux Fort anchorage, close to the airport, to drop off and pick up crew. Be sure to secure your boat carefully if leaving it at anchor here.

Flights to and from St. Lucia can be expensive. Many people take the fast ferry to neighbouring Martinique and fly from there.

Inter-Island Ferries

Express Des Isles operates a modern high speed Catamaran service between Saint Lucia, Martinique, Dominica and Guadeloupe. L' Express Des Isles service runs between Saint Lucia and Fort de France, Martinique.

The schedule varies, but normally there are 3 ferries a week. Fares are approx. 67 Euros one way, and 100 Euros round trip. The trip takes approximately three hours.

For more information on Express des Isles Ferry service contact Cox & Company Limited on 758 452 2211 or visit www.express-des-iles.com

Channel Shuttles Inc. / Compagnie Maritime West Indies operates a small ferry (up to 15 people) between Castries and Marin, Martinique which is very useful if leaving your boat in Martinique for refit or shopping etc. Tel: 758 452 8757/451 8161 Fax: 758 458 0840 email: channelshuttles@candw.lc

Car Rentals

Driving regulations: Driving is on the left hand side of the road in St Lucia. A local driving permit or International Driving Licence is required if you wish to drive on the island. A local permit costs EC$54 and can be obtained from the car hire company when you hire the car, or at airport Immigration offices and local Police stations. It lasts for three months. Drivers must be over 25 years of age and less than 65.

There are plenty of car hire companies on island offering a wide range of saloon and 4x4's. Note that most are automatic transmission

The main roads are all in good condition but some roads inland and off the beaten track may require four wheel drive. Driving offers spectacular views especially near the Pitons and Soufriere.

Estimated driving times:
Vieux Fort/Hewanorra - Castries 1 hour
Castries - Rodney Bay 30 minutes
Vieux Fort - Soufriere 50 minutes
Castries - Soufriere 1 hour

Tip: If you need to go to Castries, try to park in the Castries Central Car park - a multistorey car park near Bank of St Lucia.

Taxis

Taxis are widely available at the airports and in towns and can be identified by the blue ‘TX’ license plates. They vary from small saloons to minibuses and are usually modern and airconditioned. If by chance you cannot find one, just walk into the nearest hotel and they’ll call one for you.

Taxis are not metered, as fares are fixed by law. However it is a good idea to confirm the cost of your journey before setting off and the rate in EC$ or US$ as rates can vary according to number of travellers.

Buses

Local buses can be used to get pretty much anywhere on the island, at very little cost. They have green "M" registration plates..

Water Taxis

Water taxis operate at Rodney Bay Marina, normally located on the main pontoon or contact them on VHF Ch.16. This is a quick and easy way to reach the beach or restaurants and bars, and boats at anchor.

Events

Saint Lucia Jazz Festival: www.stluciajazz.org
End of April / May

Saint Lucia Carnival: www.stluciacarnival.net
Carnival is the biggest show on the island's calendar. Activities span a whole month and culminate in early July with calypso and soca monarch competitions, a carnival queen show, panorama the steel pan competition and two days of "jump up." Costumed bands take to the streets for the Carnival pageant: parade of costumes.

Flower festivals: La Rose, August; La Marguerite, October

ARC Finish: December, at Rodney Bay Marina.

Friday Seafood Feast - Anse la Raye
Only 10 minutes away from Marigot Bay, this fishing village offers an excellent Friday Night fish feast. Starting from 6pm, tables are set up in the middle of the street overlooking the bay, while cooking is done in booths that line the two sides of the road. There is lobster in season and a variety of seafood, barbecued, fried or cooked en papillote. There is music and occasionally live band entertainment.

Street Party - Gros Islet
The original street lime. Gros Islet Friday Night on Friday evenings has been in existence for over 20 years. Barbecue grills come out onto the sidewalks and glowing coals sizzle beneath chicken and conch kebabs. All the bars down Gros Islet's main street are a hive of activity with visitors and St.Lucians. Speakers set up on street corners keep the audience hopping with a variety of Caribbean rythmns. Go in a group and don't take your wallet.
