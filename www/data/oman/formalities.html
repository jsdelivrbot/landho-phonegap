Oman - Formalities

Clearance

The port should be called on Channel 16 one hour before arrival. Normally permission will be given to enter the harbour and directions should be followed as to where to go for clearance.

On arrival boats are normally boarded by Quarantine, Immigration, Customs and Harbour officials. No one should go ashore before formalities are completed. Passport and ship's papers are retained until departure.

Yachts can obtain a cruising permit from the Royal Oman Police (ROP) in the harbour at Mina Qaboos - it is issued from Jan 1st until Dec 31st - and costs 150 O.R. (Approx USD400).

Formalities for outward clearance are complicated and sufficient time should be allowed for them. All offices must be visited in turn, starting with police and finishing with the Harbour Master.

The official Guidelines are that vessels over 30 net tonnage are required to have a agent; under 30nt it is not required but may be an advantage. However, in reality (in particular in Salalah), all yachts are required to clear through an agent (see Noonsite report ), and cannot "go it alone". This can be expensive.

Last updated November 2015.

Immigration

Passports should have a validity of at least 6 months.

Visas are required by all nationals except those of the Gulf States.

If arriving by yacht, a 10 day short visit visa will be issued on arrival by Immigration for nationals of European, Scandinavian and South American countries, Australia, Brunei, Canada, Indonesia, Japan, Malaysia, Malta, New Zealand, Singapore, South Korea, Taiwan, Thailand, Turkey and United States.

Nationals of the following countries are only issued visas when arriving as part of a organised tour group :Egypt, Iran, India, Jordan, Albania, Morocco, Syria, Ukraine, Russia and Bosnia.

Without a visa you will not be allowed to leave the port.

Israeli passport holders, and those with evidence of travel to Israel, will be denied entry.

Visa applications can now be made online at www.rop.gov.om/visa/english/onlineservices_visaapplication_main.aspx

See Royal Oman Police website for the latest information.

The loss or theft of a passport can result in considerable difficulties because Omani law requires the traveller to report the loss or theft to the Royal Oman Police (ROP) and try to recover the passport by placing an advertisement in local newspapers.

Last updated November 2015.

Customs

Firearms must be declared.

Meat, dates, alcohol and fresh fruit and vegetables may be restricted.

All prescription drugs need to be accompanied by the the prescription.

Last updated November 2015.

Health

Malaria prophylaxis is recommended.

Fees

Immigration and Customs fee, 15 Omani rial (about US$40) per yacht.

There is a 6 rial visa fee per person. There is an ATM outside the port gate which you can visit after clearing in and obtaining your visa, to take out Omani rial in order to pay the authorities and retrieve your US dollars.

Local Customs

This is a Muslim country and Islamic law must be respected. Sensitivity regarding standards of dress should be observed.  Women should dress conservatively and men must not wear shorts or go shirtless in public. During the month of Ramadan, one should not eat, drink or smoke in public.
