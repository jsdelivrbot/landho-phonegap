Port Antonio - Profile

Location

This port lies on Jamaica's north east coast. It comprises of East harbour and West Harbour with a narrow, well-marked (and lit) channel between the two. The marina and cruise ship terminal are in the West harbour.

It is a good place to visit, however cruisers have reported being a little put off by the constant attention from hustlers.

Position 18° 10.5'N,76° 27.1'W.

Clearance

See Jamaica Formalities for full details re. clearing into and out of Jamaica.

Port Antonio is an official Port of Entry. Customs, Immigration and Quarantine clear yachts in and out at the Erroll Flynn marina.

On approach, call the marina on VHF channel 16 and they will advise on where to berth and make arrangements with the Authorities for clearance.

Docking

The newest marina at Port Antonio (now called Errol Flynn Marina) has the best facilities and the staff are very helpful. It is a government-owned marina and is in the West harbour.

The two old marinas are now mostly in disrepair.

It is also possible to anchor off the marina in West Harbour, or pick up a mooring, for a daily fee which includes the marina security and use of the dinghy dock, showers, WiFi, laundry access and pool usage.

There is also a small daily garbage and environmental fee for boats at anchor or berthed in a marina.

See latest fees reported by cruisers at bottom of page under comments.

The entire shoreline at West Harbour lies inside a gated, fenced perimeter. Along the length of the waterfront runs a boardwalk and park. The area is accessed through a security gate. It is a very lively place, popular with the locals and visitors, especially on the weekends.

It is possible to get basic provisions here or take a bus into Kingston, which takes 3 hours.

Last updated December 2015.
