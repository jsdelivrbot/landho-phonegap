Greenland - Formalities

Clearance

In spite of its association with Denmark, Greenland is not a member of the EU and therefore all arriving boats must follow the usual clearance procedure and make their first stop at a port of entry.

Contact the port authorities well in advance of arrival for special permission to enter the ports, if possible 24 hours beforehand on VHF Channels 12 or 16.

Last updated November 2015.

Immigration

All visitors must have a valid passport.

Greenland is neither a member of the European Union nor a member of the Schengen Agreement Area, but its visa requirements are similar.

For visitors wishing to explore the glaciers and mountains or visit the national park view this website www.eastgreenland.com.

Last updated November 2015.

Customs

The importation of firearms is forbidden.

Note: Special permission is needed to export souvenirs of whales’ teeth and walrus tusks. Authorisation forms are available from shops and tourist offices.

Import of fresh food is prohibited.

Last updated November 2015.

Health

A head-net is essential if ashore during the summer (especially July) to prevent the clouds of mosquitoes, midges and mini-flies getting into eyes, noses and ears. Such nets are widely sold in Greenland.

Restrictions

The military areas of Gronnedal Fladestation (naval base), Thule Air Base and Sondre Stromfjord Air Base are prohibited for yachts. All military areas must be avoided.
