Bosnia - Formalities

Clearance

Nothing is known about clearance procedures for yachts here.

Last updated June 2015.

Immigration

A passport is required and must be vaild for at least 3 months.

Bosnia & Herzegovina does not recognise passports issued by the Former Yugoslav Federation Republic (Serbia & Montenegro), which have a red cover.

No visa required for citizens of the EU, Argentina, Australia, Brazil, Brunei, Canada, El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, Hong Kong, Iceland, Israel, Japan, Korea, Kuwait, Macau, Macedonia, Malaysia, Mexico, Montenegro, New Zealand, Nicaragua, Norway, Panama, Paraguay, Qatar, Russia, San Marino, Serbia, Singapore, Switzerland, Turkey, Uruguay, USA and Venezuela for stays of up to 90 days.

All other citizens must apply for a visa prior to entry.

Ensure your passport is stamped on entry or you may be fined when you leave.

Last updated June 2015.

Customs

Firearms must be declared.

The export of works of art is prohibited.

Last updated June 2015.

Health

Immunisation against typhoid and polio are recommended.

Hepatitis A occurs and vaccination is usually advised. Immunisation against hepatitis B, diphtheria, tuberculosis and tick-borne encephalitis is also recommended.

Medical and dental facilities outside Sarajevo and major towns are limited.

Restrictions

Landmines and other unexploded ordnance remain from the 1992-95 war. Highly populated areas and major routes are clear of mines and are safe to visit, but you should take special care near to the former lines of conflict. Although roads themselves may be clear on major routes, there are many landmines close to the edge of roads. Don’t stray from roads and paved areas. You should avoid the open countryside and especially destroyed or abandoned buildings, neglected land, un-tarred roads, woods and orchards, private property and abandoned villages. Abandoned buildings, even in towns and cities may be booby trapped with mines. For further information, check the Mine Action Centre website

Pets

Dogs and cats may be taken into Bosnia without any restrictions or quarantine. (Highly exotic pets may require an import licence) At the point of entry a Pets Passport and Health Certificate issued by recognised Veterinarian stating breed,colour, name, sex and name of owner must be produced. Dogs and cats must be vaccinated for Rabies more than 30 days and not more than 11 months prior to arrival. The certificate should indicate type, batch number and date of vaccination. Certificate should also show that pet is healthy to travel and is free from parasites such as flees and ticks and that it is currently on a prevention medication program. Microchip not required but recommended.
