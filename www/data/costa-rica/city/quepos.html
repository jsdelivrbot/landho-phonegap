Quepos - Profile

Location

This popular town lies in the centre of Costa Rica's Pacific coast. It is an attractive resort with excellent beaches and good facilities for visiting yachts.

Position 9°25.5′N, 84°9.4′W﻿.

Clearance

See Costa Rica Fomalities for full details on clearing into and out of Costa Rica.

Quepos is now a port of entry. Clearance is available at the Marina Pez Vela but the officials are not based at the port and have to travel from Puntarenas at additional cost.

To enable the marina to arrange clearance a number of documents need to be sent in advance. See http://www.marinapezvela.com/en/clearing-information/ for full details.

Last updated September 2016.

Docking

The marina is not cheap, so many cruisers choose to anchor long term here. They go to Playa Biesanz, just SE of the marina. Take care as there are a number of rocks visible at low tide.

A steep path from shore will take you up to the road where you can catch a taxi in to town. Call from the beach (or ask one of the vendors to do so for you) and the taxi will be waiting for you at the top.

There is good snorkeling around the reef here.

Last updated September 2016.

This is a huge marina with good clean facilities and security and helpful staff. Facilities include power, water, fuel, WiFi, small chandlery, Customs and Immigration, pumpout at each berth. Rates are high.
Modern boatyard with 200 ton travel lift (up to 28’6’’ wide), all repairs. Small shop, but only 10 mins walk south from main town which offers shops, restaurants and stores.
