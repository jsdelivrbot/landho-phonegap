Sevastopol - Profile

Location

Sevastopol, on the SW corner of the Crimean peninsula, has very much the feel of a Russian city and that is the principal language spoken here. For many years it was a "closed" city due to the fact that it was an important Russian Naval base.

Balaklava is part of Sevastopol, a distant district which is 15km away from Sevastopol's city limits, 30km away from city centre.

Update 2015: Due to the uncertain situation in this region, it is recommended that cruisers avoid this area.

Position 44º 37' N, 33º 32' E.

Clearance

Currently this port is closed to international shipping.

This was recorded as a Ukrainian a Port of Entry, but the current situation is not known.

Call Port Authorities of VHF channel 16.

Last updated February 2016.

Docking

This large harbour has several berthing options on its south side. For a full list of yacht clubs in Sevastopol, see the Sevastopol Sailing Federation Website (in Russian but google translate works well and there are pictures).

As well as using the Passenger Ship Terminal (44°36.99’N, 33°31.62’E) for clearance if coming from outside the Ukraine, it can be used for longer term berthing offering easy access to the city. It is reported to be uncomfortable however with wash from passing vessels.

The fully serviced Golden Symbol Marina should also be considered in Balaklava. This is where the majority of cruising boats in the area are berthed.

Last updated February 2012.

This is a full-service marina with electricity, water, fuel etc. Berthing is stern-to a fixed dock. The marina has a travelift and offers full repair facilities (see website for further details). Laundry service and cafe on site.
Russian Navy Yacht Club
44°37.01’N, 33°30.38’E
Although a dinghy sailing club really, they are reported to be very friendly and there is berthing suitable for smaller yachts. There is no sign to announce what it is. A simple place (electricity, water and showers) with floating pontoons or stern-to the pier. City centre location with plenty of shops in the immediate area. This club belongs to the Russian Government.
Ushakova Balka Marina
44°37.0’N, 33°32.78’E
This slightly drab, but friendly mooring option, is located in a housing suburb East of the town centre. Steel pontoons make up inner swimming area, inside a compound. Places are limited. Watchman day & night. Taxi or bus to town. No shoreside facilities.
Yacht Club Kozachya Bay
Situated in a protected bay, this marina is reported to be quiet and safe. Many yachts (15-20m) stay there during the winter and the Club is currently improving facilities to attract more yachts. Unfortunately the Club is approx. 20 km away from the city.
