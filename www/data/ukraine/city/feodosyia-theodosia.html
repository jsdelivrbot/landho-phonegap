Feodosyia (Theodosia) - Profile

Location

This ancient city was originally call Caffa (or Kaffa). As well as being the largest commercial and naval port in this region of the Crimea, Feodosyia also has many beaches, mineral springs and mud baths, and in the high season is a popular resort town.

Position 45°02.4’N, 35°25.0’E.

Clearance

Currently this port is closed to international shipping.

This was recorded as a Ukrainian a Port of Entry, but the current situation is not known.

Call Port Authorities of VHF channel 16.

Last updated February 2016.

Docking

This harbour is open the the north and northeast but offers good shelter in winds from other directions.

There appears to be room to anchor here and the holding is good, however a stern line ashore may be required. Otherwise berth to the east pier or where directed.

This is a state owned port and therefore charges, if any, are cheap.

It may be possible for fuel and water to be delivered.

Basic provisons can be obtained in the town. Some repairs are possible and there is a mobile crane.

Last updated September 2011.
