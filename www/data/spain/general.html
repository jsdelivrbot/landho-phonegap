Spain - General Info

Time Zone

UTC +1, summer time UTC +2 April to September.

Electrical Supply

Voltage: 230 V

Frequency: 50 Hz

Opening Hours

Traditional Spanish hours are 1000-1400 and 1600 to 2000. Large outlets tend to stay open until later and only large retail outlets open on a Sunday. Bars are open all day, there are no alcohol sale time restrictions. Restaurants open for lunch and dinner, the latter normally not before 2030.

Money

Spain's currency is the Euro.

Banks and cash machines are numerous.

Note You are required to carry some form of photo ID with you at all times in Spain and will be asked for it in shops and restaurants etc. when making payment with a credit card.

Communications

International dialing code for Spain is +34.

Post: Every town will have a post office. Stamps are sold here and also at outlets called "Estancos" (old tabac dispensing shops).

Mobile Telephones: Many choices exist for mobile phone service in Spain. Spanish mobile phone service operates on a GSM network although roaming charges may apply. Buying and installing a Spanish SIM card in the phone (and therefore obtaining a Spanish mobile phone number) is a less-expensive option. Phones from North America that operate on the CDMA system will not work in Spain.

Internet: Internet cafes, many marinas have internet access and more and more are offering WiFi. Accessing through your mobile can be a problem as the mobile phone providers will demand a Spanish bank from which to Direct Debit. Roaming charges will be high.

Diplomatic Missions

Foreign Embassies in Spain

Emergencies

The Coastguards in Spain speak English and respond both to telephone and VHF calls. The lifeboats however are contracted from private firms, so rescue will involve a charge, generally calculated as cost per hour. Life rescue is of course free and immediately put in action in case of emergency.

Pan-European emergency - Dial 112
Ambulance Service (Ambulancia) - Dial 061
National Police (Policía Nacional) - Dial 091
Local Police (Policía Municipal) - Dial 092
Fire Service (Bomberos) - Dial 080
Medical emergencies on the Costa Blanca - Dial 085
