Comoros - General Info

Time Zone

UTC +3

Electrical Supply

Voltage: 220 V

Frequency: 50 Hz

In Mutsamudu, Anjouan there is no electricity after 2pm until the evening.

Money

Comoron Franc (CF).

All foreign currency must be declared on arrival.

Euros are accepted in some shops and is the best currency to bring. US Dollars are less easy to use.

The Banque pour l’Industrie et le Commerce (BIC), which has branches on the islands of , is the only place where currency can be exchanged.

You will find banks in both Grande Comore and Anjouan with ATMs. Note however that electricity on Anjouan, for example, is typically turned off between noon and 6pm, when ATMs may not function.

Credit card facilities are limited.

Communications

International dialing code for Comoros is +269.

Cell phones in Comoros are rapidly overtaking land phones as many cellular phones now provide Internet access and cell phone computers. SMS, MMS and broadband are becoming standard features on cell phones.

Comoros Telecom is a government run monopoly. A SIM card was 4,000 CF in 2015. Additional data, cost per GB, decreases with each additional purchase; about $50 for 8 GB in August 2015.

Note that VOIP services can deplete pre-paid data plans by as much as 100 times the expected rate, as the telecom monopoly tries to steer users to long-distance calling instead. So don’t use Skype, Facetime, etc. or buy data in small increments so any “surprise” drop in data doesn’t cost you too much.

Last updated September 2015.

Transport

A bike is the best means of travel.

There are taxis but they are very crowded and often unsafe

It is possible to rent a car, but due to the hazardous driving conditions, rental agencies make hiring a driver obligatory with their vehicles.

Driving at night, on any form of transport, is to be avoided.
