Port Dickson - Profile

Location

A popular holiday destination on the west coast, Port Dickson is about 60 NM north of Melaka. It is famous for its beaches. This is a great place to stop while sailing the Malacca Straits and a convenient base to tour the wonderful city of Melaka.

Clearance

For full details on clearance into and out of Malaysia see Malaysia Formalities.

Unfortunately the marina no longer has immigration and customs offices as it is now a requirement for all visitors to Malaysia to be fingerprinted. Visiting cruisers now need to take a taxi (or bus - runs every hour) into Port Dickson for clearance with Immigration, the Harbour Master and Customs. Cruisers report that the process is quick and straightforward and there are no fees.

The Marina is hoping to offer a service in the future to escort skippers to the official offices in Port Dickson.

Last updated May 2015.

Docking

The marina here is well sheltered from all weathers but the entrance is tricky to get into as the bay has a sand bank in the middle. To enter come in at right angles from the deep water channel (02º 28' 36N, 101º 49' 86E) to the end of the breakwater.

The marina should be contacted on VHF Ch14 before entry. If arriving after hours, berth at the fuel pontoon (pumps not working). Staff are reported to be friendly and professional.

This marina has been completely refurbished. Access in all weathers. Spacious and well sheltered. All facilities, fuel (delivered by jerry jug), laundry, internet, excellent restaurants and bar, swimming pool and boaters' library. Staff very helpful. Limited space during peak periods. Dry docking available and light repairs. 24hr security. Haulout by mobile crane can be organised from outside the marina.
