Kota Kinabalu - Profile

Location

Kota Kinabalu (often called K.K.), lies on the north west coast of Sabah, and is the capital of the state. It is a major gateway for visitors to Borneo.

Location 5°58′N, 116°05′E﻿.

Clearance

For full details on clearance into and out of Malaysia see Malaysia Formalities.

There is only one walk-on marina (Sutera Harbour Marina) and various public anchorages where one can moor the boat before proceeding into town to visit the various offices.

If staying at Sutera Harbour Marina, the Marina Office are now able to undertake CIQP clearance on behalf of yacht owners/captains.

Immigration
Must be visited first. Office on the waterfront, north, in town, on Jalan Pantai. It is located in a small building behind the main building.

Customs
At the Cargo Terminal at Jalan Tanjung Lipat. It is a small security/gate building where the guard has been replaced by the officer dealing with clearance. It is on the right of the main Custom Office when facing the sea.

Jabatan Laut
Located at Sepanggar, approx. 25 kms north of town, past 1Borneo. This is difficult to get to unless you take a taxi, which will be expensive (about 100RM return – ask the taxi to wait for you!).

There are no charges at any of these Departments although 2-3 RM for the price of the form may be requested.

Checking out (if you are leaving the state of Sabah) is the same but in reverse order. Sailing within the state of Sabah only requires a simple port clearance from the Jabatan Laut. Nothing from Customs or Immigration.

Last updated April 2015.

Docking

It is possible to anchor in the harbour and Harbour Control will tell you of any restricted areas. It is also possible to anchor in front of the city (05°58.738'N, 116°04.128'E), but be very careful with your dinghy and don't leave any valuables on deck.

Other options are to anchor/moor off the Kota Kinabalu Yacht Club, at Tanjung Aru, or outside Sutera Harbour Marina, 3 km from Kota Kinabalu. Note that yachts anchored outside Sutera Harbour Marina are no longer able to access the marina with their tenders, as berths are reserved for visiting yachts only.

There have been reports of thefts of dinghies and outboard motors from yachts at the Shangra-La anchorage/mooring area in the past. However latest reports are that it is safe and offers good holding. Sadly though, whilst they now have a small jetty for their fleet of island hopping boats, this is no longer available as a dinghy dock and it seems visiting cruisers are no longer welcome at the resort.

Noonsite has been advised that yachts are no longer welcomed by the management of the Gaya Island Resort. Nevertheless, there are some beautiful anchorages around Pulau Gaya on both the NE and SW sides.

Thefts have also been reported in the past at the Sutera Harbour Marina from yachts on the (overflow) Southern pontoon, however additional security and CCTV are now in place (photo courtesy of SV Ariel). The marina is usually full with only a few berths available for visitors on a first come, first served basis. Making a reservation is advisable.

Last updated April 2015.

Gaya Island Resort
Yachts no longer welcomed by Management
Two poorly maintained moorings were available here for a fee. Facilities ashore: toilets, water and cold shower. Charge of RM20 roundtrip per/person for the ferry ride to Kota Kinabalu. Good anchorage in the SE monsoon, located at one of the state parks with wildlife near the city.

5 mins to town & 10 mins from KK International Airport.
Popular 100+ berth marina including 8 berths for superyachts up to 200ft. The clubhouse is attached to two 5-star hotels and a 27-hole championship golf course, the facilities of which are available to berth holders (pools, restaurants, gym, bowling, billiards, tennis, squash and badminton courts). Yachts with live-in crew must have holding tanks. The marina is well-protected.
Facilities include water, electricity, showers, toilets, fuel jetty and pump out facility, 24 hour security with CCTV (exit and entry to pontoons now requires a swipe card). There is an AC shuttle service to the city centre and travel information available at the Marina Office.
Fuel bunkering is also available on site at the best quoted price from 3 fuel suppliers.
Advice to intending visiting yachts. Sutera Harbour Marina, though primarily a members-only marina, does usually have available spaces for visitors. Call in advance for availability and to make a reservation. Since early 2014 the whole resort has been under new management and prices have risen. There is a min. charge for 30ft on wet berths and yachts mooring on longer pontoons are charged by berth length. Multihulls pay fees x 1.5 and a security deposit is required by all berth holders. For the latest fee structure and regs. see the marina website.





Tanjung Aru Marina
Shangri-La's Tanjung Aru Resort , W.D.T. 14, 89459 Tg. Aru , Sabah
Tel:214215 Fax:240966
The Shangri-La hotel at Tanjung Aru has an anchorage area, however no landing facilities or hotel facilities are available as these are exclusively for the use of Shangri-La guests only (Oct. 2013). Care and a good chart is needed for getting into the anchorage area. You can walk to the Golf Club, Yacht Club or the hawker stalls that are past the hotel for meals. The local bus into Kota Kinabalu stops at the hotel staff entrance. There are also plenty of taxis from the hotel car park.

Yacht Clubs

It is a short walk from the Shangri-La Hotel along the beach or road to the Yacht Club. There is an anchorage off the beach but in SW winds this could be untenable, you have to leave your dinghy on the beach but there is usually someone at the yacht club who may keep an eye on it. The buses pass right outside the gate. The Yacht Club asks that you pay 25rm a week membership but it is well worth this to take advantage of the cheap good food and drinks that they serve in air-conditioned surroundings. The use of the pool is included, the showers are also very good, and they can arrange laundry for you. Water is available but it is not potable. Cruisers recommend this as the best anchorage in Kota Kinabalu during the NE Monsoon.
