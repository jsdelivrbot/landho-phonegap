Romania - Formalities

Clearance

Clearance formalities for visiting yachts are generally straightforward.

Yachts must clear in and out of each port. Crew should remain on board until formalities have been completed.

On arrival at any of the ports a yacht will be visited by Border Police (Immigration), Customs and Health. They will want to see a completed crew list in duplicate in addition to the ships registration and valid insurance, plus the Customs declaration. It is helpful to have a ship’s stamp with the ships' particulars on it.

On departure for the next port in Romania, or on leaving the country, all the visits are repeated and an exit stamp added.

Once the formalities are completed a Clearance Permit is given by Harbourmaster to sail from each port to the next port or country. Agents are not mandatory and there is no charge for formalities.

Last updated March 2015.

Immigration

All visitors must have a passport valid for 6 months.

No visa is required for visits of up to 90 days by nationals of the EU and certain other countries. See www.mae.ro for an up to date list.

Extensions of stays beyond 90 days may be obtained from the local passport office.

For a current list of countries whose nationals do require a visa to enter Romania, see Ministry of Foreign Affairs (appendix 1)

Last updated March 2015.

Customs

Firearms must be declared and will be detained.

A customs declaration must be completed on arrival, where video cameras, personal computers and other valuables should be detailed.

The export of articles of cultural, historical or artistic value is prohibited.

Last updated March 2015.

Health

There is a hepatitis risk.

Fees

Visa fee. Harbour fees.

Restrictions

Reports on the firing ranges and dates of activity are given following the weather on VHF ch. 12. The firing area can be very close to the coast and on direct the path to/from Constanta.

Pets

The Pets Travel Scheme (PETS) operates in Romania requiring all animals to have a Pet Passport, a current Health Certificate and Rabies Vaccination Certificate (valid at least 30 days prior to and not more than 6 months) and to be microchipped.

Translation of documents is not needed if they are in English, French or German.

Rabies is present in Romania and all restrictions must be strictly adhered to. Contact www.romaniatourism.com for more information.
