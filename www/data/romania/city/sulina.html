Sulina - Profile

Location

Situated at the end of the Sulina Canal, this port is one of the main outlets of the Danube River into the Black Sea.

The Danube River Delta is one of the best places in Europe and perhaps the world for bird watching – local boats offer tours. For visiting nature reserves in your own boat, a special permit is required and is issued in the town of Tulcia, 40 NM upstream.

If coming from the Black sea, locate the “safe water” buoy at N 45 08.075 and E 29 47.613 then steer 301 M to enter the 5 mile long breakwater at the mouth of the Sulina Canal - standard channel markings are in place at time of writing. The town itself is another 2 miles upstream after the end of the long breakwater. The current varies and depends on the time of year, winter run off and recent rainfalls upstream.

Port of Sulina: 45° 09.4'N, 29° 39.2'E.

Clearance

Port of Entry. See Romania Formalities for details.

Tie up anywhere along the South shore where space is available. The best place is reported to be across from the "Restaurant Marea Neagra", where most non-local pleasure boats tie up.

Customs and Immigration will come to the boat, usually a friendly local will notify them. Skippers then need to visit the Harbourmaster, who will issue a harbour permit at no charge. A completed crew list in duplicate in addition to the ships registration and valid insurance is required. It is helpful to have a ship’s stamp with the ships particulars on it (one cruiser reported he needed to use his stamp at least 10 times!).

On departure upstream, visit the Harbourmaster who will exchange the harbour permit for a departure permit to be produced at the next port upstream.

On departure for the Black Sea, visit the Harbourmaster who will issue a departure document and notify Customs and Immigration who will come to the boat to complete formalities.

Last updated June 2011.

Docking

Navigating in the river can be very difficult because of the strong current (see notes above in Location). Traffic on the river/canal is heavy at times ranging from medium sized ocean-going vessels to ferries and local boats. The local boats only have 2 settings on their engines, either dead stop or full speed, so wash can be a problem. Good fenders should solve most of the problem.

The Harbourmaster will come to the boat and collect US$10, or the equivalent local currency. This pays for power when available and water. The water seems to come from the firefighting water system and its quality or suitability has not been verified. If leaving the boat for the day, be sure to inform the Harbourmaster.

Some of the restaurants and café’s along the waterfront have open Wi-Fi and there is a small produce market and a decent grocery store. You can find an ATM in town.

Diesel is available a little further upstream.

Last updated June 2011.
