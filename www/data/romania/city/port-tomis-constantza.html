Port Tomis (Constantza) - Profile

Location

Port Tomis is the name of the yacht harbour located in the ancient sea port of Constanta. It lies 4 NM North of the commercial port.

Constanta has been a port since the 1st century BC and is the oldest city in Romania. The area around the marina is particularly attractive.

Position 44°10.5' N, 28°39.9'E.

Clearance

Port of Entry. See Romania Formalities for details.

Call on VHF Channel 67 on approach, but do not be surprised if no one responds. The harbour is serviced by the Border Police, housed in a white metal container about halfway along the East quay. It is best to tie up alongside the East quay, close to the sentry box, to complete formalities.

The friendly Border Police officer fills out the customs declaration and advise you that the Port Authorities may or may not visit the boat. If you are already cleared into Romania this is all that is required, if not, more formalities might be necessary.

On departure, inform the Border Police of your intended destination.

Last updated January 2014.

Docking

Take care to keep at least 1nm off the coast as the water is shallow close in.

This is an all-weather harbour and safe in winds from any direction. The entrance can be difficult to see until almost upon it, especially when arriving with the sun setting in the west. Use extra care and attention when arriving in strong S or SE winds.

photo of Tomis Marina from Ioan Cobzarenco (Oct 2015)

The marina is in the old harbour north of the commercial port of Constanta and consists of 4 floating pontoon. At the northern pontoon (A), the depth is 4 m on the southern side, and 2 m on northern side. 'B' is deep both sides. Call Contantin Sailing on VHF 16 for help and advice on mooring.

There is no security; the entire area is open to the public.

Last updated October 2015.
