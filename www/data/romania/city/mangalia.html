Mangalia - Profile

Location

First founded in the 6th century BC, Mangalia is the oldest continuously inhabited city in Romania. Archaeological museums in the town display ancient Greek and Roman artifacts.

Mangalia is just 4M north of the border with Bulgaria and has a secure marina inside the commercial port, in the inner harbour. It is probably the safest place to leave a boat if exploring the interior or returning home for a while.

Position: 43°48’N, 28°35’E.

Clearance

Port of Entry. See Romania Formalities for details.

Proceed to the inner harbour ("tourist harbour") in this large, well buoyed commercial port used for ship building. Border Police and Customs/Immigration should be waiting on one of the pontoons. If not, they are located in a white metal container/office across from the Southerly pontoon.

If coming from another Romanian port, only a visit to the Border Police is required.

Last updated September 2015.

Docking

This marina is safe in all weather conditions and winds from all directions. Depths are 3.5m or more.

The inner harbour (called the "tourist harbour") has now been converted to a marina with floating pontoons. As you approach the marina looking for a berth an official will probably come out to assist.

Water and electricity are available alongside the pontoons as is free Wi-Fi. The marina office can be found to the left as you leave the pontoons which houses toilets and showers.

The pontoons are gated and, according to the officials, locked at night and guarded. The Harbourmaster will visit at some point. Cruisers report that docking fees seem negotiable.

The entire waterfront has been renovated over the last 5 years.

Last updated February 2016.
