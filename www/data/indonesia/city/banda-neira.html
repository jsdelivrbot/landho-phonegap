Banda Neira - Profile

Location

This amazing harbour is formed by three of the volcanic islands in the Banda group. The nutmeg, cloves, and cinnamon which once drove explorers to Indonesia have long since been dispersed to grow in other places around the world. This is a sleepy island town now, but reminders of the past are everywhere.

Position 4°35′S 129°55′E

Clearance

The is not an official Port of Entry.

The port of Tual in the nearby Kai Islands is the nearest Port of Entry.

Last updated March 2016.

Docking

There are several anchoring options here:-

Hotel Anchorage (04° 32.641'S, 129° 54.133'E)
Stern to wall with two lines ashore
Advantages:-good shelter, in town centre, easy to get water, rubbish disposal
Disadvantages:- can be noisy, danger of prop wash from ferry, no wind for wind generator.

West Anchorage (04° 31.108'S, 129° 53.885'E)
Stern to trees with lines ashore
Advantages:- Quiet, good for wind generator
Disadvantages:- Difficult to get anchor in, lee shore, 0.75 mile to dinghy dock

South anchorage (04° 32.641'S, 129° 54.133'E) 
Free swinging anchorage
Advantages:-calm, good holding, good for wind generator, little current even at springs.
Disadvantages:- 1 mile to dinghy dock.

Middle anchorage (04° 32.052'S, 129° 53.982'E) 
Free swinging anchorage
Advantages:- Good holding, good for wind generator, relatively calm
Disadvantages:-0 .6 mile to dinghy dock, strong current at springs

Fuel and provisions available. WiFi at the anchorage.

Restaurant and boat trip to the outlying islands are worth doing. Plenty to do and see here including a walk up to the active volcano.

Last updated September 2015.
