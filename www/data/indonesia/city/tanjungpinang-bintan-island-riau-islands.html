Tanjungpinang (Bintan Island, Riau Islands) - Profile

Location

These are two groups of islands lying south of Singapore. The departure point for exploring these islands is Nongsa Point Marina on Batam Island, just 20nm from Singapore.

Riau is the island group to the east of Batam Island, while the Lingga group is just over the equator to the south.

Be aware that the charting of these islands is inaccurate, both in position and depths, and that some of the tidal streams around them can be quite strong.

The Lingga group, in particular, is largely uninhabited.

Tanjungpinang is the ferry port on the western side of the large island of Bintan, which, itself lies immediately east of Bantam Island. This big, busy city is well worth a visit if only for the market.

Position 0°56’N, 104°27’E. (Tanjungpinang)

Docking

Approach is straight forward and the many fast ferries will indicate the route in but the buoyage is clear and the leading line accurate.
There is an anchorage in good on stiff mud near the fuel barge at 00° 56.17'N,  104° 26.267'E.

Protection is good and the wash from ferries is not a nuisance as the ferry terminal is slightly down stream. Tugs and tows use the river so it is important to keep the fairway clear.

Fuel is not available at  the barge for yachts but only by can  at a stilt house close by with plants on its boards 250m up river.

Landing can be made at one of the small piers used by local ferries. Either the one painted yellow or at the excellent Chinese fish restaurant just north of it.

It is easy to get a water taxi ashore during the day but more difficult at night. Security might be an issue, as it has been in the past, so take normal precautions.

There is an excellent fruit vegetable and fish market close east of the landing jetties. There are supermarkets, but at some distance from the river.

The locals are very welcoming.

Last updated January 2014.
