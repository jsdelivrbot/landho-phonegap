Medana Bay - Profile

Location

This bay is just east of the Oberoi peninsula at the northern end of Lombok's west coast. It has a great anchorage during the south East Monsoon and has good facilities ashore. Close to the three Gili Islands and with views of Mount Rinjani to the east at 3,726m, this is a very quiet and restful place.

Position 8° 21.9'S, 116° 7.8'E.

The Marina website has good approach information on it, which is accurate. (Photos from MBM website).

Clearance

This is not a Port of Entry.

Docking

The villagers here have built a small marina with floating pontoons for 16 vessels (10 of which are serviced) and about 15 moorings in varying depths and a dinghy dock. Electricity and water available. Berthing is alongside or med-mooring.

Noonsite has been told there are plans to replace the moorings with new ones.

Access to / from the dock is easiest when the wind is lighter i.e. early or late in the day. You can anchor or take a mooring to wait until the wind has eased.

There is anchorage space available as well. Yachts are charged a small fee for anchoring to cover the cost of shore-side facilities (dinghy dock, showers and toilets, WiFi etc.).

The villagers are very helpful and friendly. They can arrange laundry, fuel, small repairs, cheap massages and tours of the island. Hotel Oberoi is a short walk away and dinner there is very reasonable.

Supplies are available from the small mini-market at the marina, and WiFi is available at the beach cafe on site.

Tanjung, the local town, is 4 km away and has a good morning market, numerous ATMs, pharmacies and a post office. For larger supermarkets, it's best to go south to the capital. The marina can organise air-conditioned cars and drivers to take you there or to take you for sight-seeing tours. The roads on Lombok are good, although the traffic can be quite heavy around the capital.

Cruisers report this is a more comfortable alternative to the often rough anchorages on Gilli Islands.
