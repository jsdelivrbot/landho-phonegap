Marshall Islands - Formalities

Clearance

The captain of a vessel must notify the Immigration Division of the date/time of entry and provide the crew/passenger list at least 24 hours before entering the Republic.

Avoid overtime fees of up to $250 (Immigration $100, Customs $75, Quarantine $100) by arriving during business hours Monday to Friday.

Clearance is quick and easy at Majuro Atoll. Call ‘Majuro Port Control’ on VHF 16 upon arrival and ask for instructions for clearance. If no answer, call the MBYC on VHF Ch 71 and ask for advice.

Port Control may schedule Customs and Immigration officials to meet the skipper at the Shoreline dinghy dock (near Uliga Wharf and RRE hotel) to complete clearance.  If not, a take to the Capital Building (about 10 minutes away) where, on the ground floor, you will find Customs who will then give you instructions to go to Immigration in the Mako Building, which is a five to 10 minute walk. Remember for men to wear a collared shirt and trousers and for for women a dress or skirt that reaches below the knee.

Immigration

All passports must be valid and if applying for a visa, must be valid for 6 months.

Visas are not required for citizens of the United States, Federated States of Micronesia and Palau.

No visas are required by crew of private yachts for stays of up to 7 days.

Tourist visas for stays of 30 days (with extensions for another 60 days) will be granted on arrival for citizens of All Pacific Forum Member Countries, including Australia and NZ, the EU, Canada, Japan, South Korea, The Philippines and Taiwan. When being issued with a tourist visa, it may be necessary to provide certain documents (letter stating purpose of visit, police record dated within last three months and health record dated within last three months showing traveller is free of HIV and TB).

All other person must obtain a visa prior to arrival. See contact below.

Entry Permits are required for every atoll to be visited. See Outer Islands page for more details.

Firearms must be declared on arrival.

Coral and turtle shell are prohibited exports.

Health

There is now no health risk to visiting cruisers from the 1950s nuclear testing.  Avoid eating coconuts, pandanus fruit and coconuts crabs on Bikini and Rongelap atolls. It’s okay to eat lobster and fish even from Bikini lagoon where 67 bombs were tested.

Majuro has one private clinic and one public hospital. Ebeye has one public hospital. Most outer islands have very basic medical dispensaries.

There may be some risk of cholera. A health certificate is required if arriving from infected areas. An HIV test is required for visits over 30 days. Diseases such as tuberculosis and leprosy exist on the Islands.

Dengue Fever endemic in the islands and precautions should be taken to avoid mosquito bits.

ZIKA VIRUS ALERT: (September 2016) There have been recent safety alerts from the US State Department, UK Foreign Office, and Center for Disease Control (CDC) regarding travel to parts of Central and South America, Africa, southern Asia, the Caribbean, and the South Pacific islands. The Marshall Islands is an area of interest. There is growing concern about the rapid spread of the ZIKA Virus and the impact of the virus on pregnant women and babies. ZIKA is transmitted by mosquitos in tropical and sub-tropical climates, and there is currently no cure or vaccine. This situation is evolving rapidly, so please refer to the CDC’s dedicated website if you are intending to cruise in one of the effected areas.

Cases of locally transmitted Zika virus have been confirmed in the last 9 months.

Documents

Cruising Permits are required for every outer island. See Outer Islands page for more details.

Permits may be obtained from the Ministry of Internal Affairs in Majuro. Permit fees are payable upon arrival to the permitted island. Yacht crews should seek out the island mayor, bring two copies of the permit and exact change.

Fees for most of the atolls are $25-$50, but one is reported to be as high as $250 (note: this has in the past been negotiated down by cruisers) .

Fees

Tourist visas for stays of 30 days (with extensions for another 60 days) cost $25 for citizens of all Pacific Forum Member Countries, including Australia and NZ, the EU, Canada, Japan, South Korea, The Philippines and Taiwan.

Non-Americans can purchase a single entry one-year visa for $200.

A $10 fee for yacht checkout at Marshall Islands Ports Authority.

There is a $20 departure tax. People over 60 are exempt.

Last updated April 2014.

Restrictions

The test site on Kwajalein Atoll occasionally conducts live fire exercises within a 200 mile radius. Contact Kwajalein Atoll Control by radio beforehand if sailing in this area.

Restricted islands are those used by the US army: Kwajalein Island (but not the entire Kwajalein atoll) is a closed military base, and the missile range includes Kwajalein and the islands up to Roi-Namur Island. Monitor SSB 2.716 and VHF Ch 71 for test notices.

Local Customs

The outer islands are very conservative. Covered shoulders and long skirts for women, and shirts and trousers/pants or long board shorts for men.  Display as little skin as possible.

When islanders visit your yacht, cover-up!

The drinking of alcohol is banned on all of the outer islands except for Kwajalein and Likiep and should never be given as a gift.

Pets

Animals must be declared to Customs on arrival but are permitted to stay on board (most pet friendly nation in the Pacific).
