Grand Mannan Harbour (Grand Manan Island) - Profile

Location

This is a fishing village on the northeast corner of the island of Grand Manan. It is a bustling fishing harbour, full of brightly painted boats and an interesting place to spend a few days.

Grand Manam lies at the entrance to the Bay of Fundy, about 20NM off the northern shore.

Position 44°45.65’N, 66°45.00’W.

Clearance

All the border authorities are based here.

Customs
(in the brick house at the root of the wharf, which is also the Post Office)
Tel:001 506-662-3232

Docking

Moor to Fishermans Wharf in Flagg Cove. This is primarily for the fishing boats but transient boaters are also welcomed and every effort is made to find safe, comfortable berthing whenever possible. Some electricity is available. No pump-out.

The most convenient place to lie is inside the recently-dredged westerly part where there is more room and better access to a float for landing. The inner corner is reserved for large herring seiners.

Along the western portion of the wharf, you can probably find a boat about your size to tie alongside. Boats here usually moor heading westward. If there are fishermen around, they will help with your lines.

There is a CAN$30/day (plus tax) fee for dockage here, even when you are rafted to the fishing boats, and the harbormaster will come by to collect it. He might also shepherd you into his pickup and give you a lift if you need one!

Last updated November 2014.
