Halifax - Profile

Location

Lying on the Atlantic coast of Nova Scotia, Halifax is one of Canada's major ports. It is marked by the oldest lighthouse in North America. It is also an important cultural centre for eastern provinces of Canada.

Position 44° 38"N, 63° 33"W.

Clearance

This is a Port of Entry.

On arrival, call the Canadian Border and Security Authority (CBSA).

No one except the skipper may leave the boat until authorized to do so by the CBSA.

Last updated November 2014.

Tel:+1-888-226-7277

Docking

The Waterfront Development Corporation manages the Halifax Waterfront with its 9 wharves stretching along over half a mile in the harbour area of downtown Halifax. See here for a useful map.

(photo Halifax Harbour © Halifax Waterfront Corp.)

There are five major yacht clubs in town. The Royal Nova Scotia Yacht Squadron is particularly welcoming to visiting yachts and has good facilities.

For those looking for a more sheltered location, consider going further into the Northwest Arm and either picking up a mooring or finding dockage at the Armdale YC, the Shearwater YC or anchoring out. This end of the Northwest Arm is much closer to town than the RNSYS and there are several large grocery stores within walking distance, as well as a bus service into town.

Last updated May 2015.
