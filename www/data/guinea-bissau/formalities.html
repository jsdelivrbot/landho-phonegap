Guinea-Bissau - Formalities

Clearance

Bubaque is the best place to clear in. Administration and procedures are complicated and take time and patience to negotiate fees, but the officials are nice if you are pleasant to them.

Bissau is said to be unpleasant and mooring there to clear in or out is not recommended.

Last updated January 2016.

Immigration

Passports must be valid for at least 6 months.

Carry evidence of I.D. (e.g. a passport) at all times.

All visitors, except nationals of Economic Community of West Africa States (Ecowas) countries, require a visa. These are normally valid for 45 days.

Extensions are easy to obtain at Serviço de Estrangeiros (Ave 14 de Novembro, Bissau), behind the main Immigration building across from the Mercado de Bandim. They are normally ready the same day if you go early.

Last updated January 2016.

Health

January 2016: The Ebola outbreak in the neighbouring countries has now been controlled and the situation reduced to surveillance and monitoring.

August 2014: An epidemic of Ebola virus disease (EVD) is ongoing in West Africa. It is the most severe outbreak of Ebola yet in terms of the number of human cases and fatalities since the discovery of the virus in 1976. The outbreak began in Guinea in December 2013 but was not detected until March 2014, after which it spread to Liberia, Sierra Leone, and Nigeria. Find out more at Ebola World Health Organization Fact Sheet.

A yellow fever vaccination certificate is required.

Malaria and other tropical diseases and are common.

Cholera is also a risk.

You should take particular care with uncooked foods and you should drink or use only boiled or bottled water and avoid ice in drinks.

Health facilities are very poor.
