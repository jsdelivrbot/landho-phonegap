Jordan - Formalities

Clearance

Yachts must call the Port Authority on Channel 16 or 12 on arrival in Jordanian waters. Instructions will be given where to proceed. Officials will come out to the yacht in a patrol boat.

Advisable to contact one of the marinas 2 or 3 days in advance of arrival as they can then help with the formalities.

Last updated April 2015.

Immigration

A passport valid for 6 months beyond the date of entry is required.

A 1 month visa on arrival will be issued to citizens of most countries if arriving at the port or airport.

A visa or special permission is required by citizen of the following countries:-

Albania, Bosnia &Herzegovina, Macedonia, Moldavia, Belize, Colombia, Cuba, Afghanistan, Cambodia, Iran, Laos, Mongolia, Nepal, Pakistan, Papua New Guinea, Philippines, Sri Lanka, Thailand, Uzbekistan, Vietnam, and most African states (excluding South Africa). See Visit Jordan official website for an up to date list.

The validity of the visa depends on nationality, and is usually between one and three months. Persons with HIV/AIDS are denied entry.

Shore passes will be issued on arrival.

Passports should be carried at all times when near the Israeli border.

Last updated April 2015.

Customs

Firearms must be declared.

Last updated April 2015.

Health

Cholera is a risk.

Fees

Sea tax JD6 per person.

Visas cost US$15 per person.

Restrictions

Spearfishing and the removal of shells or coral are prohibited.

Local Customs

This is a muslim country and Islamic law must be respected. Sensitivity regarding standards of dress should be observed.  Women should dress conservatively and men must not wear shorts or go shirtless in public. During the month of Ramadan, one should not eat, drink or smoke in public.

Pets
