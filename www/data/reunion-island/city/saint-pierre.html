Saint-Pierre - Profile

Location

Saint Pierre on the south-west coast welcomes yachts. It is a charming place and very French - almost like being somewhere on the French Riviera with street cafes and little bars and bistros.

Position 21° 20'52"S, 55° 28'39"E (harbour entrance)

Clearance

This is a Port of Entry.
See Reunion Island Clearance for full details of clearing in and out here.

Clearing in here was reported to be very relaxed in 2013 with no problems if you arrive at weekends. However in 2014 the Port Master of Le Port confirmed that yachts could only clear during working hours, so the same may apply here now.

Last updated October 2015.

Docking

It is essential to telephone in advance to ensure there is space. If not, yachts will be turned away.

The port entrance is quite difficult and the approach can look a little daunting, however there are clear range markers. If the swell is too high, the harbour will be closed. Call in advance on Ch.16 or 09 if possible.

The approach heading leading to the first pair of markers is 354 degrees true. The second pair of markers leads through the entrance on a bearing of 029 degrees true. When level with the port side breakwater (red light) favour that side and pass between a red beacon and two green buoys to reach the marina. The water on the starboard side of those buoys is shallow.

The marina in St. Pierre is right in the middle of the city, and one can walk off the boat to supermarkets, bakeries, etc in a few minutes. Also, for surfers, there is a great break just on the other side of the breakwater (a 30 second walk from the marina).

Last updated October 2015.

Call on VHF before entering channel. Visitors get 3 days free on arrival in St Pierre marina. Thereafter it's 15 euro/day or 90 euro/week regardless of size. Boats over 50ft may have to tie to the wall.
Water and electricity are included in the docking fee. Showers, free WiFi, 25 ton travel lift.
