Jastarnia - Profile

Location

A fishing port located on the Hel peninsular within the Bay of Gdansk, with records going back to the XIV century. It is now a popular holiday centre.

Position 54° 41'N, 18° 40'E (approach channel)

Docking

Jastarnia lies midway along the 33km long thin peninsular extending eastwards from the western shore of the Gulf of Gdansk. It is connected by a railway to the mainland. In places the peninsular is only 200m wide and not much more than a metre high.

The harbour is on the southern shore, but can be a little exposed to swell from that direction. In strong winds from this direction it is better to go to Gdynia or Gdansk. The approach to harbour is simple and well marked (buoys, leading light, beacons), but keep to the channel as each side is very shallow. Keep a sharp lookout for a fishing nets.

In northern part of harbour there are three pontoons (two with fingers) with water and electricity. 10/15 guest moorings with a maximum draft of 3.5m.

There is free mooring at the pier on the eastern side of the harbour, but it has no water or electricity. Moor alongside or med-style using an anchor.

Kuźnica
This old fishing harbour lies about 5nm further north of Jastarnia along the Hel Peninsula. It is a useful alternative destination when Jastarnia is crowded.

The approach channel is due to have channel markers this spring (2014) but is dredged and yachts with a draft of up to 2m can enter. The church tower makes a conspicuous landmark.

In this recently re-built harbour, there are toilets, electricity and water and the town also has good fish bars and some provisions.
