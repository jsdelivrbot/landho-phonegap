Gdynia - Profile

Location

Gdynia is one of the largest Polish yacht harbours as well as being a large and busy commercial port with ferries, container ships and a cruise ship terminal. The yacht harbour is in the southern part of the harbour with direct access to the Bay of Gdansk.

Position 54° 31'N, 18° 33'25"E (marina).

Clearance

If clearance is required, moor at the Harbour Master's quay on arrival. He will arrange for Customs and Immigration clearance. Return to this quay for clearance on departure. The yacht may be searched.

Docking

This is a large container and cruise ship port on the western side of the Gulf of Gdansk. It is also home to the Polish Navy.

The yacht basin is at the southern breakwater entrance. South of this lies an attractive beach area with many pavement cafes. It is an active sailing centre and many regattas are held here.

It is advisable to call the marina in advance to request a berth.

Yachts must monitor VHF channel 12, but do not have to report their presence.
