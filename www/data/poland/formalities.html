Clearance

The Q flag is not used, and it is your responsibility to report your arrival.

Foreign boats must check in with Customs and Immigration at all major ports. The authorities should be contacted at Port Control on Channel 16 or working channel (usually 10 or 12) before entering or leaving a port. Details of ship's name, nationality, number of crew and previous or next port of call. English is understood, but it may be necessary to speak slowly.

Immigration and Customs procedures must be completed when arriving or departing from Poland. Official Border Control (check-in) is required on arrival at the first Polish port and similarly on departure from the last Polish port.

For EU members arriving from EU countries, this is only passport control. In practice it is, however, necessary to report in and out of every port in Poland. With the exception of the first and last port, a verbal report over the VHF radio giving boat name, names and nationality, number of persons on board and previous or next port of call appears to be all that is required.

Swinemunde, Ziegenort, Stettin and all larger ports on the Polish coast have Immigration offices or stations.

Only Gdynia and Swinoujcie can be entered under all weather conditions.

Peenestrom Channel Yachts coming from Germany through the Stettiner Haff (Zalew Szczecinski in Polish) must show their papers to the Polish patrol boat stationed at the border (marked by buoys) before continuing to Nowe Warpno, Trzebiez or Swinoujscie for clearance.

You must not continue south on the Odra River without clearing in.

Health

The medical services are good and Private medical facilities are comparatively inexpensive and of a good standard. However, medicines are scarce and expensive.

Tick-borne encephalitis is common to Poland, especially if you are planning to visit forested areas in the summer months.

Restrictions

There are restricted areas near Ustka and along the Hel peninsula, where passage is occasionally prohibited. Hel fishing harbour is now open to foreign yachts.

There is a zero tolerance for drink driving in Poland. If you drive and have been drinking (even 1 unit of alcohol) you can be charged.

Jay walking is an offence and if caught by the police you will be fined.

Pets

The Pet Travel Scheme (PETS) applies in Poland and animals require a passport, to have been microchipped and have a current Health Certificate and Rabies Vaccination Certificate issued by recognised Veterinary Service and have been blood tested prior to travel.
