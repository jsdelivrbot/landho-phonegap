Munda (New Georgia Island) - Profile

Location

Munda was the Japanese base on New Georgia Island during World War II, eventually overrun by American forces on 5 August 1943.

It is located just south of Noro, on New Georgia's NW (southern) coast.

Munda is a small yet pleasant town with adequate services, a protected anchorage and daily flights to Honiara.

Position 08° 33'20"N, 157° 26'72"E

Clearance

Yachts can clear in at nearby Noro.

Docking

Munda is situated in the picturesque Roviana Lagoon. There are no detailed charts for Roviana Lagoon, but Dirk Selling's guide has good mud maps for getting around. At the time of writing most of the reef markers were in place, but it is recommended to keep a good watch in good visibility.

Anchoring

A good anchorage can be found in front of Agnes Gateway Hotel in 8-10 meters, sandy mud, good holding. The hotel management welcomes yachts, has a restaurant and bar, and 24hr security.
Photo courtesy of SY Yawana.

Ashore

Munda has several grocery stores, a BSP bank with ATM, Telecom office, and a police station. There is a fruit and vegetable market most days, the busiest day being Friday.

Fuel is available at several waterfront vendors.

There is exceptional diving in the area with pelagic wall dives and World War 2 wrecks. Dive Munda operates out of Agnes Gateway Hotel. They have a special Yachtie rate and do pick-ups from yachts. They also offer broadband Wi-Fi to divers.
