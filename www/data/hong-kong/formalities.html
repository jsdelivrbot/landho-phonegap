Hong Kong - Formalities

Clearance

The Hong Kong Port Operations Service operates on VHF Channel 12, 14 and 68, call sign MARDEP 24 hours a day. Yachts may be boarded and searched in Hong Kong territorial waters, as illegal immigration and smuggling are major problems.

On arrival, non-commercial yachts may go to an anchorage or a yacht club (usually the Royal Hong Kong YC).

Within 24 hours of arrival report to Immigration (2nd Floor, Government Pier, Central - open 24hrs) and then the Marine Department, (Pleasure Vessel, 2nd floor, in the same building - normal office hrs). A General Declaration form must be completed and port and light fees paid.

To cruise in Hong Kong waters, a permit to navigate must be applied for.

Any of the marinas or the Royal Hong Kong Yacht Club will advise on the correct procedure.

It is necessary also to report to the Port Formalities Office again before departure with the necessary papers. A Port Clearance Permit must be obtained before departure, which is valid for 72 hours.

For full details of entry procedures to Hong Kong, link for the Hong Kong Marine Department (Mardep).

Immigration

Passports must be valid for 6 months beyond the proposed stay in Hong Kong.

Citizens of most countries are allowed a visa-free stay varying from 7 days to 90 days. British citizens are allowed 180 days. Most Nationalities being permitted 90 days. See Official Hong Kong site for the complete list.

Those allowed visa-free stays may be required to show proof of adequate funds for their stay.

Immigration regulations are different from those for travel to mainland China, where everyone must arrive with a visa obtained in advance. The situation in Hong Kong is far more relaxed.

Some form of photo identification such as a passport should always be carried while in Hong Kong.

Last updated February 2016.

Customs

Firearms must be declared and handed into custody until departure, along with personal protection devices like mace, pepper or bear sprays.

Yachts may remain up to six months, after which a Pleasure Vessel Licence must be obtained, and the yacht must have third party insurance.

Last updated February 2016.

Health

There may be a risk of malaria in some rural areas.

Fees

There is a clearance fee of HK$ 58 and a similar fee when clearing out. There is no daily harbour fee.

Last updated February 2016.

Restrictions

Prohibited areas are Kai Tak Airport Area No. 1 and 100 metres from both Green Island and Stonecutters Island.

It is not permitted to sail through Hong Kong harbour (i.e. between Hong Kong Island and Kowloon).

A minimum of two qualified crew members must be on board.

Local Customs

There are on the spot fines for littering and spitting. There is zero tolerance for ticketless travel on the Mass Transit Railway (MTR).

Don’t take photographs of military installations.

Pets

The Pet Travel Scheme (PETS) is in operation at Hong Kong and all animals require a passport, to be microchipped and have current Health Certificate and Rabies Vaccination Certified. Further information can be obtained in advance from the Senior Veterinary Officer, Agriculture and Fisheries Department, 393 Canton Road Government Offices, 12F Kowloon, Hong Kong
