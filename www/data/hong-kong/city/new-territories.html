New Territories - Profile

Location

This area covers an area of mainland China south of Shenzhen and north of Hong Kong island and the Kowloon Penisula. It includes the surrounding islands of Lantau, Lamma, Cheung Chau and Peng Chau and many other smaller ones. Large parts of this area are still rural in nature and there are several National Parks.

Clearance

Clearance must be made within 24 hours of arrival at one of the designated District Marine offices. In the New Territories there are offices at Tuen Mun and Sam Mun Tsai, Tai Po. See www.mardep.gov.hk/en for actual address, telephone numbers and opening hours.

For full details of entry procedures to Hong Kong, link for the Hong Kong Marine Department (Mardep).
http://www.mardep.gov.hk/en/pub_services/ocean/notes_ymaster.html

A General Declaration Form M.O.618A, together with the Certificate of Registry of the vessel, a crew list (ID 207A) stamped by the Immigration Department and the Port Clearance issued by the authority of the last port of call should be produced at the time of reporting arrival. A prescribed fee of HK$58 for the arrival clearance would be charged.

Clearance at Hebe Haven Yacht Club

Under the “Special Arrangements” clause of the Immigration Department you may come directly to Hebe Haven Yacht Club, instead of anchoring at the Immigration Anchorage for Inward Immigration Clearance. On arrival, you should berth alongside the Big Yellow Pin on the South Pontoon at about 22' 21.52” N 114' 15.42”, give or take a metre or two. It is necessary to advice the YC of your approximate ETA in advance.

Last updated February 2014.

Docking

Photo of Hebe Haven, Hong Kong :© Karin Stubbs

There are several marinas in the New Territories. At Hebe Cove, Sai Kung, in the eastern part of the Territories, there is the Hong Kong marina as well as the Hebe Haven Yacht Club.

The yacht club does not usually have mooring available but the anchorage there is sheltered. It also has good repair and boatyard facilities.

It is not permitted to anchor in Hebe Haven Pak Sha Wan Harbour.

Last updated February 2014.
